## The One-Paragraph Truth

**The fix is not more building. It's wiring what exists.**

## What's Genius About What's Built

1. **The homepage hero** — "What will you create?" with chat box is exactly right. Chat IS the product. No hero image, no corporate speak, just an invitation to create.

2. **The pricing model** — Free (Library) / Credits ($5-49) / Forge ($29/mo) is elegant. Free is genuinely useful. Credits are low-friction. Forge is for power users. The naming (Library, Credits, Forge) fits the mythology without being weird.

3. **The content depth** — 200K words is a genuine moat. This isn't generated slop — it's a coherent creative philosophy with 17 collections, teaching methodology, and practical applications.

4. **Ten Gates progression** — Gamification that isn't cheap. Each Gate represents real creative mastery. The ranks (Apprentice → Luminor) give users something to work toward.

5. **Origin Classes** — 8 creator archetypes that serve as persona matching AND community identity. "I'm a Gate-Touched" is something people would put in their bio.

6. **The design system** — Liquid glass with aurora gradients is distinctive and premium. Not another Tailwind template site.

## What's Not Genius (Yet)

1. **Navigation tells me this is a platform, not a product** — 8 nav items says "we built a lot of things." 4-5 says "we know exactly what we do."

2. **No social proof anywhere** — The hero says "190K+ words" which means nothing to a creator. Show creations, not word counts.

3. **"Creative superintelligence" in the subtitle** — Claims without evidence. Show what it does, don't label it.

4. **Too many product names** — ACOS, SIS, Arcanea OS, Arcanea Code, Arcanea Vault. Users see confusion. Simplify to just "Arcanea" externally.

5. **Stubs destroy trust** — If I click "World Builder" and get an empty page, I'm gone. Redirect or noindex.

6. **No end-to-end flow works** — I can chat, but I can't pay. I can see pricing, but I can't buy. I can sign up (theoretically), but auth isn't configured.
