# The Arcanean Prompt Language — A Practical Guide

> *"The gap between AI slop and something that haunts you is one sentence of truth."*

## Who This Is For

Anyone who creates with AI and wants better results. Authors, world-builders, musicians, visual artists, game designers, prompt engineers, educators. No mythology required. No syntax to memorize.

## The Problem

90% of AI output sounds the same because 90% of prompts say the same thing. "Write a fantasy story." "Create a beautiful landscape." "Compose an epic track." The AI has no reason to produce anything specific, so it produces everything generic.

APL fixes this with three words.

## SPARK. SHAPE. SHARPEN.

### SPARK — The one detail that makes it yours

Not a description. A **truth**. The detail so specific it could only come from you.

**Before:** "Write a story about a lonely king"
**After:** "Write a story about a king who eats dinner alone and sets a place for his dead wife every night — including pouring her wine"

The Spark is what makes someone stop scrolling. Every AI can generate a king. Only YOUR Spark makes it yours.

**How to find it:** Close your eyes. See the thing you want to create. Find the detail that surprises YOU. That's it.

### SHAPE — The sensory world

What does this creation feel, smell, sound, taste like? Not what it looks like first — what it FEELS like.

Five sensory palettes (use one, blend two, or ignore them):

| Palette | Feels Like | Sounds Like | Looks Like |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Forge** | Heat on skin, rough metal, heartbeat | Crackle, percussion, roar | Ember, gold, molten glass, dawn |
| **Tide** | Cool stone, mist on face, deep breath | Echo, sustained notes, rainfall | Silver, deep blue, mirror surface |
| **Root** | Packed earth, bark, weight in the chest | Low hum, grinding, drum | Moss green, amber, bone, fossil |
| **Drift** | Wind through hair, electric tingle | Whistle, chime, distant thunder | Cloud white, pale blue, ozone |
| **Void** | Weightlessness, silence, vertigo | Sub-bass, overtone, nothing | Starfield, ink black, iridescent |

### SHARPEN — What it must NOT be

The most powerful prompt technique almost nobody uses: **tell the AI what to avoid.**

**The Seven Defaults to Cut:**

| Default | What It Sounds Like | The Sharpen |
|---------|-------------------|------------|
| **The Opener** | "In a world where..." | Start in the middle. Start with a sound. |
| **The Avalanche** | "Hauntingly beautiful ethereal cascading majesty" | One strong word beats five weak ones. |
| **The Slop** | "I'd be happy to help!" | Respond AS the thing, not ABOUT the thing. |
| **The Explanation** | Telling the reader what the metaphor means | Trust them. They're smart. |
| **The Perfection** | Every hero is noble, every villain is evil | Scars are more interesting than polish. |
| **The Resolution** | Everything wraps up neatly | Sometimes the best ending is a door left open. |
| **The Safety** | Generic, forgettable output | If it doesn't make you feel something, it's not done. |

## The Quality Ladder

**Level 1 — SPARK Only** (10 seconds, beats 80% of AI output)
Add one specific detail.

**Level 2 — SPARK + SHARPEN** (30 seconds, beats 95%)
Add one detail and one thing to avoid.

**Level 3 — SPARK + SHAPE + SHARPEN** (60 seconds, beats 99%)
The full pattern. This is where people ask "how did you make this?"

## Complete Examples

### Character

```
SPARK:    She counts things when she's nervous — ceiling tiles, buttons, heartbeats
SHAPE:    ROOT — earthy, grounded, heavy. Smells like coffee and old paper.
SHARPEN:  NOT a manic pixie. NOT tragically broken. NOT saved by love.

A data scientist who quit her job to open a bakery.
Her sourdough starter is named after her thesis advisor.
```

### Image

```
SPARK:    A library where the books are warm to the touch — they're alive, dreaming
SHAPE:    VOID — near-black space, single warm light from within the shelves.
SHARPEN:  NOT a grand fantasy library. NOT colorful spines. NOT orderly.

Cinematic wide shot. Film grain. 21:9.
A figure stands small in the center aisle, touching a spine that pulses faintly gold.
```

### Music (Suno/Udio)

```
SPARK:    The cello is slightly out of tune — on purpose. It sounds like someone trying not to cry.
SHAPE:    TIDE — sustained, reverberant, oceanic. Piano underneath like distant rain on glass.
SHARPEN:  NOT epic. NOT building to a climax. It stays quiet the entire time.

Ambient neoclassical, 72 BPM, solo cello with sparse piano, large reverb, no percussion.
```

## For World-Builders

APL's palettes become your **world's DNA**:

- FORGE world? Every location feels hot, every conflict is about transformation.
- TIDE world? Memory is everywhere, the past is always present.
- ROOT world? Things grow slowly, patience is power.
- DRIFT world? Change is constant, freedom is the highest value.
- VOID world? Potential is everywhere, silence speaks.

**Blend two palettes** for complexity. A FORGE+TIDE world is about transformation through memory.

```
WORLD SPARK:    [the one thing true here that's false everywhere else]
WORLD SHAPE:    [primary palette] + [secondary palette]
WORLD SHARPEN:  [the three biggest cliches your world refuses]

Use this as a PREFIX for every prompt. Every character, location, and song inherits the world's DNA.
```

## For Musicians

APL's biggest gift to music is SHARPEN:

```
- NOT a build-drop structure
- NOT every instrument playing at once
- NOT a clean, polished production (leave the room noise)
- NOT resolving to the tonic (end on tension)
```

The SPARK for music is the **anchor moment** — the 5 seconds someone would hum back to you.
