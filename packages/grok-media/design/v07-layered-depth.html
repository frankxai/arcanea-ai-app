Design Notes - v07 Layered Depth

Design Philosophy

Inspired by Apple Vision Pro's spatial computing paradigm, this design creates a sense of physical depth through CSS perspective transforms, multi-layered glass panels, and progressive shadow intensities. Every element exists at a specific Z-depth in 3D space, creating a hierarchy that feels tangible and immersive. The design rewards interaction — hovering reveals deeper layers, and selected items literally rise toward the user.

Typography

System font stack led by SF Pro Display for macOS harmony, falling back to Segoe UI and system-ui. The spatial design demands clean, readable typography that doesn't compete with depth effects.

Animation Approach

Three-tier easing system: var(--transition-fast) at 200ms for micro-interactions, var(--transition-smooth) at 300ms for card transforms, and var(--transition-spatial) at 400ms for panel-level movements. All use cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1) — a spring-like easing that feels physical. CSS perspective() + translateZ() creates true 3D movement. Cards rotate subtly on hover (rotateX/rotateY 1-2deg) for parallax effect.

Recommended Icon Library

Lucide Icons (feather-style) or Phosphor Icons (thin weight). The clean line style complements the spatial depth without visual clutter.

What Makes It Unique

True CSS 3D spatial hierarchy. Every element lives at a different Z-depth: header at 30px, filters at 15px, stats at 10px, media items at 0px (rising to 12px on hover). The perspective grid in the background creates a visionOS-style infinite plane. Stacked stat cards with progressive Y-offsets suggest physical layering. Inner glow effects on focused elements (inset box-shadow) create the sense of containment and depth that flat designs cannot achieve.
