Design Notes - v08 Retro Synthwave

Design Philosophy

A love letter to 80s retrowave aesthetic filtered through modern UI sensibility. The design channels the neon-soaked nights of a cyberpunk future imagined in 1985 — sunset gradients, chrome typography, perspective grid floors, and pulsing neon accents. Yet underneath the nostalgia, the UX remains crisp and modern. The VHS tracking line animation and radar sweep scanner connect the past and future in a way that feels both playful and premium.

Typography

Orbitron for all headers and labels — wide, geometric, unmistakably retro-futuristic. Rajdhani for body text — a tech-flavored sans with great readability. Share Tech Mono for data displays, counters, and file names — the LCD readout feel. All three combine to create a coherent "future as seen from the past" typographic identity.

Animation Approach

Signature animations: VHS tracking line (vhs-tracking), radar sweep (radar-spin with conic-gradient), neon pulse (neon-pulse), star twinkle (twinkle). Intentionally retro feel — simple linear and ease-in-out easings rather than spring physics. The neon pulse creates a breathing quality to active elements, suggesting they are alive with electric energy.

Recommended Icon Library

Lucide Icons or Heroicons (outline). The clean strokes work well against the neon glow effects. Icons should remain thin and geometric to match the Orbitron display font.

What Makes It Unique

No other media manager looks like an 80s arcade cabinet. The sunset gradient header with chrome text, perspective grid floor, VHS tracking line animation, radar sweep scanner, LCD-style stat readouts, and "INSERT MEDIA" empty state all create an experience that is unmistakable and unforgettable. The alternating magenta/blue neon glow on media items adds visual rhythm. The beveled arcade-button styling on the primary CTA actually feels like you could press it.
