## TEAM OVERVIEW

The founding team of [THE FLAGSHIP TEAM] was not assembled. It was survived into.

Before there was a formal Starlight Corps structure, before sectors had names, before the Oath of Starlight had been written — there were seven individuals in the same place at the wrong moment, facing something none of them could survive alone. They did not agree. They did not trust each other. They fought the thing anyway.

What came after is legend.

## First Meeting

The team encounters Kaelindra at a Corps holding station in the Solar Sector — a transit hub that is not yet on any official map because it was constructed in a week and will likely be abandoned in another. She is briefing three Aspirants when they arrive, but she is not looking at them. She is looking at the door. She tells the Aspirants the briefing is over before they are finished, says their names — correctly, without introduction — and turns to the newcomers. She already knows why they are here. She has prepared three tactical options. She lays them out without greeting, without preamble, as if the conversation started before they walked in. The only moment she pauses is when she sees Ryn — the Gate-Touched youth — and for exactly two seconds, the gold lattice around her eyes goes bright and then goes dark, like something briefly overwhelmed her assessment. She recovers so quickly that only someone watching very carefully would notice. Nobody who is paying attention to her briefing notices.

## Poster Description

Kaelindra stands at the center of a star-chart projection, one hand raised with two fingers extended — not pointing at anything, pointing at everything. Her obsidian coat is immaculate. The gold lattice of Tactical Sight traces fine lines from the edges of her eyes across the star-chart, as if the map is an extension of her vision rather than a tool she is using. Behind her the Solar Sector is rendered in cold light — every route, every threat, every exit visible. She is not looking at the chart. She is looking at the viewer. The 7-pointed captain's star on her shoulder is the only ornamentation. The scratched House Lumina ring catches the light of the projection. Her expression is not coldness — it is the absolute stillness of someone who has already decided.

## First Meeting

They are found by accident — or they allow themselves to be found, which amounts to the same thing. A Void Ascendant sweep team is working through a district of the Solar Sector that has an Underground haven in its basement. The team stumbles into the tail end of a cascade: a street of cracked stone, a smell like burning copper, and Ryn standing in the middle of it, eyes cycling white-gold-amber, three Ascendant operatives unconscious in a rough circle around them. The frequency scars are blazing. Ember is spinning like a panicked planet. Ryn sees the team and immediately assumes they are another containment unit. The first words out of their mouth are: "You're too late for cleanup and too early for an apology." They have the map pressed to their chest like armor. They do not stop watching the exits for the entire duration of the conversation that follows.

## Poster Description

Ryn stands at the edge of a cascade mid-release — the instant between control and becoming the event. The frequency scars on their arm and collarbone are blazing amber-to-white, and the light has bled upward to fill their eyes. The air around them is shimmering with heat-distortion and visible resonance waves that split into rings at the edges of the frame. Ember orbits their left shoulder like a warning. Their jacket collar is up. They are small against the composition — deliberately so — but the cascade fills the space their body doesn't, and the eye goes to them first, last, and continuously. Their expression is not fury. It is something harder: a person who has decided to stop being afraid of themselves for exactly as long as necessary.

## First Meeting

The team finds Mireth, and then they realize they were looking for her before they knew they were looking for her. She is in a location that appears on no map — a small workshop at the edge of the Solar Sector that has either always been there or has never quite been there — and she is working on something that she puts down without urgency when they enter. ## Poster Description

Mireth stands at the edge of the team composition — not outside it, never fully inside it — in the simple linen that has never needed to be anything else. The space around her is wrong: the wall behind her opens onto a room that does not exist in the building they are standing in, and the shadow at her feet falls in a direction that has no light source. She holds nothing except the Kyuro Shard, which absorbs the light it touches into a small perfect silence. Her expression is the question before the question — the patient attention of someone who has already seen what is coming and has decided that what comes is worth caring for completely. She is not the most dramatic figure in the composition. She is the reason the composition has weight. Every other character in the lineup is pointing, fighting, glowing, moving. She is still. She is watching. She is the ground the image stands on. The age in her face is not diminishment. It is everything she refused to forget.
