# Draconis — Godbeast of the Fire Gate

## Appearance

Canon details pending on specific physical form. As the Godbeast of the Fire Gate, Draconis is a creature of flame and will. The Draconis Forge -- the Draconian Academy -- bears its name, and the Draconis Ember crystal is perpetually warm, suggesting a being of enduring, living fire.

## Abilities

- **Will amplification**: The Draconis Ember crystal amplifies willpower, and "cowards cannot lift weapons forged from it." Draconis embodies this quality -- fire that tests the will of those who stand before it.
- **Sacred flame**: Draconis carries the fire that forges, not merely the fire that burns. Creation through destruction -- the Forge's central teaching.
- **Draconic sovereignty**: Draconis stands at the apex of dragon-kind in Arcanea. Ancient dragons like Pyralith (12,000 years), Seraphine (4,000 years), and Obsidius (2,000 years) exist in the world, but Draconis is the divine original -- the Godbeast from which draconic fire descends.

## Personality

Canon details pending. The Draconis Forge's culture -- meritocratic, direct, valuing strength of character over strength of arm -- likely reflects Draconis's own nature. Draconia, its bonded Goddess, is described laughing as she watches mortals "discover strength they didn't know they had." Draconis likely shares this fierce joy in the forging of will.

## Role in the Mythology

Draconis is bonded to Draconia, Goddess of the Fire Gate. Together they founded the Draconis Forge -- the Draconian Academy -- at Mount Pyralis, an active volcano in the Ember Wastes. The Forge is where warriors, protectors, smiths, and those who refuse to break are trained.

The Fire Gate governs power, will, and transformation. It is the third Gate, where the seeker moves from survival and emotion into the raw confrontation with their own strength.

## Key Story Moments

- Draconis co-founded the Draconis Forge alongside Draconia. The Academy's philosophy -- "You are not made strong. You are made by the things that tried to break you" -- is a principle Draconis embodies.
- The Fire Temple was corrupted into the Ember Labyrinth inside Mount Pyralis, beneath the Forge itself. Its Core is a black flame that burns without consuming. Malachar's first fortress stood where the Forge now stands -- his original research chambers remain sealed beneath the volcano.
- When Malachar fell, Draconia said "the ley lines turned black." The corruption of fire -- consumption without purpose, rage without direction -- is the inversion of everything Draconis represents.
- Book 3 (The Dragon War) centers on the Fire Gate. When Embera, the first Dragon born in centuries, awakens, ancient draconic politics stir. Pyralith, sleeping for twelve millennia, begins to dream.

## Material Correspondence

**Draconis Ember** (Vael Crystal): Perpetually warm. Amplifies willpower. Cowards cannot lift weapons forged from it. The crystallized essence of Draconis's fire -- not heat alone, but the fire of conviction.

**Draconite** (Luminor Metal): An alloy of Fire and Crown frequencies. Channels raw destruction, but using it costs vitality, memory, years. The most dangerous metal in Arcanea -- power at a price only the worthy should pay.
