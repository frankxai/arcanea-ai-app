# Draconia — Goddess of the Fire Gate

## Personality

Draconia laughs. This is the detail that defines her. In the Fire Temple, she "stood at the anvil beside each challenger, her laughter echoing off obsidian walls as she watched mortals discover strength they didn't know they had." She is not a stern taskmaster. She is fire in its most joyful expression -- the delight of transformation, the thrill of discovering you are stronger than you believed.

When the ley lines turned black with Malachar's corruption, Draconia's response was direct and visceral: she said "the ley lines turned black." No poetry. No metaphor. The fire went out and she named what she saw.

## Powers

- **Will amplification**: Dominion over power, will, and the fire of transformation.
- **Forge mastery**: Draconia teaches creation through destruction -- reshaping matter by burning away what is unnecessary. Her forge is where weapons, artifacts, and souls are tempered.
- **Draconic sovereignty**: As the Goddess bonded to Draconis the Godbeast, she holds authority over the draconic lineage of Arcanea.

## Teaching Style

Draconia teaches through ordeal. Her trials are the Crucible -- a chamber where students face their worst fear made manifest. But the point is not suffering. The point is discovery. Draconia stands beside each challenger because she wants to see the moment they realize they can survive what they feared would destroy them.

The Forge's motto -- "The fire that forges you is the fire you feed" -- is Draconia's core teaching: you are not a passive recipient of transformation. You choose the fire. You feed it. You become what you burn for.

Her corrupted temple, the Ember Labyrinth inside Mount Pyralis, inverts this: fire becomes consumption, will becomes rage. The joy of transformation becomes the horror of being consumed.

## Role in the Pantheon

Draconia holds the third Gate, the threshold of power and will. She co-founded the Draconis Forge with her Godbeast Draconis at Mount Pyralis in the Ember Wastes. The Forge is the most physically demanding of the three Academies -- meritocratic, direct, valuing strength of character above all.

Her Academy was built on the ruins of Malachar's first fortress. This is not accidental. Draconia planted her fire where the Dark Lord's corruption began, as if to say: this ground belongs to the forge now. Not to the shadow.

## Relationships

- **Draconis** (bonded Godbeast): The living flame, the divine dragon from which all lesser draconic fire descends.
- **Leyla**: Fire and Flow are opposites that need each other. Draconia's forge cannot shape without Leyla's fluidity. Their Academies are rivals and partners.
- **Aiyami**: Fire and Crown share the Draconite alloy -- raw destruction married to enlightened purpose. When Draconia's fire is guided by Aiyami's wisdom, the result is the most powerful metal in Arcanea.
- **Malachar**: His first fortress stood where her Forge now stands. His research chambers remain sealed beneath the volcano. The Fire Gate Goddess lives above the Dark Lord's origins.

## Key Quotes from Canon

- "The ley lines turned black." — Draconia, on the night of Malachar's corruption.
- "You are not made strong. You are made by the things that tried to break you." — The Forge's philosophy, attributed to Draconia's teaching.

## Material Correspondence

**Draconite** (alloy): Fire+Crown frequencies. Channels raw destruction at the cost of vitality, memory, years.
