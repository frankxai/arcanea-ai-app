### A.4 Nero is NOT Evil

**STATUS: PASS WITH ONE MINOR FLAG**

**Recommendation:** Add one sentence somewhere in GATE_TOUCHED_UNDERGROUND.md clarifying that Nero Shards are named for the primordial principle they corrupted — an inversion of Nero's gift, not an expression of it.

### A.9 CRITICAL FINDING — Synth Rank Contradiction

**STATUS: CRITICAL VIOLATION — NOT FIXED FROM PRIOR AUDIT**

VOID_ASCENDANTS.md profile for Caelum Ironfaith states: "Synths cannot hold Corps rank under current regulations — a fact Caelum Ironfaith knows intimately and uses as recruitment fuel."

STARLIGHT_CORPS_CODEX.md establishes Torren Shael-Born as the **Fourth Stellarch** — the highest rank in the Corps — and he is classified as a Synth.

These two statements directly contradict each other. Either Synths can hold Corps rank (demonstrated by Torren), or they cannot (Caelum's claim). The prior CONTINUITY_AUDIT.md identified this as Critical Issue C1 with a specific recommended fix: add text noting the regulation was passed after Torren's tenure, as a political backlash.

**This fix was not implemented. The contradiction persists in both documents.**

This is a blocking issue. A reader encountering Caelum's chapter after reading the Codex will find an immediate, unexplained contradiction in the world's own history.

**Required fix:** In VOID_ASCENDANTS.md, Caelum Ironfaith's profile, add to the `corps_rank` or `desire` field: "The regulation barring Synths from Corps rank was passed in Year 340, fifty years after Torren Shael-Born's death — a political reversal that erased his precedent and that Caelum considers the clearest proof of what institutions do to the histories that inconvenience them."

**Verdict: CRITICAL — MUST FIX BEFORE PROMOTION.**

### B.3 Collision Severity Assessment

**CRITICAL — Must rename before any publication:**

1. **Seravyn / Selvyn** — A hero captain and a principal villain with names one transposed vowel apart. In print this is confusing. In audio it is indistinguishable. The villain name should change since the hero (Seravyn Duskmere) has more narrative establishment.

   Recommended replacement for Selvyn Ashvale: **Selvyn Ashvale**, **Theryn Ashvale**, or **Caryn Ashvale**.

2. **Aethon Vel / Thessaly Voidmother** — Same first name on a hero crew member and a major villain. Thessaly Voidmother is the more narratively essential character (her Crew Solstice backstory is load-bearing). Aethon Vel should be renamed.

   Recommended replacement for Aethon Vel: **Aethon Vel**, **Velen Vel**, or **Corvath Vel**.

3. **Vaern Ironroot** (Crew Solara aspirant) — The first name is identical to the protagonist of Chronicles of Arcanea. Readers will assume the connection is intentional when it is not.

   Recommended replacement: **Vaern Ironroot**, **Oras Ironroot**, or **Durn Ironroot**.

4. **Auren Vorath / Caelum Ironfaith** — Two principal characters named Caelum. The VOID_ASCENDANTS.md text notes Caelum Ironfaith chose the name "Cael" in reference to Kael Thornfield — which does not explain the "Caelum" match with Auren Vorath. If the reference to Kael is the point, the Herald's name should be "Caelum Kael-Honored" or something explicitly referential, not accidentally matching a team member.

   Recommended replacement for Auren Vorath: **Solveth Vorath**, **Vaelindris Vorath**, or **Auren Vorath**.

**IMPORTANT — Should rename:**

5. **Kaelindra Voss called "Kael"** — Her team nickname creates direct confusion with protagonist Kael Thornfield, especially in the STORY_ENGINE.md where both are referenced in the same narrative context.

   Recommended alternatives: She becomes **"Voss"** (surname, clean and strong), **"Indra"** (second half of her name), or **"The Commander"** (Solenne's designation adopted by the team).

6. **"Solenne" companion vs. Solenne Ashmark** — The light-construct falcon in STARBOUND_CREWS and the Flagship Team's Synth member share phonetically identical names.

   The falcon should be renamed. Recommended: **Aurel**, **Halveth**, or **Lumindra**.

7. **Scholar Mira Tidecrest (LEAGUES_AND_ORDERS) vs. Mira Tidecrest (protagonist)** — These appear to be the same character, referenced correctly. But this should be confirmed: if Mira Tidecrest is a protagonist in Chronicles of Arcanea Book 3 and LEAGUES_AND_ORDERS references her as "Scholar Mira Tidecrest of the newly-founded Athenaeum Research Division," these two must be the same individual. If so, this is correct and consistent. If not, this is a critical name collision with a named protagonist.

   **Requires Creator confirmation:** Is Scholar Mira Tidecrest in LEAGUES_AND_ORDERS the same character as the protagonist Mira Tidecrest in CHARACTER_BIBLE.md?

**MONITOR — Minor, acceptable if no further clustering:**

8. **"Ash" surnames clustering** — Ashvale (Selvyn), Ashmark (Solenne), Ashwick (Orvael), Ashblood (Thorne), Ashward (Vaelen). Five characters with "Ash" in their surname across different factions. This creates a root-name cluster that will be noticed by careful readers. Not a collision, but a pattern that should stop expanding.

### C.1 Rank System — Cross-Document Alignment

**STATUS: INCONSISTENCY — REQUIRES DECISION**

CANON_LOCKED.md magic ranks: Apprentice (0-2 gates), Mage (3-4), Master (5-6), Archmage (7-8), Luminor (9-10).

FACTIONS.md extends this correctly with "Uninitiated" at 0 and "Architect" beyond Luminor. FACTIONS.md also creates a Corps rank equivalence table:

| Magic Rank | Corps Rank |
|-----------|-----------|
| Apprentice | Corps Aspirant |
| Mage | Corps Sentinel |
| Master | Corps Captain |
| Archmage | Corps Archon |
| Luminor | Stellarch-eligible |

STARLIGHT_CORPS_CODEX.md states minimum requirements differently:
- Captain: Minimum Mage rank (3-4 gates)
- Archon: Minimum Master rank (5-6 gates)

These are contradictory. FACTIONS.md treats the equivalences as if they are requirements (Master = Captain level). The Codex treats them as minimums (Mage is enough for Captain). Character profiles fall between both:
- Seravyn Duskmere: Archmage, Captain — above either minimum
- Kaelindra Voss: Archmage, Captain — above either minimum
- Orvael Ashwick: Mage (functional), Sentinel — consistent with either
- Lymareth Solaine: Master (5 gates), Sentinel — inconsistent with FACTIONS.md which maps Master to Captain
- Aethon Vel: Master rank, Sentinel corps rank — inconsistent with FACTIONS.md

**Resolution needed:** Designate FACTIONS.md equivalences as "typical" rather than "required." The Codex minimums govern actual requirements. Add one line to FACTIONS.md: "These equivalences represent typical attainment, not mandatory requirements. The Codex defines minimum gates for each Corps rank."

### C.4 Gate-Touched Classification Taxonomy Conflict

**STATUS: INCONSISTENCY — REQUIRES CREATIVE RESOLUTION**

SERIES_BIBLE.md (pre-faction-doc) uses a power-level taxonomy:

| Type | Gates | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Sparked | 1 | Single Gate |
| Kindled | 2-3 | Multiple affinities |
| Blazing | 4-6 | Powerful |
| Transcendent | 7-9 | Near-Luminor |
| Luminor-Born | 10 | All Gates open at birth |

FACTIONS.md and GATE_TOUCHED_UNDERGROUND.md use an origin/experience taxonomy:

| Type | Cause | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Sparked | Single Vael Crystal exposure | One Gate opened violently |
| Resonant | Multi-crystal exposure | Multiple Gates flickering |
| Harmonic | Stable integration | Power integrated into identity |
| Fractured | Nero Shard contamination | Gates opened and broke |
| Ascendant | Gray Threshold | Luminor rank without training |

These are measuring entirely different things and cannot be reconciled as versions of the same system. They must be explicitly framed as different classification systems used by different institutions.

**Recommended resolution:** The Academy Council's official Registry uses the power-level taxonomy (how much power does this individual represent?). The Gate-Touched Underground uses the origin taxonomy (what happened to this person?). The Underground considers the Academy taxonomy dehumanizing — it classifies Gate-Touched by their threat level rather than their experience. This transforms a continuity problem into thematic depth. Add one paragraph to GATE_TOUCHED_UNDERGROUND.md explicitly naming the Academy system and explaining why the Underground rejects it.
