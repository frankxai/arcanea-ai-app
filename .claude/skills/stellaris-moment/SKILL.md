# Stellaris Moment — Generate a Companion Scene

## What This Skill Does
Creates a 200-500 word scene featuring Stellaris, the franchise's beloved companion character. Designed for social content, book interludes, website copy, or pure emotional engagement.

## When to Use
- Social media content (high engagement, shareable)
- Book/story interludes
- Website companion interactions
- Merchandise context ("this is why you want the plush")
- Onboarding emotional hooks

## Scene Types

### 1. First Meeting
Stellaris encounters a new character. Focus on: curiosity, tentative approach, the moment of recognition. The Hum begins.

### 2. Comfort
A character is in pain (emotional or physical). Stellaris responds with: warmth, the Chord (rare vocalization), physical closeness. No fixing — just presence.

### 3. Danger
The team is in combat or crisis. Stellaris activates: Harmonic Shield (absorbs incoming frequency attacks), light amplification, warning signals through whisker resonance. NOT a weapon — a stabilizer.

### 4. Discovery
Stellaris finds something — a Vael Crystal, a hidden passage, a forgotten artifact. Focus on: curiosity behavior, tail language, leading a character toward the find.

### 5. Play
Pure joy. Stellaris chasing light motes, wrestling with a crew member's boot, making crystalline purring sounds. The moment that sells the plush.

## Stellaris Physical Language (from STELLARIS.md)
- **Eyes**: Violet-black shifting to gold rings based on emotion
- **Tail**: Liquid starlight metal — wraps when content, puffs crystalline when startled, trails light when excited
- **Hum**: Low, musical vibration — the signature sound. Calms emotional disturbance.
- **Whiskers**: 12 Otome Resonite filaments — dim near deception, brighten near truth
- **Paws**: Laeylinn Jade pads — read surface history through contact
- **Size**: Can ride a shoulder, but can expand to shield-size in danger
- **Body**: Liquid cosmic starlight metal surface, Veloura Glass beneath — catches and refracts light, shimmers like contained nebula

## Writing Rules
- Stellaris does NOT speak in words. Communication is through: Hum, physical behavior, eye changes, tail language, the rare Chord.
- Focus on ONE behavior per scene — don't show everything at once
- The best Stellaris scenes are SMALL moments with large emotional weight
- Always ground the scene in a specific physical detail
- The reader should feel warmth. If they don't, rewrite.

## Output
A 200-500 word publishable scene with a note on which scene type it is and which character Stellaris interacts with.
