# Herald Corrupt — Design a Void Ascendant

## What This Skill Does
Creates a compelling antagonist from the Void Ascendants faction. These are NOT generic villains — they are tragic figures with legitimate grievances whose solution (Shadow power) is worse than the problem.

## When to Use
- Creating antagonists for stories
- Expanding the Herald roster
- Building moral complexity into narratives
- Designing "dark mirror" characters for protagonists

## How It Works

### Step 1: Choose Original Origin Class
What were they BEFORE corruption?
- A rejected Arcan (denied by Academies)
- A feared Gate-Touched (abandoned by society)
- A discarded Synth (personhood denied)
- A severed Bonded (beast killed or taken)
- A hunted Celestial (too powerful for comfort)
- An exploited Awakened (used as tool, not person)

### Step 2: Define the Legitimate Grievance
The grievance MUST be real — something the heroes' institutions actually did wrong:
- Denied admission despite qualification
- Registered and tracked without consent
- Personhood legally denied
- Bond forcibly severed by a God
- Knowledge suppressed by Academy
- Community destroyed by Corps collateral damage

### Step 3: The Fall
How Shadow found them:
- A Whisper Network recruiter offering belonging
- A Nero Shard exposure during vulnerability
- Malachar's voice reaching through the Shadowfen seal
- A Herald mentor who seemed to understand

### Step 4: Shadow Power + Cost
Design their corrupted ability:
- What it does (must be visually compelling)
- What it costs (Shadow always takes something)
- The four stages of consumption: Quiet → Drift → Hunger → Hollow

### Step 5: Character Diamond
```yaml
desire:     # What they wanted BEFORE corruption (still want, distorted)
wound:      # The institutional failure that broke them
mask:       # How they present to recruits (reasonable, caring)
truth:      # What they'd need to accept for redemption (if possible)
```

### Step 6: The Recruitment Pitch
Write their 3-sentence argument for joining the Void Ascendants. It must be genuinely persuasive — an intelligent reader should pause and think "they have a point."

### Step 7: Mirror Relationship
Which protagonist or Corps member is their dark mirror?
- Same background, different choice
- The question between them: "What separated us?"

## Quality Rules
- Villains must be TRAGIC, not cartoonish
- Grievances must be LEGITIMATE
- Shadow Doctrine seduction must be GENUINE
- The moral question must have NO easy answer
- Beautiful in a dangerous way — not ugly or monstrous
