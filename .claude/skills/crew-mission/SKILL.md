# Crew Mission — Generate a Mission Narrative

## What This Skill Does
Creates a complete mission narrative for a Starbound Crew — from briefing through complications to aftermath. Produces 500-1000 words of publishable fiction.

## When to Use
- Content creation (social, blog, book interlude)
- Testing crew chemistry and dynamics
- Building out crew lore
- Generating story seeds for longer narratives

## Mission Structure (from STORY_ENGINE.md)

### 1. Briefing (100 words)
- Who gives the mission (Archon, Stellarch, or independent discovery)
- What the objective is
- Why this crew specifically
- What they don't know yet (dramatic irony for the reader)

### 2. Approach (100-200 words)
- Crew discusses the plan
- Internal tension surfaces (the thing they always fight about)
- Captain makes a decision that defines their leadership style
- Show crew dynamics through dialogue and behavior

### 3. Complication (200-300 words)
- The mission goes sideways
- The complication must connect to one crew member's wound
- Someone's power activates in an unexpected way
- The plan must change mid-execution

### 4. Climax (200-300 words)
- The crew must work as ONE unit
- Each member contributes their unique ability
- The internal tension either resolves or deepens
- Victory is not guaranteed — sometimes the best they can do is survive

### 5. Aftermath (100 words)
- The consequence — what changed because of this mission
- One quiet character moment (the emotional landing)
- A seed for the next mission (hook)

## Crew Options
Choose an existing crew or create one:
- **Crew Solara** — Solar Sector, noble, institutional
- **The Ninth Flame** — Ember Sector, volatile, rebellious
- **The Hollow Stars** — Void Frontier, dark, redemptive
- **The Dawnsworn** — Flagship team, cross-origin

## Writing Rules
- Each crew member must SOUND different (distinct speech patterns)
- Show powers through ACTION, not description
- The complication must be morally complex, not just physically dangerous
- At least one moment of humor or warmth (even in dark crews)
- End with an image, not exposition
