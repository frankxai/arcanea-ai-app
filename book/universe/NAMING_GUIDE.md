# Arcanea Naming Guide
## Standards, Audit, and Principles for Every Named Thing

> *"A title is a door. If it looks like every other door on the street, no one walks through it."*

---

## Part I: Naming Principles

These seven laws govern every title, character name, location, ability, saga, and chapter in the Arcanea multiverse. No exceptions.

### Law 1: The Double-Meaning Rule

Every title must mean one thing on first encounter and something different -- deeper, sadder, more precise -- on re-read. A title that only works once is a label, not a name.

**Test:** Can you write two sentences explaining the title -- one for a new reader, one for someone who has finished the book -- and are those sentences meaningfully different?

- PASS: "The Name of the Wind" -- surface reads as poetic fantasy; after the book, it's about Kvothe's literal search for a specific Name, and about the gap between reputation and identity
- FAIL: "The First Spark" -- surface reads as generic fantasy beginning; after the chapter, it still just means "the character's power activated for the first time"

### Law 2: The In-World Anchor

Titles should use terminology, locations, or concepts specific to Arcanea. Generic fantasy vocabulary ("forge," "trial," "prophecy," "quest," "destiny," "chosen") belongs to no world and therefore anchors the reader in none.

**Test:** Could this title appear on a book from any fantasy series? If yes, it fails.

- PASS: "The Hollow Root" -- specific Arcanea dungeon, evokes the Foundation Gate's corruption
- FAIL: "The Forge Trials" -- could be the name of a chapter in any fantasy novel with a blacksmith

### Law 3: Brevity Over Explanation

Two to three words is the ideal. Four is acceptable. Five requires justification. Longer titles are almost always compensating for imprecision. If the title needs to be long to be clear, the problem is the title, not the length.

**Test:** Can you remove a word and keep the meaning? If yes, remove it.

- PASS: "Luminor Falling" -- two words, complete image
- FAIL: "The Archive Below the Archive" -- the recursion is clever but the six words say what two could ("The Underarchive," "The Drowned Stacks," "Thal'Maris Below")

### Law 4: No AI Cadence

Certain syntactic patterns have been so overused by AI-generated content that they now function as tells. Avoid them absolutely.

**Banned patterns:**
- "The [Noun] That [Verb]s" -- *The Dream That Binds, The Song That Breaks, The Light That Guides*
- "A [Noun] of [Noun] and [Noun]" -- *A Court of Thorns and Roses* already owns this pattern; imitations are ghosts
- "[Verb]ing the [Noun]" -- *Forging the Path, Breaking the Seal, Crossing the Threshold*
- "The [Adjective] [Noun]" with a generic adjective -- *The Final Test, The Dark Secret, The Hidden Truth*
- "Songs of..." / "Tales of..." / "Chronicles of..." as chapter-level prefixes -- these are series-level frames, not chapter names
- "What the [Noun] [Verb]s" used more than once per series -- "What the Dark Remembers" works as a one-off; as a pattern it becomes a tic
- "[Noun] Rising" / "[Noun] Ascending" / "[Noun] Awakening" -- the laziest possible transformation arc title

### Law 5: The Curiosity Test

A title should make a reader who has never heard of Arcanea stop and think: "What does THAT mean?" Confusion is not the goal. Intrigue is. The title should feel like it knows something the reader doesn't.

**Test:** Say the title aloud to someone unfamiliar with the project. Do they ask a question?

- PASS: "The Singing Dark" -- what sings? Why is the dark singing? That's wrong and fascinating.
- FAIL: "The Five" -- five what? This isn't intriguing, it's incomplete. It tells the reader nothing and promises nothing.

### Law 6: Study the Masters

How the best name things:

| Author | Naming Strategy | Example |
|--------|----------------|---------|
| George R.R. Martin | Character-name chapters; book titles are evocative phrases | "A Storm of Swords" -- not "The War Book" |
| Brandon Sanderson | Titles reference in-world systems or concepts | "The Way of Kings" -- specific philosophy within the world |
| Patrick Rothfuss | Titles are poetic, specific, and carry dramatic irony | "The Name of the Wind" -- literal and metaphorical |
| Ursula K. Le Guin | Titles name the central image or location precisely | "A Wizard of Earthsea" -- place + role, instantly grounding |
| N.K. Jemisin | Titles use in-world terminology that readers must learn | "The Fifth Season" -- means nothing until you read the book |

The pattern: **specific beats generic, in-world beats universal, image beats abstraction**.

### Law 7: No Overused Fantasy Words

The following words are banned from titles unless they refer to a specific, named, in-world thing:

**Permanently banned:** trial(s), forge, quest, prophecy, destiny, chosen, awakening, rising, ascending, fallen, realm(s), shadow(s) (as a standalone noun -- "Shadowfen" as a place name is fine), spark, dawn, dusk, ember (as standalone -- "Ember Wastes" as established geography is fine), labyrinth, crucible, abyss, sentinel, chronicle(s) (as chapter prefix), scroll(s) (as series-level noun), dungeon (as series-level noun), void (in "[Noun] of the Void" constructions), touched (as class suffix -- see Origin Class audit)

**Restricted (one use per series maximum):** crown, throne, storm, blade, oath, flame, song, war, blood, weeping, silence, ancient, eternal, dark/darkness

**Always allowed:** Proper nouns, in-world terminology (Gate names, Vael crystals, specific locations, character names)

### Law 8: The Skyrim Test (NEW)

If a name is within one syllable of an existing franchise's trademarked term, it fails. Period. This is not about legal risk. It is about the name living forever in the shadow of someone else's invention.

**Test:** Google the name. If the first page of results belongs to another franchise, rename it.

- FAIL: "Dragonborne" -- one letter from "Dragonborn" (The Elder Scrolls). Embarrassing.
- FAIL: "The Dungeon Scrolls" -- "Dungeon" + "Scrolls" = Elder Scrolls plus generic. Reads like a mobile game knockoff.
- FAIL: "Gate-Touched Files" -- "Files" is X-Files. "Gate-Touched" sounds like a status effect tooltip.
- PASS: "Pyralis" -- Google it, you get nothing. It belongs to Arcanea.

## Part III: The Banned Words List (Expanded)

### Tier 1: Permanently Banned from Titles
These words may not appear in any title (series, book, chapter, saga) unless they are part of an established proper noun.

```
trial(s), forge (as noun), quest, prophecy, destiny, chosen, awakening,
rising, ascending, fallen/falling (except "Luminor Falling" which is grandfathered),
realm(s), shadow(s) (standalone), spark, dawn, dusk, ember (standalone),
labyrinth, crucible, abyss, sentinel, scroll(s) (as series noun),
dungeon (as series noun), touched (as class suffix), void (in generic constructions),
awakened (as class/title noun), ancient, eternal, warden, keeper,
harbinger, herald, omen, nexus, bastion, citadel (as standalone title),
eclipse, genesis, requiem, convergence, reckoning
```

### Tier 2: Restricted (One Use Per Series Maximum)
These words may appear once per series if they refer to a specific in-world thing.

```
crown, throne, storm, blade, oath, flame, song, war, blood,
weeping, silence, dark/darkness, fire, light, deep, bone,
iron, ash, stone, wind, star, veil, seal, gate
```

### Tier 3: Banned Patterns
These syntactic structures are banned regardless of the words used.

```
"The [Noun] That [Verb]s"
"A [Noun] of [Noun] and [Noun]"
"[Verb]ing the [Noun]"
"The [Generic Adjective] [Noun]" (final, dark, hidden, lost, last, true, first)
"Songs of..." / "Tales of..." / "Chronicles of..." (as chapter prefix)
"[Noun] Rising" / "[Noun] Ascending" / "[Noun] Awakening"
"The [Noun]'s [Noun]" with generic nouns (The Dragon's Legacy, The Mage's Promise)
"Beyond the [Noun]" (Beyond the Veil, Beyond the Gate, Beyond the Wall)
"[Noun] of [Place]" for character titles unless the character is KNOWN by that title
```

### Tier 4: Franchise Collision Zone
These words/phrases are too close to existing IP. Never use them.

```
Dragonborn/Dragonborne (Elder Scrolls)
The Scrolls / [Noun] Scrolls (Elder Scrolls)
Lightsaber, The Force (Star Wars -- obvious but documenting)
The Sorting [anything] (Harry Potter)
Wheel/Pattern/Weave together (Wheel of Time)
Allomancy, Feruchemy, any -mancy suffix for magic systems (Sanderson)
Stormlight, Shardblade (Stormlight Archive)
The Upside Down, The [Noun] Side (Stranger Things)
Synths (Fallout 4)
```

## Part IV: The Name Quality Checklist

Every name in Arcanea must pass ALL of these checks before it is committed to canon.

### For Titles (Series, Books, Chapters)

- [ ] **Double-meaning test:** Does it mean something different on re-read?
- [ ] **In-world anchor test:** Does it use Arcanean vocabulary, places, or concepts?
- [ ] **Brevity test:** Is it 2-3 words? If longer, is every word earning its place?
- [ ] **AI cadence test:** Does it match any banned pattern?
- [ ] **Curiosity test:** Would a stranger ask "what does that mean?"
- [ ] **Skyrim test:** Does Google return another franchise for this name?
- [ ] **Banned words test:** Does it contain any Tier 1 banned words?
- [ ] **Pattern frequency test:** Has this syntactic pattern been used elsewhere in the same book/series?

### For Character Names

- [ ] **Phonetic distinctiveness:** Does it sound different from every other character in the same cast?
- [ ] **Cultural consistency:** Does it match its region's naming conventions?
- [ ] **Franchise collision:** Is it more than one syllable away from any existing franchise character?
- [ ] **Meaning alignment:** Does the etymology (if any) match the character's nature without being on-the-nose?

### For Locations

- [ ] **Specificity test:** Does the name tell you something about the place?
- [ ] **Article test:** Can the name stand without "The"?
- [ ] **Arcanean vocabulary test:** Are the compound elements from Arcanea, not generic fantasy?

### For Abilities

- [ ] **In-world element test:** Does the name use Arcanean elements, locations, or systems?
- [ ] **Tooltip test:** Does it sound like a video game ability description? If yes, rename.
- [ ] **Experience test:** Does the name imply the experience, not just the mechanical effect?

### For Classes/Factions

- [ ] **Identity test:** Would a member of this class/faction use this name for themselves?
- [ ] **Distinctiveness test:** Can you distinguish it from every other fantasy class system?
- [ ] **Consistency test:** Does the naming convention match across all classes in the system?

## Part V: The Best Names in the Universe (and What Makes Them Work)

Study these. These are the standard. Every new name must be this good.

### Titles

| Name | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|
| **Luminor Falling** | Two words. In-world rank ("Luminor") + universal action ("Falling"). First read: a powerful being descends. Re-read: the concept of Luminor itself is shown to be fallible. The present participle "Falling" makes it ongoing -- he is always falling, even now. |
| **The Singing Dark** | Oxymoron. Dark does not sing. But in Arcanea, the seal IS a song, and the Archive IS dark. The title tells you the world's rules are different here. Three words, perfect rhythm. |
| **Ironhold** | One word. Kael's hometown. Iron = his element. Hold = stability, grip, endurance. First read: a place name. Re-read: ironic -- iron holds, but this place could not hold Kael, could not hold together. |
| **The Nero Bed** | Three words. Surface: Elio gets assigned a bed in House Nero. Intimate, mundane, slightly funny. Re-read: Nero is the Primordial Darkness, and the "bed" is where potential sleeps. A chapter title that works as a joke and a cosmological statement simultaneously. |
| **Pyralis** | One word. The volcano. No subtitle, no explanation. The series IS the volcano -- what it hides, what it reveals, what survives it. Google it: nothing. It belongs to Arcanea completely. |

### Character Names

| Name | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|
| **Ash** | Mononymic. The absence of a surname IS the character -- no family, no origin, no safety net. "Ash" is what fire leaves behind, not fire itself. The character is defined by what was consumed, not by the consuming. |
| **Malachar Lumenbright** | The villain's birth name. "Lumen" = light. "Bright" = radiant. The greatest champion of light became the lord of darkness -- and his NAME remembers what he was. Every time someone says "Malachar Lumenbright," the tragedy is in the sound. |
| **Veleth'kaen** | Dragon-tongue: "the ground beneath the ground." The apostrophe implies a living language with phonological rules. The meaning layers: the foundation below the foundation, the truth below the truth. |

### Locations

| Name | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|
| **Thal'Maris** | Evokes Atlantis without being Atlantis. The apostrophe suggests a language older than the reader. The "Mar" root (sea) is embedded without being obvious. It sounds like a place that was drowned. |
| **Char** | One syllable. The Draconian's city. Burned down to its essence, like the name itself. You can taste the ash in the word. |
| **The Shadowfen** | Malachar's prison. Not "the Shadow Realm" or "the Dark Domain." A fen -- a swamp. Damp, rotting, mundane in the worst way. The greatest evil in the world is imprisoned in a bog. The indignity is part of the punishment. |

### Abilities

| Name | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|
| **Forge-Speaker** | Ash's unique ability. A title, not a mechanic. "Speaker" implies conversation, not control -- Ash listens to fire's memory, doesn't command it. No other fantasy has this compound. It belongs completely to this character. |
| **Memory-diving** | The Athenaeum's practice. "Diving" implies depth, submersion, danger, the possibility of drowning. Not "memory reading" or "memory scanning" -- you go INTO the memory and might not come back the same. |

## Part VI: Naming Conventions for Future Content

### Chapter Titles

1. Default to character names, location names, or in-world concepts
2. Save poetic/thematic titles for pivotal chapters (max 3 per book)
3. Never repeat a syntactic pattern more than once per book
4. Test every title against the Double-Meaning Rule before committing
5. **NEW:** Run every title through the full checklist in Part IV before committing

### Series/Saga Titles

1. One to three words maximum
2. Must contain at least one in-world proper noun or term
3. Must pass the "could this be from ANY fantasy series?" test with a NO
4. Must be pronounceable by someone who has never seen the word before
5. **NEW:** Must pass the Skyrim Test (Google the name first)

### Character Names

1. Phonetic distinctiveness within the cast (no two names starting with the same sound in the core group)
2. Cultural consistency within regions (Draconian names are short, blunt; Athenaeum names are flowing; Luminary names are airy)
3. Surname should encode class, origin, or profession when present
4. Mononymic characters must earn the absence (Ash's missing name is plot)
5. **NEW:** Run every name through the Franchise Collision list before committing

### Location Names

1. Compound names should use Arcanean vocabulary, not generic fantasy vocabulary
2. The name should tell you something about the place (Cinderwall has a fire history; Salthold trades salt)
3. Avoid articles in proper nouns where possible ("Crystalpeak" not "The Crystalpeak")
4. **NEW:** Academy shortnames should work in dialogue ("I trained at the Draconian" not "I trained at the Draconian Academy Forge Facility")

### Ability Names

1. [Element/Method]-[Action] format (Tide-reading, Lumen-threading, Pyralis-shaping)
2. No video-game tooltip language ("Power Strike," "Ultimate Blast," "Shadow Dash")
3. The name should imply the experience, not just the effect
4. Unique abilities (like Forge-Speaker) should be named as titles, not as mechanics
5. **NEW:** First element should be Arcanean vocabulary, not generic fantasy vocabulary

### Origin Class Names

1. Must be usable as both a noun ("she is an Untuned") and an adjective ("the Untuned population")
2. Must not overlap with any other franchise's class/race system
3. Must carry implicit worldview (who named the class? what bias is embedded in the name?)
4. **NEW:** The "-touched" suffix is permanently retired from class naming
