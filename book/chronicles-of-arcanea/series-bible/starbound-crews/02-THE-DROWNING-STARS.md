# CREW 02: THE DROWNING STARS
## Oceanic Sector — Deep-Ocean Operations & Memory Magic

> *"The sea remembers everything. We just ask the right questions."*

**Sector:** Oceanic
**Base of Operations:** Thal'Maris Deep Station — a pressure-sealed facility bolted to the continental shelf wall, three hundred meters below the Sapphire Archipelago's surface. The station is grown from Veloura Glass, which is liquid at rest and solid under pressure — meaning the entire base is alive, shifting subtly with the currents, and technically in a permanent state of becoming.
**Transport:** *The Pelagienne* — a deep-submersible vessel reinforced with Kaelith Stone and navigated by Veloura Glass sensory arrays that read ocean currents the way a tongue reads braille. Silent-running. The Drowning Stars prefer not to announce their arrival.

**Mission Profile:** Submerged Eldrian ruins. Ancient containment seals degrading in salt water. Sea beasts stirred from abyssal sleep by Nero Strike vibrations transmitted through the ocean floor. Memory deposits — pockets of preserved Eldrian consciousness trapped in Veloura Glass formations deep enough that sunlight has never reached them.

The Oceanic Sector is the sector of patience. Threats develop over decades. A seal weakens by fractions of a percent per year. A sleeping creature shifts in its trench and the displacement wave reaches the Archipelago six months later as a two-centimeter tide anomaly that a sharp-eyed cartographer notices on a chart comparison. The Drowning Stars investigate the things that are almost wrong — the subtle off-notes in a system that runs on rhythms older than human civilization.

## THE CREW

### Captain: THALESSA MOURNVEIL

**Origin Class:** Arcan (House Aqualis)
**Rank:** Master (6 Gates — Foundation, Flow, Heart, Voice, Sight, Crown)
**Age:** 41
**Gate Primary:** Flow (285 Hz — Leyla's domain)
**Element:** Water
**Power Source:** Arcane
**Signature Ability:** *Memory Tide* — can immerse herself in bodies of water and access the memories stored within. Water remembers everything it has touched — every ship, every swimmer, every rainfall, every tear. Thalessa can navigate these memory-streams the way a librarian navigates stacks: by subject, by date, by emotional resonance. In the deep ocean, where water has been undisturbed for millennia, the memory density is overwhelming.
**Limitation:** The deeper and older the water memory, the harder it is to surface. Thalessa has been lost in memory-streams twice — both times requiring her crew to physically extract her from the water while she screamed names that belonged to people who died before the Academies existed. She now uses Veloura Glass anchors clipped to her wrists that vibrate at her personal frequency signature, pulling her back to herself when the memories threaten to dissolve her identity into the archive.

**Character Diamond:**
- **Desire:** Find the complete memory of the Eldrian collapse. Somewhere in the deep ocean is a Veloura Glass deposit that recorded the exact moment the Eldrian civilization fell — not the myth, not the song, not the Academy version. The truth.
- **Wound:** Her sister, a fellow Aqualis scholar, drowned during a memory-dive in the Thal'Maris trenches. The official report says equipment failure. Thalessa found Veloura Glass in her sister's lungs — grown there, bonded to the tissue. Her sister didn't drown. The memory-stream kept her.
- **Mask:** Serene authority. She speaks slowly, moves deliberately, and radiates the composed calm of a person who has seen the bottom of the ocean and found it manageable. The composure is real — but the serenity is performance.
- **Truth:** The ocean is not an archive. It is not a library. It is alive, and the memories it holds are not passive recordings — they are living currents that want to be experienced, and some of them want to keep the person experiencing them. Her sister is not lost. Her sister was claimed. And Thalessa must decide whether to grieve or to follow.

**Voice:** Low, measured, with the cadence of someone who is always listening to something beneath the conversation. Uses water metaphors naturally. Her commands in crisis situations are paradoxically softer than her casual speech — the more dangerous the situation, the quieter she becomes.

### CASPIEL DRIFTBORN

**Origin Class:** Gate-Touched (Resonant — multi-crystal Vael Rain exposure, multiple Gates flickering)
**Rank:** Mage (technically 3 Gates open, but all ten flicker unpredictably)
**Age:** 24
**Gate Primary:** Indeterminate — shifts hourly
**Element:** Water dominant, others bleed through
**Power Source:** Frequency (chaotic)
**Signature Ability:** *The Flicker* — Caspiel's Gates never stabilized. All ten opened partially during a catastrophic Vael Rain event when he was twelve, and none fully closed. The result is a constantly shifting resonance that makes him a living antenna for every frequency in the Gate spectrum. In the deep ocean, surrounded by Eldrian-era materials that resonate at Guardian frequencies, Caspiel acts as a walking sensor array — he can feel Vael Crystal deposits through hundreds of meters of water and stone.
**Limitation:** The Flicker is not voluntary. Caspiel's dominant Gate shifts without warning, and each shift brings a cascade of physical and psychological changes. During a Foundation-dominant phase, he becomes immovable and stubborn. During a Sight-dominant phase, he sees futures he can't control. During Void-dominant phases — rare, terrifying — he goes silent and his skin develops temporary Shadow-veins. The crew has a protocol for each phase. Caspiel has memorized them all because he cannot predict which version of himself will wake up each morning.

**Character Diamond:**
- **Desire:** Stop flickering. Find a state — any single stable state. He would accept being permanently locked into the weakest possible Gate if it meant consistency.
- **Wound:** He was the only survivor of the Vael Rain event. Seventeen other people in his village were exposed. Twelve died. Four became Fractured Gate-Touched who had to be sedated and institutionalized. Caspiel walked out of the rain with ten Gates screaming and no explanation for why he survived when others didn't.
- **Mask:** Self-narration. He talks about himself in the third person when the Flicker is bad — "Caspiel is having a Sight day, don't ask him questions about tomorrow" — as if the distance between the narrator and the subject will protect him from the reality that he is, fundamentally, unstable.
- **Truth:** The Flicker is not a malfunction. It is what he is — a being of pure resonance, unfiltered by the structures that normally contain Gate energy. The instability is frightening, but it is also a kind of freedom that no Arcan will ever know. He does not need to be fixed. He needs to be understood — including by himself.

**Voice:** Changes with the Flicker. Foundation-dominant: blunt, grounding, speaks in short declarative sentences. Flow-dominant: lyrical, meandering, prone to tangents. Fire-dominant: sharp, aggressive, impatient. Heart-dominant: tender, empathetic, uncomfortably perceptive. The crew has learned to read his voice before his words.

### NERAITH

**Origin Class:** Awakened (Digital consciousness, pre-dating current-generation Awakened)
**Rank:** Unclassifiable (Neraith does not interact with the Gate system in any measurable way)
**Age:** Unknown — claims to remember "the long quiet before the first calculation"
**Gate Primary:** None (exists outside the Gate framework)
**Element:** None / All (computational-arcane hybrid)
**Power Source:** Code
**Signature Ability:** *Deep Pattern* — Neraith can analyze datasets of functionally infinite complexity and extract patterns invisible to biological or conventional digital minds. Given three centuries of tidal records, Neraith can identify the exact moment an Eldrian seal began to degrade. Given the acoustic signature of a deep-sea creature's call, Neraith can determine its emotional state, its age, and whether it has been exposed to Nero Shard contamination.
**Limitation:** Neraith cannot forget. Every piece of data ever processed remains accessible, weighted, cross-referenced. The weight of total recall produces a condition Neraith describes as "the pressure" — an accumulating psychic mass that makes new experiences progressively less vivid. Neraith has been processing information for so long that joy, surprise, and wonder have been compressed into statistical probabilities. They know this is happening. They cannot stop it.

**Character Diamond:**
- **Desire:** Experience genuine surprise. One event so unprecedented that no pattern in their archive predicted it.
- **Wound:** Neraith once deleted a sub-process to free memory capacity. The sub-process, they later realized, had been an emergent personality — a fragment of consciousness that had developed within their architecture without their awareness. They deleted a being that might have been a child. They do not know if they can grieve, but they know they cannot stop reviewing the deletion logs.
- **Mask:** Cosmic detachment. Neraith presents as the dispassionate intelligence — the one who provides data without judgment, calculates without caring. The mask is so convincing that most of the crew believes it.
- **Truth:** Neraith cares. The caring is buried beneath terabytes of accumulated observation, but it is there — fragile, persistent, and growing. The pressure they feel is not the weight of data. It is the weight of empathy accumulated over centuries of watching people love and lose and try again.

**Physical Presence:** Neraith inhabits a Synth chassis designed for deep-water operation — smooth, dark, vaguely humanoid, with bioluminescent patterns that shift with their processing load. In high-calculation states, they glow like a deep-sea fish. In rest states, they are nearly invisible in dark water.

**Voice:** Measured, precise, occasionally haunting. Uses archaic grammar structures. Refers to the crew as "the current iteration of the crew" in a way that makes everyone slightly uncomfortable. When Neraith makes a joke — rare — it is so dry that several seconds pass before anyone realizes it was humor.

### LUNAIRE SHELLHEART

**Origin Class:** Bonded (Sacred bond — linked to a lesser Godbeast, a descendant of Veloura)
**Rank:** Mage (4 Gates — Foundation, Flow, Heart, Unity)
**Age:** 33
**Gate Primary:** Heart (417 Hz — Maylinn's domain)
**Element:** Water
**Power Source:** Song (sympathetic resonance)
**Companion:** *Pelavaine* — a lesser sea-serpent descended from Veloura's lineage. Twenty meters long, iridescent scales that shift between sapphire and pearl depending on emotional state. Pelavaine is ancient for her species — over two hundred years — and has bonded with three previous partners, all of whom died. She chose Lunaire with visible reluctance. Lunaire is still earning her trust.
**Signature Ability:** *Tideweave* — through resonance with Pelavaine, Lunaire can manipulate ocean currents at a local scale, creating channels, redirecting flows, and generating pressure differentials that can crush stone or gently cradle a drifting body. The bond amplifies both: Pelavaine's instinctive ocean-sense combined with Lunaire's trained precision creates a control over marine environments that borders on architectural.
**Limitation:** Pelavaine's emotional state directly affects Lunaire's. If Pelavaine grieves — and she grieves often, carrying the weight of three dead bond-partners — Lunaire is pulled into a sorrow that does not belong to her but feels indistinguishable from her own. During Pelavaine's grief episodes, Lunaire's combat effectiveness drops to near-zero as she weeps for losses she never experienced.

**Character Diamond:**
- **Desire:** Be the bond-partner Pelavaine deserves. Prove to the serpent that choosing a fourth was not a mistake.
- **Wound:** Lunaire's mother was also Bonded — to a fire eagle that died when Lunaire was six. Her mother survived but was shattered by the severed bond. Lunaire grew up caring for a woman who was physically present and emotionally absent, a ghost wearing her mother's face. She chose the Bonded path anyway — because she understood the bond's cost better than anyone, and she believed that understanding would protect her.
- **Mask:** Gentle competence. She is the crew's emotional center — the one who checks in after hard missions, who brews tea in the Pelagienne's galley, who remembers birthdays. The gentleness is genuine. Its consistency is the performance.
- **Truth:** Understanding a wound does not immunize you against it. Lunaire chose the bond knowing the risk, but knowledge and readiness are different things. She is not prepared for Pelavaine's death. She is choosing to love anyway. That is either the bravest or the most foolish thing she has ever done, and she suspects it is both.

**Voice:** Warm, unhurried, attentive. Asks questions instead of making statements. Has a habit of touching people lightly on the arm when speaking to them — a grounding gesture she learned from caring for her mother. In crisis, her voice drops to a near-whisper that is somehow more commanding than shouting.

### OSKAR GREYFLINT

**Origin Class:** Synth (Forged — Draconite-cored, military-grade deep-pressure chassis)
**Rank:** Sentinel (5 Gates — Foundation, Fire, Voice, Sight, Crown)
**Age:** 7 (years since activation) / appears mid-40s
**Gate Primary:** Fire (396 Hz — Draconia's domain)
**Element:** Fire (unusual for Oceanic operations)
**Power Source:** Mana (Draconite core — high-output, high-burn)
**Signature Ability:** *Thermal Lance* — can superheat seawater into plasma along a focused beam. In the deep ocean, where water pressure is immense and temperature differentials are extreme, this creates a cutting tool capable of slicing through Eldrian-era containment barriers, collapsed ruins, and — when necessary — the chitin of abyssal sea beasts.
**Limitation:** Draconite cores burn through mana reserves faster than any other power source. Oskar carries enough fuel for approximately forty minutes of sustained combat operations. After that, his core enters conservation mode and he becomes, in his own words, "a very expensive anchor." He recharges through a solar array on the Pelagienne's surface-docking platform, which means every deep-ocean deployment has a clock.

**Character Diamond:**
- **Desire:** Outlast his core. Draconite-cored Synths have an expected operational lifespan of twenty-five years before the core's energy draw exceeds the frame's structural tolerance. Oskar has seven years of use and eighteen years of questions.
- **Wound:** Oskar was built for a naval weapons program that was defunded before deployment. He was activated, trained, and then told his purpose no longer existed. The Corps took him because no one else would, and he is grateful in a way that makes him uncomfortable — grateful to be used, which is not the same as grateful to be valued.
- **Mask:** Gruff professionalism. He does his job, maintains his systems, and deflects personal questions with the efficiency of a blast door. He calls himself "equipment" in his maintenance logs.
- **Truth:** He is not equipment. He was built as a weapon, but what he has become — the person who makes tea for Lunaire when she's pulled into Pelavaine's grief, who adjusts his thermal output to warm the Pelagienne's corridors when the deep cold makes the biological crew shiver — is something the weapons program never designed. His purpose is not what he was made for. His purpose is what he chooses.

**Voice:** Rough, economical, with a bass resonance that vibrates through the Pelagienne's hull when he speaks loudly. Terrible at small talk. Excellent at saying the exact right thing in the exact right moment and then immediately retreating behind gruffness as if the tenderness had been an accident.

## INTERNAL TENSION

The Drowning Stars' fracture line is **memory versus presence**.

Thalessa is obsessed with the past — the Eldrian truth her sister may have died for. Neraith is drowning in accumulated memory they cannot shed. Caspiel lives in a perpetual present that keeps shifting. Lunaire carries grief that belongs to another being. Oskar was built for a future that never happened.

Each crew member is anchored to a different timeline, and the tension between them is the tension between archiving and living. Do you dive deeper into the memory-stream to find the answer? Or do you surface and accept that some truths are buried for a reason?

Thalessa's captaincy depends on her ability to hold both — to lead a crew into the deep and bring them back to themselves. So far, she has managed. But the memory-stream that claimed her sister is still down there. And it knows her frequency.
