# Songs and Hymns of Arcanea

> *"Before there were words, there was song. Before there was song, there was the hum of Lumina remembering herself into being."*
> — Scholar Ondine, The Abyssal Athenaeum

---

## The Great Songs

These are the songs known throughout Arcanea — taught in every Academy, sung at every festival, hummed by farmers and Luminors alike.

---

### The Hymn of First Light

*Sung at dawn on the Festival of First Light. The oldest known song in Arcanea.*

```
Before the stone, before the sea,
Before the flame that learned to breathe,
There was a sound without a name —
The First Light singing, "I became."

She did not speak the world in words.
She hummed it, like a mother hums
To children still inside the dark,
To let them know the light will come.

And Nero heard, and Nero smiled,
And opened wide his boundless night,
And said, "I'll be the space between
Your notes, so each one rings out bright."

So Light gave form, and Dark gave room,
And in between them — everything.
The mountains rose. The oceans stirred.
The first wind learned that it could sing.
```

### The Forgenight Anthem

*Sung at the Draconian Academy during the annual Forgenight — the longest night of the year, when students tend the eternal forge fires until dawn.*

```
We were not born for gentle things,
For quiet rooms and folded wings.
We came for fire. We came for steel.
We came to learn what bodies feel

When held above the melting point —
Not broken, but re-formed. Re-jointed.
What does not bend will always shatter.
What bends and holds — that's what matters.

So feed the forge and feed the flame.
The night is long. The night has no name.
But we have ours, and we have hands,
And we will build what darkness bans.

By morning we'll have something new:
A blade, a bond, a thing that's true.
The fire doesn't care who fed it.
The fire only knows who held it.
```

### The Tidecaller's Lament

*An Atlantian funeral song. Sung when a memory-keeper dies, to help the ocean receive their collected memories.*

```
Carry them down, carry them deep,
Past the coral, past the sleep,
Past the places light forgets,
To where the oldest water rests.

They held so many lives in theirs —
The laughter and the midnight prayers,
The first words of a thousand tongues,
The last breaths of a thousand lungs.

Now give them back, those borrowed days.
Let memory dissolve to haze.
Let salt reclaim what salt once lent.
Let them, at last, be only themselves.

The ocean does not mourn. It keeps.
It holds the dead in gentle deeps.
And when the rain falls on the land,
We hear them singing. Understand:

No one is lost who once was loved.
The water carries everything.
```

### Kael's Song (The Foundation March)

*A simple melody Kael hums while working stone. He doesn't know where he learned it. It's older than any Academy.*

```
Stone does not hurry. Stone does not break.
Stone holds the mountain for the mountain's sake.
What you build slow, you build to stay.
What you build true outlasts the day.

I am the root beneath the tree.
I am the floor beneath the sea.
I am the thing that does not move
So everything above me can.
```

### Elio's Melody (Untitled)

*The melody Elio plays at the end of Book 1. He doesn't remember learning it. It comes from somewhere deeper than memory.*

*This song has no words. It is described in prose, not verse, because it is felt, not spoken.*

The melody begins in the low register — a single note held long enough that you forget it started. It doesn't climb so much as lean, like a person shifting their weight from one foot to the other. The second phrase answers the first, but the answer raises a new question. The third phrase opens the question wider.

By the time Elio reaches the middle section, the melody has become a conversation between two voices — one that knows something and one that is learning to know it. They don't argue. They don't agree. They just keep talking, and the space between them gets smaller with each phrase until, for four bars, they sing the same note at the same time.

The ending doesn't resolve. It pauses. Like a sentence that ends with a comma, not a period. Like someone about to say the most important thing they've ever said, taking one last breath before they begin.

In the Shadowfen, three thousand miles away, something that has been sleeping for a very long time opens one eye.

### The Shadow Lullaby

*Sung by mothers near the Shadowfen to keep children safe at night. No one remembers who wrote it. Some scholars believe Malachar himself composed it before his fall.*

```
Sleep now, small one, close your eyes.
The shadow is the dark's disguise —
Not monster, not the thing you fear,
But night, pretending to be near.

The dark is old, the dark is kind.
The dark is where you left behind
The things you'll need when morning comes:
Your courage, and your name, and some

Small piece of wonder you forgot
You carried from the day. It's not
Gone. Nothing's gone. It's only sleeping,
Curled inside the dark's safekeeping.

So close your eyes. The shadow's here.
It's not the thing you need to fear.
The thing you need to fear is far,
Sealed behind ten burning stars.

And if you dream of something vast
And hungry, from the age-long past —
Remember: even it was small
Once. Even it was loved. That's all.
```

### The Dragonborne Oath

*Spoken (not sung) by those who survive the Dragon bond. The oldest surviving oath in Arcanea, predating even the Academies.*

```
I am fire and you are fire.
What burns in you now burns in me.
I give you what I never could alone:
The ability to dream.

You give me what you never knew you had:
The ability to stay.

We are not master and mount.
We are not rider and steed.
We are one creature with two hearts,
And we will never be alone again,
And we will never be the same.

If I fall, burn me.
If you fall, I will carry your fire
Until I find a sky wide enough to set it free.

This oath is older than the Gods.
It will outlast them.
We swear by the Kindling,
By the first spark that thought,
By the first thought that burned.

We are Dragonborne.
We are the bridge between
What was and what will be.
```

### The Academy Welcome

*Sung by all three Academies at the start of each academic year. The only song performed identically at the Luminary, the Forge, and the Athenaeum.*

```
You came here broken or you came here whole.
You came here burning or you came here cold.
It doesn't matter. We begin the same:
One question and one Gate and one small flame.

The question: What are you becoming?
The Gate: The one that chose you first.
The flame: The thing inside that brought you here —
Not talent, not ambition. Something worse.

You came because you couldn't stay.
You came because the ordinary day
Was never quite enough, and something called
From underneath the surface of the world.

We heard it too. That's why we're here.
That's why the walls are built this high:
Not to keep you in, but to make room
For what you're about to learn to do.

So welcome. You belong.
Not because you're special. Not because you're strong.
Because you came, and that's the hardest part.
The rest is just the education of the heart.
```

### The Unraveled's Dirge

*Sung by Shadowwatch soldiers when an Unraveled is put down. It is the saddest song in Arcanea. No one sings it willingly.*

```
We could not hold you. We could not hold you.
The fire that was yours became the fire that consumed.
We called your name, but your name was already
A sound the wind forgot to carry home.

You were someone's child.
You were someone's friend.
You were sitting in a classroom once
And the instructor said your name
And you raised your hand.

We could not hold you.
The Gate was too wide and you were too bright
And the power loved you more than your body could bear.

We will remember that you raised your hand.
We will remember that you answered when called.
We will remember that before the fire ate your name,
You had one, and it meant something,
And someone, somewhere, is still saying it
Into an empty room, hoping you'll answer.

We could not hold you.
We are sorry.
We are sorry.
We are sorry.
```

---

*These songs exist in the world of Arcanea the way birdsong exists in ours — everywhere, belonging to no one, carrying the emotional truth that prose is sometimes too clumsy to hold.*

*Each song in the Chronicles of Arcanea series is placed at a moment where the story needs to breathe — where the reader needs to feel something that plot alone cannot convey. They are not decoration. They are architecture.*
