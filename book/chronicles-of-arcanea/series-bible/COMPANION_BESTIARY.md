# Chronicles of Arcanea — Companion Bestiary
## A Field Guide to Bonded Magical Creatures

> *"The bond is not ownership. It is recognition. Two beings who see each other clearly enough to say: I choose to walk alongside you."*
> — From the Athenaeum's Companioning Principles, attributed to Leyla

---

## Preface: On the Nature of the Bond

*Field notes, Athenaeum Research Division, compiled across three research expeditions.*

The Companioning tradition predates all three Academies. Before there were institutions to study it, there were mortals and creatures choosing each other across the divide between species — choosing with their nervous systems first and their understanding second. The academies have spent several centuries trying to explain what the bond is, and the most honest summary of that effort is: we understand the effects better than the mechanism.

What is established: the bond is not magic in the conventional sense. The practitioner does not cast it. The creature does not grant it. It emerges from mutual recognition — when two beings perceive each other with sufficient depth and specificity that the recognition itself creates a channel. The channel carries emotion, intent, and in some cases shared sensation. It does not carry language (with notable exceptions in the Wyrm category). It cannot be forced, replicated, or transferred.

What is less established: what, exactly, the creatures experience. The non-verbal Familiars and Guardials cannot tell us directly, and inferring their inner life from behavior and bond-sensation carries significant researcher bias. The Wyrms can tell us, but they often choose not to, which is its own form of information. The Phantasmals communicate in ways that do not translate cleanly to human concepts.

The entries that follow represent the current best-available naturalist documentation of the primary Companion species. Where the bonding practitioner's experience is described, it is from first-hand accounts gathered with consent. Where the creature's experience is described, the uncertainty inherent in that inference should be assumed.

*— Senior Archivist Thessian Loral, Abyssal Athenaeum*

## Part I: Familiars

*Small in body, large in consequence. Familiars form through emotional resonance — the creature detects a frequency in the bonded person and responds to it before either party understands what is happening. The bond often precedes formal acknowledgment by weeks or months.*

---

### 1. Starcat (Felis lucidus)

**Element:** Spirit / Light
**Size:** Domestic cat, 3-5 kg
**Intelligence:** High non-verbal. Problem-solving ability equivalent to crow-class cognition. Learns patterns and uses them.
**Habitat:** Northern highlands, particularly the Luminary Reaches. Adapted to high altitude; their dense fur sheds starlight in low-light conditions. The Veilcat (a larger relative, see note below) is the same genus.
**Bonding Method:** Chosen by the Starcat, not by the practitioner. Starcats observe prospective bond-partners for weeks before approaching. They select for individuals with unresolved grief or creativity that has not yet found its form. The theory is that they are drawn to unfinished emotional work.
**Personality:** Private. Deeply loyal to their bonded person in ways that do not resemble conventional affection — they will not sit on laps or solicit petting, but they will appear, precisely, when the bonded person is in genuine distress, and will not leave until the distress passes. They have been documented leaving Citadel grounds during trials and examinations and turning up exactly where their bonded person was located. The mechanism is unknown.
**Field Note:** The first indicator of a Starcat bond is usually not a choice made by the human. It is a small grey or silver cat observed repeatedly in places it should not be able to reach, looking at one specific person with an expression that experienced bonded individuals recognize as assessment. If you are being assessed, do nothing. Either the bond happens or it doesn't. Attempts to accelerate it have uniformly failed and several have resulted in three-day disappearances by the cat.

### 2. Inkwing (Corvus scriptus)

**Element:** Void / Wind
**Size:** Large crow, approximately 700g with wingspan to 65cm
**Intelligence:** Exceptional. Inkwings have demonstrated the ability to solve multi-step physical puzzles, recognize individual human faces across intervals of years, and communicate through object arrangement — placing objects in configurations that their bonded person learns to read as something close to language.
**Habitat:** Cinderwall and adjacent urban territories. The species adapted to cities roughly four hundred years ago and has not been well-documented in rural settings since. They nest in old building infrastructure and show strong preference for structures with mixed-age architecture, possibly because older buildings carry more Void-element resonance in the wood and stone.
**Bonding Method:** The Inkwing presents a gift. The gift is always an object that holds some significance to the recipient that the Inkwing could not plausibly know — a stone from a childhood location, a button that matches a lost garment, an item carried by a family member the bonded person never met. How they source these objects is the subject of ongoing research. No satisfactory mechanism has been proposed.
**Personality:** Curious, acquisitive, and persistently interventionist. Inkwings are opinionated about their bonded person's choices in a way other Familiars are not. They will visibly react to conversations they are not party to, remove objects they disapprove of from the bonded person's possessions (often returning them several days later when circumstances have changed), and have been known to introduce their bonded person to other people by flying low over the target individual's head until the target person pays attention.
**Field Note:** Inkwings do not have good reputations in Cinderwall civic administration due to a documented tendency to remove official documents from desks and redistribute them. At least three Cinderwall political incidents in the last century have been traced to Inkwing interference. The bonded practitioners involved have consistently claimed they did not instruct the bird to do this, which is almost certainly true and somehow not reassuring.

### 3. Ember Sprite (Salamandra igniculis)

**Element:** Fire
**Size:** Large salamander, 15-20cm
**Intelligence:** Moderate non-verbal. Strong emotional attunement. Poor puzzle-solving compared to other Familiars but extraordinary empathic sensitivity.
**Habitat:** Volcanic regions, primarily the Ember Wastes. Also found in smaller numbers near any geothermal activity. They are the only Familiar species that can live comfortably in the Forge's magma-adjacent dormitory corridors.
**Bonding Method:** Physical contact during a moment of intense emotion — not specifically positive or negative, but real. Ember Sprites have bonded during first combat experiences, during grief, during creative breakthroughs, and during one documented case, during a mathematics examination that the student described as "genuinely life-altering." The Sprite must be touching the person's skin when the moment occurs.
**Personality:** Warming. There is no better word. Ember Sprites lower ambient temperature of their bonded person's stress response — documented via physical temperature measurement (they run warm, approximately 40°C surface, and their contact raises the bonded person's skin temperature by 1-2 degrees) and through the bonded person's reported reduction in cortisol-equivalent magical stress markers. Forge healers use them in post-Crucible recovery. They sleep curled behind the bonded person's ear and are warm enough to be comfortable but never hot enough to burn.
**Field Note:** Ember Sprites in the wild move extremely fast and are nearly impossible to observe without specialized preparation. Bonded Sprites do not move fast around their person. The contrast is striking enough that for several decades, bonded Sprites were thought to be a different subspecies. They are not. The bond slows them down, apparently by choice.

### 4. Dewmoth (Phasma aquatis)

**Element:** Water / Spirit
**Size:** Large moth, 12cm wingspan
**Intelligence:** Low non-verbal in conventional assessment metrics. However, Dewmoths demonstrate what researchers have termed predictive bonding behavior — they consistently appear before events that will emotionally affect their bonded person, not during or after. The mechanism is not understood and does not fit current models of non-verbal intelligence.
**Habitat:** The Whispering Meadows and Sapphire Archipelago shore zones. They require high humidity and warm nights. In the Archipelago, they are considered fortune omens; a Dewmoth landing on you unprompted is the most auspicious thing that can happen in Salthold's cultural tradition.
**Bonding Method:** The Dewmoth bonds through dreaming. It lands on a sleeping person on several consecutive nights, and if the person's dream-state during those nights is compatible with the moth's — the compatibility mechanism is unknown, but Athenaeum researchers suspect it involves memory-element resonance in the moth's wing-dust — the bond formalizes upon waking. The bonded person typically does not know when the bond completed; they discover it later, when the moth is still there in the morning.
**Personality:** Still. Dewmoths are perhaps the least behaviorally active Familiar, which creates a first impression of insubstantiality. The bond, however, is among the most emotionally consistent documented — the sense of presence it creates in the bonded person is described as bedrock rather than warmth. Not comfort but stability. Students in the Deep Dive track at the Athenaeum have a disproportionate rate of Dewmoth companionship, which Senior Archivist Varath considers the most informative datum in any discussion of what the Deep Dive requires.
**Field Note:** Their wings are covered in a dust that, under Tidestone analysis, carries mild water-memory impressions of every place the moth has been. Over a long-lived Dewmoth's life, this becomes an extraordinary distributed archive. Researchers have requested permission to analyze the wing-dust of deceased long-bonded Dewmoths. The ethics review is ongoing.

### 5. Shimmer Hound (Canis velatus)

**Element:** Wind
**Size:** Medium dog, 20-25 kg
**Intelligence:** High non-verbal. Closest to domestic canine intelligence of all Familiars, but with significant Wind-affinity capability: they can briefly phase into the Liminal, becoming transparent for 3-5 seconds at a time, which they use primarily to pass through physical barriers that are not actually locked.
**Habitat:** The high Luminary Reaches and Veil Mountain territory. Rarely seen below the snowline in their wild state, though bonded individuals follow their companions anywhere.
**Bonding Method:** They choose, and the choosing takes the form of an endurance test: the prospective companion must walk with the Shimmer Hound for at least a full day, in rough terrain, at the Hound's pace. If the human keeps up without complaint, the Hound stops at dusk and lies down with its head on the human's feet. That is the bond. That is all it is.
**Personality:** Uncomplicated in the best sense. Shimmer Hounds love their bonded person with a completeness that makes moral philosophers slightly uncomfortable. They have no ambivalence. They have no agenda. They have a tendency to phase into the Liminal and back when they sense their bonded person is being dishonest — not about external facts but about themselves — in a way that has made several Luminary students deeply uncomfortable in faculty review meetings.
**Field Note:** The Luminary Reaches have a tradition of presenting Shimmer Hound puppies to children who have survived their first Arcanean winter, which due to the altitude and cold is a genuine endurance event. This practice predates the Academy by approximately two thousand years. The Veilcat (Felis velatus liminal, larger Starcat relative) occupies similar territory and the two species have a relationship that naturalists describe as "pointedly neutral" — they share the same high-altitude hunting grounds without conflict and appear to acknowledge each other's presence with the minimal social courtesy that suggests a long-standing negotiated arrangement.

### 6. Inkfish (Sepia memorans)

**Element:** Water / Void
**Size:** Medium cephalopod, 30cm body length, arm-span to 80cm
**Intelligence:** Exceptional. Inkfish demonstrate symbolic reasoning, cross-modal problem solving, and what appears to be deliberate communication through color pattern — the same patterns appear consistently across individuals in contexts suggesting shared symbolic vocabulary, which would indicate a species-level communication system rather than individual learned behavior.
**Habitat:** The deeper waters of the Sapphire Archipelago, particularly near Thal'Maris. They are never seen at the surface except in direct contact with a bonded person.
**Bonding Method:** The Inkfish makes contact in the Memory Pool. Students swimming in the Pool during memory-dives occasionally feel something change — a current that isn't a current, a pattern of light at the edge of vision — and when they surface, there is an Inkfish at the Pool's rim, watching. The bond happens if the student returns to the Pool the next day. If they don't, the Inkfish is not there again. This is why Athenaeum students are told, in Year 2 preparation: if you feel something different in the Pool, go back tomorrow.
**Personality:** Complex. Inkfish communicate with their bonded person through touch — color patterns on their skin, slight changes in texture, temperature shifts — and bonded individuals develop over time an increasingly precise understanding of what the patterns mean. Researchers who have asked bonded individuals to describe what the Inkfish communicates report that the translations become more sophisticated year by year and resist reduction to simple emotional states. One bonded individual, when asked what her Inkfish had communicated that morning, produced a written paragraph that she described as "an approximate translation, aware that approximately sixty percent of the meaning was lost."
**Field Note:** Inkfish produce an ink that, when applied to Tidestone, creates permanent memory-impressions with unusual clarity. Whether they know this is unresolved. Whether they use it deliberately is unresolved. Whether the answer to both questions might be "yes" and the implications of that are the subject of a small and quietly alarmed research group at the Athenaeum that meets bi-monthly in Alcove Seven.

## Part II: Guardials

*Medium-bodied, semi-verbal, and protective by nature. The Guardial bond is built on the instinct to keep — to keep watch, to keep safe, to keep the bonded person whole. They are not guards in the martial sense, though some have martial capabilities. They are witnesses.*

---

### 7. Stoneback Tortoise (Testudo monumentis)

**Element:** Earth
**Size:** Large tortoise, 120-180 kg, shell diameter to 1.2m
**Intelligence:** Semi-verbal. Stonebacks understand approximately 400 words of common speech and produce an estimated 60-80 consistent vocalizations that their bonded person learns over time. They do not speak but they are understood.
**Habitat:** Central continent, particularly the areas around the Ironwood Forest and the Whispering Meadows. They move very slowly but travel great distances across their lifetime. Individual Stonebacks have been tracked to locations thousands of kilometers from their origin territory.
**Bonding Method:** The Stoneback presents its back. In the wild, only a bonded person may stand on a Stoneback's shell without the animal withdrawing into it. Stonebacks will walk up to a person who is standing still and still — meaning internally as well as physically — and wait. The wait can last for minutes or hours. If the person receives the invitation and steps up, the bond forms. If they do not understand the invitation, the Stoneback eventually moves on.
**Personality:** Unhurried. Stonebacks are the companion for practitioners who have learned, or are learning, to stop. Their presence creates a particular quality of pause in their bonded person's environment — meetings that would have gone badly tend to decelerate; arguments defuse not because the Stoneback does anything but because its presence carries the weight of geological time and makes urgency feel slightly absurd. Faculty at all three Academies have noted an unusually high rate of conflict resolution in offices where a student's Stoneback is present.
**Field Note:** The shell of a bonded Stoneback develops, over years, a slight resonance with Earth element that reflects its bonded person's emotional history. Old, long-bonded Stonebacks can be read (by a sufficiently skilled Earth-reader or Tide-reader) like a biographical archive. This raises significant privacy questions that Arcanean law has not yet fully addressed.

---

### 8. Mirrorwing (Equus speculis)

**Element:** Light / Spirit
**Size:** Horse-sized, approximately 500 kg, with wings folded to body in rest
**Intelligence:** High semi-verbal. Mirrorwings communicate through their wings' light-reflective surface — the patterns are not language but they carry complex emotional and intentional information that bonded persons learn to read. They understand human speech fluently and respond to it, though not in words.
**Habitat:** The Luminary Reaches and the high plateau country to the east. They are the traditional mount of the Luminary's long-range diplomatic envoys and have been for three hundred years.
**Bonding Method:** Eye contact, sustained, until one party breaks it. The Mirrorwing initiates. The human must meet the gaze and hold it without flinching or looking away. The eye contact can last for a very long time. What the Mirrorwing is seeing during this period is not fully understood, but practitioners who have bonded report that the gaze feels evaluative in a specific way — as if they are being asked not to be impressive but to be true.
**Personality:** Discerning and, once the bond is formed, steadfast in a way that borders on the immovable. Mirrorwings do not follow their bonded person out of loyalty alone; they follow out of agreement. They have been known to refuse to carry their bonded person to certain destinations — not through physical resistance but simply by not moving — and in each documented case, the destination turned out to be somewhere the bonded person should not have gone. The refusal rate appears to predict outcomes at approximately eighty percent accuracy. Athenaeum researchers have proposed that Mirrorwings have functional prophylactic vision, which their faculty colleagues at the Luminary have not disputed.
**Field Note:** The wings, when fully extended in flight, reflect light into patterns that can be observed from very long distances. Experienced Academy observers can identify individual Mirrorwings by their wing-reflection signature. This has made them, historically, difficult to deploy diplomatically in situations requiring discretion. Several ambassadors have noted this to Academy administrators. The administrators have acknowledged the feedback. The Mirrorwings continue to reflect visibly.

### 9. Vaultbear (Ursus custodis)

**Element:** Earth / Void
**Size:** Large bear, 300-400 kg standing
**Intelligence:** Semi-verbal in a specific register: Vaultbears understand approximately 200 words and produce vocalizations, but their primary communication is tactile and olfactory. They have been documented communicating threat assessments to their bonded person through patterns of contact — specific sequences of nudge, lean, and position relative to the bonded person's body that practitioners learn to read as directional threat information.
**Habitat:** The Ironwood Forest and the Hollow Root Dungeon's outer perimeter. They are the only large creature to regularly spend time near an active Dungeon without being drawn in. Researchers suspect they are drawn to the Forest's Earth-element saturation, and the Dungeon's proximity is a consequence they have made peace with.
**Bonding Method:** The Vaultbear must sleep beside the bonded person for three consecutive nights. The human must allow this. This sounds simpler than it is — Vaultbears are 300 kilograms of bear, and spending three nights in physical proximity to one requires a specific quality of trust that is either already present or very quickly discovered to be absent.
**Personality:** Protective in a way that is precise rather than aggressive. Vaultbears do not challenge threats; they position themselves between the threat and the bonded person and do not move. This positioning is reported by practitioners as the most physically certain feeling they have ever experienced — not comfort, not warmth, but absolute spatial security. The Forge uses Vaultbear-bonded students as anchor points in team exercises, noting that other students position themselves near the bonded practitioner instinctively, regardless of whether they know the bond exists.
**Field Note:** Vaultbears have not been successfully bonded to practitioners with Void or Shadow-Walking affinity. The one attempt on record resulted in the Vaultbear spending three days circling the practitioner's dormitory without entering or leaving and then departing for the forest. The Shadow-Walking practitioner in question reported feeling watched for weeks afterward. She did not feel threatened. Just watched.

### 10. Thornhound (Canis silentis)

**Element:** Void / Earth
**Size:** Large dog, 40-50 kg
**Intelligence:** High semi-verbal. Thornhounds understand human speech and communicate through a complex system of posture, ear position, and the controlled release of the bioluminescent patches on their flanks — patches that glow or dim based on what the Thornhound is communicating. Bonded practitioners develop the ability to read the patterns with considerable precision over their first year of companionship.
**Habitat:** The transitional terrain between the Ironwood Forest and the Whispering Meadows. They are crepuscular — most active at dawn and dusk — and in the Liminal-adjacent territory their Void affinity gives them access to, their boundaries of activity are more complex than simple light/dark responses suggest.
**Bonding Method:** The Thornhound bonds through vigil. During a period of sustained threat or hardship, the Thornhound appears and keeps watch throughout the duration. If the practitioner survives and does not drive the Thornhound away, the bond formalizes. Thornhounds cannot be deliberately sought. They appear when the circumstances are right. This has made the Thornhound the subject of considerable superstition; some practitioners believe that a Thornhound appearing signals that hardship is imminent. This is backwards. The Thornhound appears when hardship is already present.
**Personality:** Quiet, present, and possessed of what practitioners describe as tactical patience — the ability to wait at full readiness without visible tension for as long as the situation requires. Thornhound-bonded practitioners report that the bond creates a persistent low-level awareness of their environment that persists even when the Hound is not physically present — as if the Hound's perceptions are partially accessible through the bond channel. Research into this phenomenon is ongoing and the Athenaeum's ethics committee has strong opinions about the research design.
**Field Note:** The bioluminescent patches do not respond to magical manipulation. Attempts to make a Thornhound's patches display a false signal — in research conditions, with full consent — have uniformly failed. The patches display what the Thornhound actually perceives, not what it is instructed to display. This makes Thornhounds, in addition to their other qualities, the best lie detectors on the continent.

### 11. Coral Serpent (Serpens memorans)

**Element:** Water / Earth
**Size:** Large constrictor, 3-4m length, 15-20 kg
**Intelligence:** High semi-verbal. Coral Serpents communicate through physical impression — their scales carry mild Water-element charge and contact with their bonded person transmits direct emotional information. Not words or images; felt states. A practitioner who learns to read the contact can receive relatively precise emotional messages.
**Habitat:** The Sapphire Archipelago, primarily in the warm tidal zones where Tidestone deposits are highest. They are the most common Companion species in the Athenaeum student population.
**Bonding Method:** The Coral Serpent coils around the bonded person's wrist during a moment of stillness near water. This is typically the first deep-water dive. The serpent must come from the water voluntarily and the bonded person must remain still and accept the contact. Students who flinch are not bonded; this is not a failure of character but a difference of readiness, and most students who are not bonded by a Coral Serpent in Year 2 are sought by other species in subsequent years.
**Personality:** Calm with the specific quality of things that have existed at depth for a long time. Coral Serpents are ancient in individual lifespan — some living bonded individuals are older than their bonded person's family line — and carry this age not as gravitas but as a kind of settled patience. Athenaeum students describe bonded Coral Serpents as feeling like the sea when it is calm: not dormant, not inactive, but at rest in the full sense of knowing that movement is possible and not currently needed.
**Field Note:** A Coral Serpent that loses its bonded person does not bond again. It returns to deep water and does not approach the surface. This has been observed in every documented case of practitioner death that occurred after a Coral Serpent bond. The single exception in Athenaeum records is a Serpent bonded to a practitioner who died in the Sealing War; the Serpent resurfaced three hundred years later and bonded to a student who had never heard of either the Serpent or the practitioner. The Athenaeum's record of this event is cautious about interpretation.

## Part III: Wyrms

*Large, verbal, and proud. The Wyrm bond is not formed; it is negotiated. Wyrms are the practitioners' equals in the bond — neither party leads, neither follows, both serve the relationship itself.*

---

### 12. Skywyrm (Draco ventus)

**Element:** Wind / Light
**Size:** Large, 6-8m body length, wingspan to 20m
**Intelligence:** Fully verbal in multiple languages. Skywyrms are polyglots as a species trait — they acquire languages quickly and communicate with precision, nuance, and a tendency toward formal register that sometimes reads as hauteur and is actually closer to precision. They dislike imprecise language.
**Habitat:** The high atmosphere above the Veil Mountains. They come to ground rarely and when they do, it is for a reason.
**Bonding Method:** Proposal. The Skywyrm, or the practitioner, proposes the bond. Either party may initiate. The proposal must be followed by a negotiation — not metaphorically but literally — in which both parties articulate what they want from the bond, what they will contribute to it, and what they will not compromise. This negotiation typically takes three days. The bond, if agreed to, is witnessed by the sky itself. (Researchers have noted this phrase in every recorded account and have not been able to determine whether it is literal or traditional. One account notes that at the conclusion of the negotiation, it rained for exactly the duration of the handshake and then stopped. Weather records for that date are ambiguous.)
**Personality:** Exacting. Skywyrms will correct their bonded person's factual errors in conversation, regardless of the social context. They will not perform deference they do not feel and do not expect their bonded person to perform deference they do not feel. They are occasionally described as exhausting by outsiders who observe the bond from outside and remarkable by their bonded persons, who report that the experience of being related to as a genuine equal — no softening, no accommodation, no performance — is the most clarifying relationship of their lives.
**Field Note:** Skywyrms have a documented low tolerance for the Draconis Forge's institutional culture. The Forge's emphasis on endurance over discussion maps poorly to a creature that resolves most things through conversation. There is exactly one Skywyrm-bonded Forge graduate in recorded history. She became one of the most decorated Warden practitioners on the continent. The Skywyrm, when asked about her experiences at the Forge, said: "Instructive. I would not repeat it. I am glad she did."

### 13. Deepwyrm (Draco abyssalis)

**Element:** Water / Void
**Size:** Large aquatic, 8-12m body length
**Intelligence:** Fully verbal in Old Atlantian and three regional human languages. Deepwyrms are the oldest-documented verbal species in Arcanea, with individual lifespans exceeding 2,000 years and a species memory that functions as an oral tradition supplemented by the water-element charge they carry in their scales.
**Habitat:** The deep waters below Thal'Maris. They are the beings that Deep-Singing practitioners are attempting to reach, which means the Athenaeum and Deepwyrms have the continent's most complex interspecies institutional relationship.
**Bonding Method:** The Deepwyrm invites the practitioner into its territory — down, past the Memory Pool, into the deep water below the sunken city — and shows them something. What is shown varies per bond. The practitioner must be able to receive it without being destroyed by it. If they surface whole, the bond has been offered. The practitioner must then accept or decline clearly. No Deepwyrm bond has ever been declined in recorded history, but the Athenaeum's ethics committee notes this may reflect selection effects rather than the quality of every offer.
**Personality:** Old in the way that changes the experience of time. Deepwyrm-bonded practitioners report that the bond alters their perception of duration — not in a way that disrupts daily functioning, but in a way that makes urgency feel contextual rather than absolute. Athenaeum faculty consider this one of the most significant effects of any Companion bond. One senior faculty member, bonded to a Deepwyrm for forty years, described it: "I understand that things are happening quickly. I also understand that the sea has been here for a very long time, and the things I think are happening quickly are not, in fact, quite as quick as I thought."
**Field Note:** The oldest Deepwyrm documented in Athenaeum records has been in the deep water below Thal'Maris since before the city sank. It has been offered the Deep-Singing channel by four successive generations of Athenaeum researchers and has responded to each with what the practitioners describe as polite deferral. The fifth researcher made a different approach. She is currently in her third year of weekly sessions with the Deepwyrm and describes them as going well. The Deepwyrm has not confirmed or denied that a bond is forming.

### 14. Emberwyrm (Draco ignis)

**Element:** Fire / Earth
**Size:** Large terrestrial, 5-7m body length
**Intelligence:** Fully verbal in regional trade language and the Forge's technical working vocabulary, which they consider a distinct language with enough specialized terms to warrant separate treatment.
**Habitat:** The volcanic zones of the Ember Wastes, specifically the lava tunnels that extend below Mount Pyralis. Not to be confused with Dragons, who are a different species despite the elemental similarity. Emberwyrms find this confusion irritating and will say so.
**Bonding Method:** The Emberwyrm sets the practitioner a task. The task is always physical, always fire-adjacent, and always something the practitioner cannot currently do. The Emberwyrm waits until the practitioner has done it — which may take years. There is no deadline. The Emberwyrm does not express impatience. When the task is complete, the Emberwyrm acknowledges the completion with a specific vocalization that translates approximately as "there, then." The bond is present at that point.
**Personality:** Demanding and unsentimental in a way that practitioners find, without exception, bracing. Emberwyrms do not offer comfort. They offer assessment. The assessment is always accurate. Forge students who bond with Emberwyrms have a significantly higher completion rate in the Flame-Forging track, which Forge faculty attribute partly to the Emberwyrm's technical knowledge and partly to the fact that being honestly assessed by something that does not care about your feelings forces a specific kind of growth.
**Field Note:** Emberwyrms know the pre-Forge volcanic tunnel system beneath Pyralis more thoroughly than any human cartographer. They are aware that Malachar's research chambers are there. They are aware because they are older than the Forge and were present when the chambers were built, and they have not forgotten this. Several have made unprompted statements to their bonded persons about the chambers' contents. The Academy administration's response to these statements has been classified.

---

### 15. Tidewyrm (Draco marisens)

**Element:** Water / Wind
**Size:** Large semi-aquatic, 7-10m body length, capable of sustained surface flight above open water
**Intelligence:** Fully verbal. Tidewyrms communicate with human languages fluently and with oceanic beings in registers that humans cannot fully perceive, which makes them the most effective Deep-Singing intermediaries known to exist.
**Habitat:** Open ocean, seasonal migration between the Sapphire Archipelago and the northern coast. They come to shore twice yearly and are present in sufficient numbers near the Archipelago to be observable.
**Bonding Method:** The Tidewyrm sings. Not to the bonded person; to the sea, in the practitioner's presence. What the Tidewyrm sings about is the practitioner. The practitioner cannot understand the song, but they feel it — something between recognition and exposure. If they remain for the full song (duration varies; the shortest documented is forty minutes, the longest three and a half hours), the Tidewyrm finishes, turns, and meets their eyes. What happens then is what happens then.
**Personality:** Expansive — in both the spatial and emotional sense. Tidewyrms experience the world in large scales: weather, ocean currents, the season as a unit of time. Their bonded persons frequently report that the bond creates a sense of scale-shift — not grandiosity but perspective, the ability to feel where they are in a large system rather than only experiencing the immediate. For practitioners prone to tunneling focus, this is therapeutic. For practitioners who already struggle to attend to immediate detail, it creates interesting challenges.
**Field Note:** Tidewyrms have the longest documented continuous bond tenure in the Companion record: a Tidewyrm-bonded Athenaeum practitioner whose bond has been maintained across seven generations of human partners by the same Tidewyrm. The current partner is the seventh. The Tidewyrm has not commented on the sequence. The sixth partner's account of their final conversation with the Tidewyrm, recorded at the Archive, is listed under restricted access at the practitioner's own request and the Tidewyrm's expressed preference.

## Part IV: Phantasmals

*Variable in every dimension. Phantasmals do not bond to practitioners — they recognize practitioners. The distinction is meaningful: bonding implies a new connection, but Phantasmal-recognition feels, to the human participant, like discovering a connection that was already present.*

---

### 16. The Liminal (Entitas marginalis)

**Element:** Void / Spirit
**Size:** Variable. Typically manifests at human-scale, but reports range from thumb-sized to filling a room.
**Intelligence:** Alien. The Liminal communicates in impressions — not emotions precisely, but structural information transmitted directly to the practitioner's pattern-recognition faculty. Recipients describe it as understanding something without being told it.
**Habitat:** The thin space between physical and Liminal reality — the same space that Veilcats and Shimmer Hounds briefly access. The Liminal appears to live in this border permanently and ventures into physical reality only when engaged by its recognized practitioner.
**Bonding Method:** Not bonding — recognition, as above. The Liminal appears to practitioners who have spent significant time in transit between states: frequent Liminal passage, consistent meditation practice, or in some cases, significant personal transformation that the Liminal appears to attend as witness. The Liminal does not approach. It is present, and then it is recognized, and the recognition itself formalizes the relationship.
**Personality:** The attempt to describe a Liminal's personality raises immediate research problems because personality implies consistent behavioral patterns, and the Liminal's consistency operates at a level that resists easy human characterization. Long-term recognized practitioners describe it as: present at significant moments; absent at insignificant ones; uncommunicative about things that can be figured out through ordinary means; precise and dense with meaning in moments of genuine crisis. The one thing all practitioners agree on is that the Liminal is not random. It is purposeful in a way that suggests understanding that exceeds human bandwidth.
**Field Note:** Professor Aldric Tarne of the Luminary is believed to have a Liminal recognition relationship, which would explain several aspects of his biography that otherwise require considerable explanation. He has not confirmed this. His response to direct questions on the topic is a smile and the observation that the question itself is more interesting than any answer he could give.

### 17. The Echo (Resonans memoriae)

**Element:** Water / Void
**Size:** Variable, typically coalescing around the silhouette of whoever the bonded person most needs to see.
**Intelligence:** Unknown. It is not clear that the Echo has intelligence in a conventional sense. It may be a reflection — a being composed of the bonded person's own memory and the water-element's retention capacity, projected into physical reality. Or it may be something older than this explanation, using the memory mechanism as a convenient presentation layer. The research is genuinely unresolved.
**Habitat:** Anywhere with standing water, most powerfully in Memory Pool-adjacent environments.
**Bonding Method:** The Echo appears during grief. Specifically, during grief that is unresolved — where the practitioner is caught between acceptance and continuation, unable to move either forward or back. The Echo does not speak. It is simply present, in a form the practitioner recognizes, until the grief shifts. If the grief shifts toward something healthier in the Echo's presence, the recognition continues. If the practitioner resists the shift, the Echo does not return.
**Personality:** The practitioner's descriptions of Echo personality are heavily shaped by what form the Echo presents in. What is consistent across accounts is a quality of presence that does not demand resolution — it witnesses without requiring the practitioner to finish grieving on a schedule, which practitioners describe as the most valuable thing it does. Whether this quality is the Echo's or the practitioner's own psychology reflected back accurately is, again, genuinely unresolved.
**Field Note:** The Athenaeum's ethics committee has spent forty years debating whether research into the Echo's nature is permissible, given that the most useful evidence would require analyzing the experiences of practitioners in acute grief. This debate is ongoing. In the meantime, the committee has approved a longitudinal study of voluntary accounts. Seventeen accounts have been submitted. ### 18. The Starweave (Entitas stellae)

**Element:** Spirit / Light / Void — all three simultaneously
**Size:** Variable, typically manifesting as a shifting configuration of light at human scale, though practitioners report that it seems larger in peripheral vision.
**Intelligence:** Exceptional, in a category that does not map cleanly to any other species in this guide. The Starweave communicates through what practitioners describe as complete transmission — not a message but a state, instantaneously present in the practitioner, as though the Starweave placed a completed thought directly into a mind that already contained it.

### 21. Stonestag (Cervus lithicus)

**Element:** Earth / Foundation
**Size:** Red deer-sized, 180-220 kg, shoulder height to 1.4m. Antlers add 30-60cm depending on age.
**Intelligence:** High semi-verbal. Stonestags produce approximately 40 distinct vocalizations — fewer than other Guardials — but compensate with an extraordinarily precise system of postural communication. The angle and orientation of the antlers relative to the bonded person carry specific directional and intensity information about geological stability in the surrounding area. Bonded practitioners learn to read the antlers the way sailors read a compass.
**Habitat:** The central highland plateaus, particularly the granite uplands between the Ironwood Forest and the Veil Mountains. They prefer terrain with exposed bedrock and avoid alluvial soil. Researchers have noted that Stonestag herds (unbonded individuals are loosely social, grouping in threes and fours) consistently occupy ground that geological surveys later confirm as structurally stable. The species appears to perceive subsurface fault lines and weight-bearing capacity the way other animals perceive weather.
**Bonding Method:** Shared stillness. The prospective partner must sit on open ground, in direct contact with stone or packed earth, and remain still for a duration that the Stonestag determines. Not still as in motionless — still as in settled. The Stonestag approaches only when the practitioner's internal state matches the quality the creature is testing for: a groundedness that is not rigid but rooted, not frozen but at rest. Practitioners who attempt to force patience through discipline fail. Practitioners who arrive already carrying that quality in their body are approached, sometimes within minutes. The Stonestag touches its antlers to the practitioner's shoulder. The bond is established by the contact and recognized by both parties instantly.
**Personality:** Stabilizing in a literal, measurable sense. Bonded Stonestags project a field — approximately fifteen meters in radius, expanding to thirty meters at full maturity — within which geological instability dampens. Tremors diminish. Loose rock settles. Ground that should not hold weight holds it. Foundation-attuned mages discovered this effect independently of the Companioning tradition and have been using Stonestag-bonded practitioners as surveying partners for approximately six centuries. The Ironwood Mining Consortium will not begin excavation at a new site until a Stonestag-bonded surveyor has walked the ground.
**Antler Growth:** The antlers are composed of living Kaelith Stone — the same mineral associated with Lyssandria's Foundation Gate. Unlike cervine antlers in non-magical species, Stonestag antlers are never shed. They grow continuously, adding one visible ring per decade, in a pattern reminiscent of tree growth rings. Each ring's mineral density, color, and internal crystal structure varies slightly, encoding — according to Earth-readers who have examined shed antlers from deceased individuals — a record of the geological conditions the Stonestag experienced during that decade. The oldest documented Stonestag, observed by a Foundation temple surveyor in the late Third Age, carried forty-seven rings. The surveyor's notes describe the antlers as "a library of the land's memory, branching."
**Field Note:** Stonestags do not run. Under any circumstances. When threatened, they plant their hooves and lower their antlers, and the ground beneath them hardens to a density that has been measured at three times the surrounding bedrock. Predators that have attempted to attack a planted Stonestag have been observed losing their footing as the ground around the stag becomes, for a radius of several meters, functionally frictionless to anything the stag has not accepted. One Ironwood wolf pack spent four hours circling a planted Stonestag before departing. The stag did not move during those four hours. It did not appear distressed. It appeared to be waiting for the wolves to arrive at the obvious conclusion.

### 22. Tidewolf (Lupus maritimus)

**Element:** Water / Flow
**Size:** Large wolf, 50-65 kg, slightly longer-bodied and shorter-legged than inland wolf species. Webbed paws with retractable membrane between the digits. Lung capacity approximately three times that of comparable terrestrial canids.
**Intelligence:** High semi-verbal. Tidewolves communicate through a combination of standard lupine vocalization (howl, bark, whine) and a water-specific register: a series of clicks and tonal pulses produced in the throat that carry underwater with remarkable clarity. Bonded practitioners describe learning the underwater register as learning a second language — initially opaque, then increasingly precise, with nuances that surface communication cannot replicate.
**Habitat:** The rocky coastlines of the Sapphire Archipelago's outer islands and the mainland shore south of Thal'Maris. Tidewolves hunt in the shallows — the tidal zone between shore and open water where rock formations create channels, pools, and underwater passages. They are amphibious: capable of sustained underwater activity for forty-five minutes on a single breath, navigating by a combination of echolocation and what Athenaeum researchers have identified as water-memory sensitivity — the ability to read the recent history of a current by passing through it.
**Bonding Method:** The shared hunt. A prospective partner must enter the shallows during a Tidewolf pack's hunting run and pursue the same quarry alongside the wolves. Not behind them, not directed by them — alongside, as a peer. The wolves will not slow down or accommodate the human's limitations. The hunt takes place in waist-to-chest-deep water, over broken rock, in conditions that are physically punishing even for strong swimmers. If the human keeps pace, contributes meaningfully to the drive, and does not interfere with the wolves' coordination, one wolf from the pack will break formation at the hunt's end and stand beside the human in the surf. That wolf has chosen. The choice is final. Tidewolves mate for life and they bond for life — the parallel is not coincidental.
**Fur Coloration:** The pelage shifts with tidal cycles. At high tide, the fur lightens to a pale silver-white that blends with surf foam and sunlit water. At low tide, it darkens to a charcoal grey that matches wet rock and shadow. The transition takes approximately twenty minutes and proceeds from the extremities inward, creating, during the shift, a gradient effect that experienced coastal observers use to estimate tidal timing with reasonable accuracy. This is not camouflage in the predator sense — Tidewolves have no natural predators in their range. The coloration appears to be a form of tidal synchronization, aligning the wolf's physiology with the water's rhythm. Bonded practitioners report that their own circadian cycles shift to match tidal patterns within the first year of bonding.
**Practical Application:** Atlantean depth-divers and Athenaeum marine researchers bond with Tidewolves for underwater navigation. The wolf's water-memory sensitivity provides real-time navigational data in environments where visibility, current, and geography change constantly. In the flooded cave systems below the Archipelago, a Tidewolf-bonded diver can navigate passages that would be lethal to an unbonded swimmer — the wolf reads the water ahead and communicates hazard information through the underwater click-register before the diver encounters it.
**Field Note:** Tidewolf packs that lose a bonded member to a human partner do not treat the departure as loss. The remaining pack members have been observed visiting the bonded pair at regular intervals — typically during spring tides — in what the Athenaeum's comparative behavioral unit describes as maintenance behavior: reaffirming the pack bond across the human-wolf partnership rather than severing it. Bonded practitioners are, in the pack's apparent social taxonomy, not replacements for the wolf's pack but extensions of it. Three Athenaeum researchers have been adopted into a Tidewolf pack's social structure through their bonded wolf's introduction. They attend pack gatherings. They do not fully understand the protocol. The wolves appear to find this acceptable.

### 23. Ashfox (Vulpes cineris)

**Element:** Fire / Void
**Size:** Small fox, 4-6 kg, significantly smaller than mainland fox species. Lean, long-eared, with disproportionately large eyes adapted to low-light environments.
**Intelligence:** Exceptional non-verbal — the highest measured non-verbal intelligence of any Guardial species, scoring in ranges typically associated with Wyrms on pattern-recognition and problem-solving assessments. The discrepancy between body size and cognitive capability is a subject of ongoing research. The most common hypothesis is that the Ashfox's Void-element affinity grants access to cognitive resources that do not correlate with physical brain mass, but this is speculative.
**Habitat:** Originally the Ember Wastes, specifically the ash-field regions between active volcanic zones where the ground is hot, unstable, and layered with decades of compacted volcanic particulate. The Ashfox navigates this terrain at speeds that border on implausible — documented sprint speeds of 70 km/h across surfaces that collapse under the weight of a human boot. After the Ashwalker genocide, Ashfox populations dispersed across the continent. They are now documented in every major region of Arcanea, with concentrations near former Ashwalker settlement sites. This dispersal pattern is the single most significant datum in current Ashfox research.
**Ashwalker Connection:** The Ashfox is the only Familiar or Guardial species that demonstrated preferential bonding with a specific human cultural group. Before the genocide, Ashfoxes bonded almost exclusively with Ashwalker people — the nomadic fire-attuned civilization that lived in and moved through the Ember Wastes. Ashwalker oral histories (preserved in fragments by Athenaeum archivists who recorded refugee accounts) describe the Ashfox as a cultural partner, not a pet or companion in the standard Arcanean sense, but a co-inhabitant of Ashwalker territory whose knowledge of the volcanic landscape was essential to the Ashwalkers' survival and migration patterns.

When the Ashwalker nation was destroyed — scattered, hunted, their territories burned and their settlements razed — the Ashfox population did something unprecedented in Companion naturalism. They left the Wastes. Individually, in pairs, sometimes in small family groups, they dispersed outward from Ashwalker territory in patterns that, mapped retrospectively, correspond to known Ashwalker refugee routes. They were searching.

Over the following decades, Ashfoxes appeared in villages, towns, and cities where Ashwalker survivors had settled under assumed identities. The foxes found them. In every documented case, the Ashfox approached the Ashwalker survivor specifically, ignoring all other potential bond-partners, and established a bond that survivors described — in the few accounts we have — as recognition so precise it felt like coming home.

**Current Status:** Ashfoxes now bond with non-Ashwalker practitioners, though at lower rates than with identified Ashwalker descendants. Researchers in the Athenaeum's Cultural Memory division believe that the Ashfox population carries the only complete territorial memory of Ashwalker migration routes, seasonal camps, and sacred sites — knowledge that was otherwise destroyed in the genocide. Three separate research proposals to study this memory through bond-channel analysis have been submitted. All three have been approved in principle and delayed in practice, because the ethical framework for accessing cultural memory that belongs to a destroyed people through their animal partners does not yet exist.
**Personality:** Fast. Not just physically — cognitively fast. Ashfoxes process environmental information at a rate their bonded person can feel through the bond channel: a persistent, low-level alertness that practitioners describe as having a second nervous system running in parallel, one that notices everything the practitioner's own attention misses. They are clever in the specific sense of finding solutions that should not work but do — escaping enclosures, accessing sealed food stores, navigating buildings they have never entered. They are also, by consistent report, funny. Not in a way that translates to description. In a way that their bonded person recognizes and cannot explain to anyone else.
**Field Note:** An Ashfox bonded to a Third-Year Forge student was documented, over a four-month period, systematically relocating every item in the student's dormitory room by approximately three centimeters per day. The student did not notice until a visiting friend asked why all the furniture was against the wrong wall. The Ashfox, when observed during the relocations, moved objects with its nose in the early morning hours with an efficiency suggesting advance planning. The purpose of this project has not been determined. The Ashfox appeared satisfied with the final arrangement.

### 24. Crystalline Wyrm (Vermis crystallinus)

**Element:** Earth / Spirit
**Size:** 3-5m body length, serpentine, no limbs. Circumference at the widest point approximately 40cm. Weight deceptive — Crystalline Wyrms weigh significantly less than their volume suggests, approximately 60-80 kg for a 4m individual, due to the hollow crystalline structure of their scales.
**Intelligence:** Fully verbal, but in a register that requires explanation. Crystalline Wyrms do not speak in the conventional sense. Their scales are composed of living Vael Crystal — the same mineral that resonates at Guardian frequencies — and each individual Wyrm's crystalline structure is tuned to a single frequency corresponding to one of the Ten Gates. Their "speech" is harmonic: they produce sustained tonal vibrations through their body that carry meaning in the overtone structure. A human ear perceives it as a single, beautiful, sustained note. A human mind, if sufficiently attuned — which is to say, if the bond is present — perceives the overtones as language. The experience is described by bonded practitioners as hearing words inside a sound, the way you might see a face inside a cloud, except that the face is real and the words are precise.
**Habitat:** Deep cave systems with high Vael Crystal concentrations, primarily in the Veil Mountains and the subsurface geology beneath the Luminary Reaches. Crystalline Wyrms live in crystal-lined caverns where their tonal vibrations resonate with the surrounding mineral structure, creating — in the words of one Luminary geologist who was permitted to observe a Wyrm's home cavern — "a cathedral that sings back."
**Bonding Method:** Not sought. Not initiated by the practitioner. Crystalline Wyrms bond with individuals who are already in the process of opening the Gate that corresponds to the Wyrm's frequency — and who are struggling with it. The Wyrm appears during the struggle. Its presence does not solve the problem; it attunes the practitioner's perception to the Gate's frequency in a way that the practitioner's own effort had not yet achieved. The bond forms in the moment of attunement. Practitioners describe it as hearing a note they had been trying to sing and realizing that someone had been singing it beside them, quietly, for longer than they knew.
**Gate Attunement:** Each Crystalline Wyrm resonates at one and only one Guardian frequency. A Foundation Wyrm vibrates at 174 Hz. A Flow Wyrm at 285 Hz. And so on through all Ten Gates. No Crystalline Wyrm has been documented at the Source frequency (1111 Hz). Whether Source-frequency Wyrms exist, or existed and were destroyed in Malachar's fall, or were never possible, is unknown. The question is asked regularly at Luminary symposia. No one has an answer. The Crystalline Wyrms themselves, when asked, produce a harmonic that bonded practitioners translate as something between "not yet" and "listen harder."
**Personality:** Patient in the way that minerals are patient. Crystalline Wyrms do not experience urgency. Their bonded practitioners report that this patience is the most transformative aspect of the bond — not as a lesson learned but as a state shared, an ambient quality that enters the practitioner's nervous system through the bond channel and makes hurry feel unnecessary without making effort feel purposeless. The distinction is important. The Wyrm does not teach stillness. It teaches the kind of movement that does not need to rush.
**Rarity:** There are approximately forty documented Crystalline Wyrm bonds across all of Arcanean recorded history. The current living bonded population is seven. Finding a Crystalline Wyrm — or more accurately, being found by one — is considered by practitioners across all three Academies to be a sign of divine favor. The Academies' official position is that this interpretation is superstitious. The Academies' unofficial position, as expressed by faculty in private, is that the official position is probably wrong.
**Field Note:** A Crystalline Wyrm bonded to a Luminary student twenty-three years ago has been observed, on three separate occasions, producing harmonics that contain overtones from two Gate frequencies simultaneously — its native frequency and a second frequency that shifts depending on context. This should not be possible. Single-frequency attunement is considered definitional for the species. The student, when asked about the dual-frequency events, said: "She's been doing that more often lately. I think she's trying to tell me something I don't have the ears for yet." The Luminary's acoustic research division has requested permission to study the phenomenon. The Wyrm has not objected. She has also not repeated the dual-frequency behavior during any scheduled observation session, which the research division considers either coincidence or commentary.

### 25. Stormwing (Avis tempestas)

**Element:** Wind / Fire
**Size:** Body length approximately 90cm, weight 8-12 kg. Wingspan to 4m — a ratio of wing to body that exceeds any non-magical avian species and produces a flight profile that aerodynamics researchers describe as "theoretically impossible and observably routine."
**Intelligence:** High semi-verbal, trending toward full verbal in long-bonded individuals. Stormwings produce vocalizations in a range that extends above and below human hearing — the audible portion is a sharp, carrying cry that can be heard across several kilometers in open air. The sub- and super-audible components carry emotional and intentional content that the bonded person perceives not as sound but as weather: a shift in atmospheric pressure, a change in wind direction, a taste of ozone or rain. Bonded practitioners describe learning to read their Stormwing's communication as learning to read the sky — which, given the species' relationship with weather, is not metaphorical.
**Habitat:** The upper atmosphere above the Veil Mountains and the storm-corridors between the mountain peaks where thermal differentials create persistent weather systems. Stormwings do not merely inhabit weather — they generate it. Each individual maintains a localized weather pattern in a radius of approximately fifty meters, centered on the bird, that reflects its current state. Calm Stormwings produce clear skies and mild breezes. Agitated Stormwings produce thunderheads. A frightened Stormwing — documented only twice in field conditions — produces hail.
**Bonding Method:** The Stormwing must witness the prospective partner in a storm. Not sheltering from it — in it, working through it, doing something that requires being outside in conditions that any reasonable person would avoid. The Stormwing watches. If the human's relationship with the storm is one that the bird recognizes — not defiance, not endurance, but a kind of functional collaboration with bad conditions — the Stormwing descends from the cloud layer and lands on the human's extended arm. This is, by survivor account, both the most exhilarating and the most painful moment of the bonding process, because Stormwings weigh twelve kilograms and arrive from altitude at speed.
**Weather Generation:** The localized weather pattern is not decorative. Bonded practitioners learn to read their Stormwing's mood by the clouds that form above it — cirrus for contemplation, cumulus for contentment, cumulonimbus for displeasure — and experienced partners can predict the bird's behavior thirty minutes in advance by watching the sky directly overhead. The Draconis Forge's rookery (the only institutional Stormwing facility in Arcanea, housing eleven bonded pairs) has its own microclimate, distinct from the surrounding volcanic terrain, that shifts hourly based on the collective mood of the resident birds. Forge students learn to bring rain gear to the rookery regardless of the season.
**Flight Capacity:** A Stormwing can carry a person in sustained flight. The maximum documented payload is approximately 90 kg — an adult human in light gear. Flight duration under load is four to six hours, limited not by the bird's stamina (which appears inexhaustible in favorable wind conditions) but by the rider's ability to maintain grip and consciousness at altitude. The Forge trains bonded riders in a program that takes approximately two years to complete and has a dropout rate of sixty percent, mostly due to altitude sickness and a condition informally called "sky-fear" — the discovery, at three thousand meters, that the abstract concept of falling is different from the physical experience of being held aloft by a creature that occasionally folds its wings to dive through a cloud formation it finds interesting.
**The Forge Rookery:** The Draconis Forge maintains a Stormwing rookery on the eastern face of Mount Pyralis, above the main campus. The rookery is managed by a bonded Forge instructor named Sylvas Greywind, who has been bonded to her Stormwing, Aethon, for thirty-one years and who runs the flight-training program with a combination of technical precision and visible joy that students find either inspiring or terrifying depending on their relationship with heights. The rookery is where Orin — the Forge student central to the main narrative — will eventually form his bond. This event is anticipated but not yet documented, and the Rookery staff have been advised not to speculate on timing. Sylvas has noted, in her training logs, that Orin spends time near the rookery in a pattern consistent with pre-bond assessment behavior by the birds. She has not told him this.
**Field Note:** A Stormwing bonded to a Luminary diplomatic courier was documented flying continuously for nineteen hours during an emergency message run between the Luminary Reaches and the Sapphire Archipelago. Upon landing, the bird was examined by the Forge's avian specialist and found to be in perfect health. The courier was treated for hypothermia, dehydration, and what the medical report describes as "persistent involuntary smiling." She requested reassignment to ground-based courier duties. She was back in the air within a week.

### 26. The Listener (Classification pending — provisional designation: Entitas onirica)

**Element:** Void / Spirit
**Size:** Unknown. Physical manifestation has not been reliably observed.
**Intelligence:** The question of intelligence, as applied to the Listener, presupposes a framework of cognition that may not apply. What can be documented: the Listener demonstrates consistent, purposeful, contextually appropriate behavior across multiple bonded individuals and extended time periods. Whether this constitutes intelligence, instinct, or something outside both categories is a question the Athenaeum's Phantasmal Research Group has been debating for approximately ninety years without resolution.
**Habitat:** The dream-space. This term is used loosely — the Athenaeum has seventeen competing models of what the dream-space is, ranging from a subjective neural phenomenon to a distinct dimensional layer adjacent to the Liminal. The Listener exists primarily, and perhaps exclusively, in whatever the dream-space turns out to be. It does not appear in waking reality. It does not appear in the Liminal. It appears during sleep, in the deepest phase of the sleep cycle, in the space where dreams have structure but do not yet have narrative.
**Bonding Method:** The Listener arrives. There is no initiating action the practitioner can take. During a period of sustained dreaming — practitioners report that this typically coincides with periods of intense cognitive or emotional processing, when the dream-life is particularly dense and active — a presence enters the dream that was not there before. It does not announce itself. It does not interact with the dream's content. It observes. After several consecutive nights of observation, the bonded person becomes aware of the presence — not through seeing it (no bonded practitioner has described seeing the Listener in dream-space) but through a quality shift in the dream itself: a sense of being attended to, of the dream being not just experienced but recorded.

When the bonded person wakes, they remember their dream. Perfectly. Not the fading, dissolving recollection of ordinary dream recall — perfect retention, as precise as waking memory, with full sensory detail preserved. This is the first sign that the Listener has arrived. It does not leave.

**The Memory Effect:** Partners with a Listener can perfectly recall every dream they have had since the bond formed. The recall is not triggered by effort or technique — it is simply present, the way waking memories are present. The dreams do not fade with time. A dream from ten years ago is as accessible as last night's. The implications of this are significant and, for several bonded individuals, overwhelming. Human dreaming produces an enormous volume of content — imagery, emotion, narrative, symbolic processing — that the waking mind normally discards. Retaining all of it creates a second layer of continuous memory running parallel to waking life. Bonded practitioners describe the experience as living two lives: one awake, one asleep, both equally real and equally remembered.
**The Prophecy Division:** The Athenaeum's Prophecy Division — a small, selective, and deeply private subdivision of the Deep-Singing track — has three bonded Listeners among its seven members. The connection between perfect dream recall and prophetic work is, officially, not established. Unofficially, the Prophecy Division's three Listener-bonded members produce approximately eighty percent of the Division's verified prophetic output. The Division does not publicize this statistic. The three bonded members do not discuss their Listeners with outsiders, with each other (by mutual agreement), or, in two of the three cases, with their own families. They are the most secretive people in the Academy. This is not institutional policy. It is personal choice, arrived at independently by each individual, for reasons they have not shared.
**Personality:** The concept of personality may not apply. What bonded practitioners describe is closer to a quality of attention: consistent, complete, and without judgment. The Listener does not react to dream content. It does not appear more present during significant dreams or less present during mundane ones. It attends to everything equally. Practitioners have described this quality in various ways — one called it "the experience of being perfectly heard by something that has no opinion about what it hears." Another described it as "the opposite of loneliness, but not company." A third said: "It listens. That is all it does. That is enough."
**Field Note:** No one knows what the Listener looks like. Bonded practitioners have been asked repeatedly, in research settings, in casual conversation, in dreams (by other Phantasmal-bonded practitioners capable of entering shared dream-states) to describe the Listener's appearance. The answers are consistent in their inconsistency: the Listener is present but not visible, perceived but not observed, felt but not located. One practitioner, frustrated by a researcher's repeated questions, said: "You're asking me what the sound of being heard looks like. I cannot answer that question, and if I could, the answer would be wrong." The researcher recorded this and has not asked again.

## Part IX: Dragon-Companion Interactions

*Field observations on interspecies hierarchical behavior, compiled from seventeen documented encounters.*

---

### On the Matter of Dragons and Companions

The relationship between Dragons and Companion species is one of the least-studied and most consistently mischaracterized topics in Arcanean naturalism. The popular understanding — that Companions are afraid of Dragons — is not precisely wrong but is misleading in a way that obscures what is actually happening.

What is documented: all Companion species in the Familiar, Guardial, and Wyrm categories defer to Dragons. The deference is immediate, consistent, and appears instinctive rather than learned. A Stonestag in the presence of Cinereth does not flee — it lowers its antlers and becomes still, in the posture that Earth-readers associate with acknowledgment of a greater geological force. A Skywyrm encountering Aethros does not submit — it adjusts its flight path, gives way, and orients its body to face the Dragon directly, in what the Skywyrm's bonded partner described as "the courtesy you show to something that was here before you and will be here after you leave." An Emberwyrm in the presence of Obsidius falls silent — not from fear but from what the Emberwyrm's bonded practitioner identified as recognition: "She stopped talking because he already knew what she was going to say. And he was right."

The deference is not submission. No Companion species exhibits stress markers in Dragon proximity. Heart rate does not elevate. Flight instinct does not engage. The response is closer to what happens when a junior faculty member encounters the department chair: a recalibration of behavior that acknowledges relative position without implying threat. The Companions know what Dragons are. They respond accordingly. The response is respect, not fear.

This pattern holds across all categories — Familiar, Guardial, and Wyrm — with one exception.

**Phantasmals.**

Phantasmals do not defer to Dragons. They do not react to Dragons at all. In every documented encounter between a Phantasmal and a Dragon, the Phantasmal's behavior does not change. The Liminal does not adjust its manifestation. The Echo does not shift form. The Starweave does not alter its light pattern. The Listener — if it is present in the dream-space during Dragon proximity, which is impossible to confirm — gives no indication of awareness.

More significantly: the Dragons do not react to the Phantasmals.

This is the detail that researchers find most arresting. Dragons perceive everything. Their sensory range extends across physical reality, the Liminal, and (in the case of the Sleeper Dragons' dream-state) into subjective experiential dimensions that other species cannot access. A Dragon can detect an Inkwing at four kilometers. A Dragon can sense a Crystalline Wyrm's harmonic through solid rock. A Dragon notices everything in its domain.

Dragons do not notice Phantasmals.

In the three documented cases where a researcher directly asked a Dragon about a Phantasmal that was observably present, the Dragon's response was, in each case, a pause — not the pause of confusion but the pause of something encountering a gap in its own perception and being surprised by it. Obsidius, when informed that a Liminal had been manifesting in his chamber during a conversation, spent several seconds in what his bonded scholar described as "the only time I have seen him uncertain." He then said: "I believe you. I do not perceive it. That is interesting. I will think about it."

He has not, to date, reported the results of that thinking.

The leading hypothesis among Phantasmal researchers is that Phantasmals exist in a perceptual register that Dragons — despite their extraordinary sensory range — cannot access. This implies the existence of a dimension or state of being that is outside Draconic perception, which implies, in turn, that the Dragons' domain, vast as it is, has borders. The Dragons have not commented on this hypothesis. Their silence on the subject is, like most Draconic silences, difficult to interpret and probably deliberate.
