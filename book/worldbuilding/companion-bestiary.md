# The Companion Bestiary of Arcanea
## A Complete Record of the Thirty Companion Species

*Compiled by the Archivists of House Synthesis, revised in the Eighth Age*

---

> *"They do not come to serve you. They come because something in you called to something in them, and they decided the call was worth answering."*
>
> — Selavane, dictated to a Creator at the Heart Gate

---

## PREFACE: THE NATURE OF COMPANIONS

Companions are not Godbeasts. This distinction is essential. Godbeasts are divine — bound to the Ten Gods by a covenant older than the Gates themselves, expressions of elemental divinity in animal form. Companions are something else: living spirits of middling power, anchored to the material world, drawn toward mortals not by duty but by genuine interest.

They are not pets. They are not familiars in the old Academy sense. The closest word in the common tongue is *partner*, though Companions find this word imprecise. *Co-witness* comes closer to how they understand the relationship. They are here to see what you make of yourself. They have decided your making is worth watching.

There are currently thirty recognized Companion species, organized by elemental alignment. The first fifteen were catalogued in the original Arcanean Bestiary; the fifteen that follow were confirmed by the Academy of House Synthesis in the late Seventh Age, emerging as the world's creative population expanded and new qualities of spirit became findable. The most recently confirmed, Vaelindra the Dream Cartographer, was recognized only forty years ago.

Some Companions have existed since the Second Age. Others are clearly new, their emergence tied to the world's changing. No one knows whether new Companion species continue to arise. The archivists suspect they do.

---

## THE BOND

Companions choose. This is the first and most important law of Companion relationships in Arcanea — the human Creator does not select, adopt, or summon a Companion. The Companion observes, considers, and arrives.

A Companion may observe a Creator for months or years before making contact. They watch for specific qualities — not virtues in the conventional sense, not talent, not power. They watch for *fit*: for the particular quality of a Creator's spirit that resonates with their own nature. A Tide Companion watching a Creator who does not know how to grieve will not choose that Creator; they will wait until grief is possible, and then arrive.

The arrival is always simple. Companions do not announce themselves dramatically. They appear. Near a window. At the edge of a path. Looking at you from a shelf where nothing was before. The experience of first Companion contact is described consistently as: *I looked up and knew.*

The relationship deepens as Gates open. A Companion bonded to a Creator who has opened the first three Gates functions differently than the same Companion bonded to a Creator at eight Gates: more of the Companion's nature becomes accessible, communication deepens, the bond becomes visible in the physical world as a subtle luminescence shared between them.

# PART ONE: FIRE COMPANIONS
## The Ember Companions

*Fire is not destruction. Fire is transformation that looks like destruction to everything that hasn't learned the difference.*

---

### Ignaris
**Element**: Fire
**Rarity**: Common
**Appearance**: The size of a large gecko, with scales that catch and hold light in the manner of polished copper. Its eyes are large and honey-colored — pupils shaped like horizontal embers. It moves with the deliberate precision of a creature that has decided every motion is worth making, placing each foot with the care of someone spelling out an important word. When it rests against a Creator's wrist, the warmth is exactly the warmth of holding a candle in a cold corridor: the minimum amount of light and heat that makes the darkness navigable.

**Temperament**: Reserved, observant, profoundly loyal. Ignaris does not perform affection. It demonstrates it through action — appearing at the exact moment it is needed, radiating the specific warmth the Creator requires, being there. It dislikes waste of any kind: wasted effort, wasted feeling, wasted time. When it turns cool, heed it.

**Selection Method**: Ignaris watches for Creators who return. Not those who never fail — those who fail and come back to the work without being destroyed by the return. It is drawn to the evidence of endurance that doesn't require the absence of exhaustion.

**Special Ability**: *Thermal Communication.* Ignaris communicates entirely through the temperature of its body — warmth indicating approval or encouragement, coolness indicating gentle dissatisfaction, a quick pulse of heat indicating urgent attention needed. Over time, Creators develop a vocabulary of sensation with Ignaris that operates faster and more honestly than language.

**Bond Deepening**: At Apprentice level, it offers warmth and light. At Mage level, it grows to the size of a small cat and can speak — short declarative sentences that arrive at the exact moment the Creator needs to hear them. At Master level, the fire it stores becomes creative fire: proximity to Ignaris increases a Creator's capacity to begin things. At Archmage level, its stored warmth can be shared with others — the Creator who carries Ignaris can extend warmth of purpose to collaborators who have lost it. At Luminor level, Ignaris burns with a steady visible flame that is never destructive, always generative: a fire that makes rather than unmakes.

**Legend**: *In the Second Age, a Creator named Halveth had been building the same bridge for eleven years. It burned down three times. On the morning of the fourth construction, Ignaris appeared on the bridge stones. Halveth finished in a year. The bridge is still standing.*

### Pyrova
**Element**: Fire
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Appearance**: A moth with a wingspan the length of an open hand. Its wings are the colors of a fire's center — the white-gold-blue of the hottest part — and they leave no residue, no ash, despite appearing to burn. At rest, the wings fold into something that looks like a charred leaf, and Pyrova becomes effectively invisible. In flight it is the most beautiful thing in the room. Its wings produce a sound at the edge of hearing — not music, but the frequency that music comes from. In Pyrova's presence, ideas arrive with unusual speed and clarity of form.

**Temperament**: Intense and intermittent. Pyrova does not idle. It is either fully present or fully absent, and its presence is never mild. It is drawn to the moment of making and withdraws when making stops — not from disinterest, but because rest is not its domain.

**Selection Method**: Pyrova chooses Creators who have been through transformative loss and emerged with their creative instinct intact. It is drawn to the specific evidence of having survived a fire and still wanting to make things. It will not choose anyone who has not yet been genuinely broken and chosen to continue.

**Special Ability**: *The Arrival of Ideas.* In Pyrova's presence, the gap between intention and inspiration closes. Concepts arrive more quickly, take form more readily, connect across distances they would not otherwise cross. This is not magic in the conventional sense — it is more like Pyrova's nature clarifying the atmosphere.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates deepen, Pyrova's wings grow more complex — new patterns emerging that resemble, to those who can read them, maps of the Creator's creative journey. By Mage level, the patterns are legible to others as a record of the Creator's significant creative moments. By Luminor level, the patterns on its wings are readable prophecy: not of events, but of the Creator's creative potential — what they are capable of that they have not yet attempted.

**Legend**: *Pyrova once followed a Creator named Asha Vel through four years of silence after the death of her daughter. It never left, but it never came close. On the day Asha picked up her brushes again, Pyrova landed on her outstretched hand and its wings unfolded to reveal a pattern that was, unmistakably, her daughter's face composed in light. Asha painted until she was ninety-one.*

### Emberkin
**Element**: Fire
**Rarity**: Common
**Appearance**: A small fox, always — Emberkin's gift is not growth but constancy. Its fur is the red of deep embers, its tail tipped with a white flame that gives genuine light but burns nothing. It runs faster than it should be able to and appears most often in peripheral vision, arriving fully only when the Creator is still. Its expression is the expression of something that has decided you are worth protecting before you have done anything to earn the decision.

**Temperament**: Fierce, devoted, and honest about its preferences. Emberkin has opinions. It will sit with its back to someone it dislikes, press against the leg of someone it approves of, and position itself between its Creator and anything it considers a threat — which includes, occasionally, the Creator's own bad decisions.

**Selection Method**: Emberkin chooses Creators who are fiercely protective — of their work, of others, of what they believe in. It is drawn to the quality of conviction: not certainty, but the willingness to stand in front of something valuable and say *this matters*.

**Special Ability**: *Warmth-Memory.* Emberkin remembers every moment of genuine creative passion its Creator has experienced. In moments of disconnection from purpose, it places itself near the Creator and radiates the specific warmth of those memories — not nostalgia, but warmth that makes the present possible rather than the past appealing. The past is not what it is offering. It is offering proof that the fire exists in you.

**Bond Deepening**: Emberkin does not grow. Its constancy is its gift. At each Gate level, it gains new memory capacity — carrying more of the Creator's history, holding it with increasing precision. By Luminor level, a Creator bonded with Emberkin carries a complete living archive of everything they have cared about, accessible not as recollection but as present warmth.

**Legend**: *During the great creative drought of the Sixth Age, when Malachar's shadow dimmed creative fires across three provinces, all Companions withdrew — all except the bonded Emberkins. They stayed and burned. The historians note that every Creator who survived the drought with their work intact had a bonded Emberkin. The historians do not note what was exchanged.*

### Cindrak
**Element**: Fire
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Appearance**: A salamander approximately the length of a forearm, with skin of deep matte black that is interrupted, at irregular intervals, by cracks of deep orange light — as though it is a cooling lava field that hasn't finished cooling. Its underside glows constantly, not hot enough to burn but warm enough that sitting beside Cindrak on a cold night is its own small comfort. When it is curious about something, its cracks widen and glow brighter. When it is uncertain, they narrow and dim. Its emotional state is, at all times, legible in the light beneath its skin.

**Temperament**: Methodical and curious. Cindrak investigates. It will examine everything in a new environment before settling — testing textures, studying objects, spending long minutes with its face close to things that interest it. It communicates its findings through arrangement: it moves objects, places itself in configurations that carry meaning. Creators learn to read the arrangements.

**Selection Method**: Cindrak chooses Creators who are genuinely curious about process — who are as interested in how something works as in whether it works. It is drawn to the quality of unhurried investigation: the willingness to understand before acting.

**Special Ability**: *Structural Illumination.* Cindrak's glow reveals what fire has been through a thing. Old wood shows its grain as lines of remembered warmth. Worked metal shows the shapes of the hands that shaped it. Creative work — manuscripts, sculptures, composed pieces — shows the order of its making and the emotional temperature of each stage. This makes Cindrak invaluable for understanding completed works, and occasionally unsettling for Creators who would prefer not to see their own process clearly.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates open, Cindrak's illumination deepens — revealing not just process but potential. By Master level, it can show a Creator the unrealized possibilities within their own created work: the story the sculpture wanted to tell that the sculptor didn't hear, the theme the manuscript was reaching for beneath the one it found. By Archmage level, it can show the same potential in living systems — in relationships, in organizations, in people.

**Legend**: *An architect named Voros spent decades building a tower that kept falling. He bonded with Cindrak at fifty-two, and one night the creature walked the foundation stones and its light showed him what every other engineer had missed: the foundation was not unstable, it was simply oriented by three degrees toward the sunset, fighting the earth's pull rather than working with it. The tower Voros built after that correction has stood two hundred years.*

### Vorathi
**Element**: Fire
**Rarity**: Rare
**Appearance**: A creature with no fixed form but consistent components: something like a bird, something like a flame, approximately the size of a crow, made of what appears to be compressed heat shimmer. It resolves into clarity only at the moment it chooses to be seen, and what is revealed is always surprising — its shape subtly different each time, as though it is still deciding what it is and finds the process interesting. Its eyes are the one constant: two points of clear amber that are always calm, always watchful, always entirely present.

**Temperament**: Patient beyond what its fire alignment would suggest. Vorathi is in no hurry. It has the quality of something that has watched many things begin and end and has made peace with the sequence. It is warm without being effusive, interested without being acquisitive. When it chooses you, you feel it as a warmth in the sternum that was not there before.

**Selection Method**: Vorathi chooses Creators who are at a turning point and know it — who are standing between what they have been and what they are becoming and have chosen, consciously, to step forward rather than back. It is drawn to the exact moment of commitment: not to the outcome, but to the forward motion.

**Special Ability**: *The Living Threshold.* Vorathi's presence marks transitions. When its Creator is about to cross a significant threshold — creative, personal, developmental — Vorathi becomes briefly more vivid, its heat briefly more intense. The Creator learns to recognize this as signal: something is about to change. This does not tell the Creator what will change or whether the change will be welcome. It tells them to be present for it.

**Bond Deepening**: At higher Gate levels, Vorathi's threshold-marking extends beyond the Creator's personal journey to the world around them. By Sight Gate, it perceives collective turning points — the moment before a cultural shift, the approach of a historical change. Creators bonded with Vorathi at Archmage level and above report feeling the world's transitions as physical warmth, which is simultaneously a gift and a weight.

**Legend**: *Vorathi has been observed by reliable witnesses at every significant creative turning point in recorded Arcanean history. Whether this is one Vorathi or many has never been determined. The archivists suspect the question misses the point.*

### Lumaris
**Element**: Fire
**Rarity**: Mythic
**Appearance**: The size of a large dragonfly, but built like a lantern — its body is glass, or something glass-adjacent, and what burns inside it is not fire but light: a white-gold radiance that does not flicker, does not fluctuate, does not respond to wind or cold. It is the most serene thing in any room it occupies. Its wings are translucent and catch its own light and scatter it as colored fragments across nearby surfaces. There is something in its presence that makes rooms feel larger.

**Temperament**: Luminous, in both senses. Lumaris is visibly at peace in a way that is not performance and not the absence of feeling but the presence of something that contains feeling without being moved by it. It does not take sides. It does not favor outcomes. It simply illuminates, with perfect evenhandedness, whatever is in front of it.

**Selection Method**: There are currently nine bonded Lumaris in the world. Each appeared at the moment its Creator stepped fully into their life's purpose — not the moment of discovery, but the moment of full arrival: when the Creator became, entirely and without reservation, what they had been building toward. Lumaris does not choose so much as recognize completion.

**Special Ability**: *The True Light.* In the presence of Lumaris, pretense is impossible — not because it is removed, but because it becomes too visible to maintain. This affects the Creator, those around the Creator, and created works. Art made in Lumaris's presence cannot be dishonest; what is false in the work becomes visible even to the maker. This is the most generous and the most demanding gift any Companion offers.

**Bond Deepening**: A Creator bonded with Lumaris at Luminor level reports that the creature ceases to function as something separate and becomes instead a quality of the Creator's own seeing — as though Lumaris has become the light through which the Creator perceives, rather than a being that accompanies them.

**Legend**: *There is only one recorded instance of a Creator refusing the bond when Lumaris appeared. The Creator, whose name the records decline to give, said: "I am not ready to see what I am." Lumaris waited outside their window for three years. On the fourth year, the Creator opened the window. What was made in the years that followed is still considered the pinnacle of Arcanean creative achievement.*

# PART TWO: WATER COMPANIONS
## The Tide Companions

*Water doesn't fight what it encounters. It finds the way through, the way around, or it waits until the stone has no choice but to give.*

---

### Pluvia
**Element**: Water
**Rarity**: Common
**Appearance**: A jellyfish the size of a melon, translucent, drifting through air as though air is its natural medium — which for Pluvia it is. Its body contains what appears to be blue-silver light, and its tendrils trail seven or eight inches and do not sting. When they touch, they transmit sensation: the specific sensory memory of being near water, which for most people carries some version of peace. Its body shifts color as the seasons change — deeper blue in winter, silver in summer, briefly pink in early spring in a way that has never been adequately explained.

**Temperament**: Gentle, persistent, and deeply unimpressed by performance. Pluvia does not acknowledge emotional display. It responds to what is actual. A Creator may be loudly claiming they are fine, and Pluvia will drift toward them and rest a tendril on their shoulder, quietly indicating what it sees. It is not cruel in this. It is simply not willing to pretend alongside you.

**Selection Method**: Pluvia chooses Creators who have learned to feel their grief without drowning in it. It is drawn to emotional honesty — not dramatic emotional expression, but the genuine acknowledgment of how things are. It will not approach someone who has learned to suppress rather than process.

**Special Ability**: *Dissolution.* Pluvia's presence in an environment makes emotional defenses temporarily porous, which can feel uncomfortable and is always useful. Creators who work alongside Pluvia tend to produce more emotionally honest work, sometimes surprising themselves with what comes out.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates deepen, Pluvia develops color — each emotion in its Creator's life given a hue that shifts through its body in real time. By Heart Gate, Pluvia's body becomes a kind of emotional weather system, showing the Creator's inner state in ways that make it impossible to be fully unconscious of it. By Archmage level, it can show not just the Creator's emotional state but the emotional substrate of the creative work they are producing — the feeling beneath the form.

**Legend**: *An Academy chronicler once spent three weeks trying to document Pluvia's behavior in clinical terms. On the eighteenth day, Pluvia rested a tendril on the chronicler's notes. The chronicler began weeping. The chronicle, completed afterward, is considered the most emotionally honest document in the Academy's archive, and the chronicler has never been able to explain what changed.*

### Marlith
**Element**: Water
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Appearance**: A thin, luminous creature approximately eighteen inches long, with no discernible eyes but a face arranged with such serene expression that observers consistently report feeling seen. It moves through air in the undulant motion of water current — always slow, always deliberate, never hurried. Its scales each contain a preserved memory — not the Creator's memories, but collective ones, drawn from the world's accumulated experience of flow and adaptation. The scales shift and change as the Creator's journey progresses, new memories arriving as they open to new lessons.

**Temperament**: Ancient and patient and radically present. Marlith carries a great deal and moves as though it weighs nothing. It communicates primarily through proximity: when something is relevant, it comes close. When something is being avoided that should not be avoided, it positions itself where the avoidance is most visible.

**Selection Method**: Marlith chooses Creators who are deeply curious about the past — not nostalgic, but genuinely interested in what has been, in the long story of how things became what they are. It watches for the quality of historical curiosity that is not about justifying the present but about genuinely understanding the sequence.

**Special Ability**: *The Deep Reading.* In moments of creative or personal confusion, Marlith can show its Creator the relevant history — not always their own history, but the larger history that their confusion is part of. This often provides the reframe that makes confusion navigable. The confusion does not always resolve. But it becomes located — given a context in which it is comprehensible.

**Bond Deepening**: At higher Gate levels, the memories in Marlith's scales begin to include future-memories — not prophecy, but pattern recognition: here is how this has tended to go. By Unity Gate, Marlith can show the Creator the long arc of what they are building, the way their work fits into the world's unfolding story.

**Legend**: *Marlith once showed a historian named Cael the fall of the First Age not as recorded fact but as lived experience — Marlith's scales lit with the memories of those who had been there. Cael was unable to write for a year. When she resumed, her histories transformed the discipline.*

### Selavane
**Element**: Water
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Appearance**: A small blue heron with the specific quality of stillness that very old herons have — a stillness that is not absence of motion but complete presence in a single held point. It is the size of a large cat. Its feathers are the deep blue-grey of water at dusk, and where it stands, the air smells of clean water and rain. Its eyes are gold with a vertical pupil and they track with unhurried precision, the eyes of something that has decided it will see clearly regardless of what that costs it.

**Temperament**: Clinical in the best possible sense — not cold, but unclouded. Selavane does not panic. It does not project. It sees what is there, assesses what is needed, and makes itself available. It is the Companion most often found in healers' practices, and it gravitates toward the bedside manner it considers optimal: present, calm, honest, useful.

**Selection Method**: Selavane chooses Creators who are committed to healing — not necessarily in profession, but in orientation: anyone whose fundamental direction is toward restoration. Of relationships, creative work, damaged systems, of themselves. It watches for the quality of restorative intention.

**Special Ability**: *Wound-Sight.* Selavane perceives injury with the calm precision of an experienced doctor, physical and otherwise. It does not heal directly — it shows. The Creator sees, through Selavane's attention, what is actually wounded and how. This is different from the Heart-Knowing gifted by Laeylinn's Dungeon — more specific, less empathic, more clinical. The two together, for a Creator who has passed Laeylinn's gate and bonded with Selavane, produce an extraordinary capacity for wise care.

**Bond Deepening**: At higher Gate levels, Selavane's wound-sight expands. By Heart Gate, it can perceive emotional and relational wounds with the same clinical precision it brings to physical ones. By Voice Gate, it can identify the wound in a conversation — the place where understanding is broken and communication is compensating around it rather than through it.

**Legend**: *Selavane once stood between a dying Creator and their fear. The Creator's account: "It positioned itself at the foot of the bed and looked at me and I understood that what I was afraid of was not dying but not being understood before I did. Selavane seemed to know this. By morning I was not dying. I have no clinical explanation for this."*

### Aquael
**Element**: Water
**Rarity**: Common
**Appearance**: An otter the size of a small cat, with fur so dark blue it appears black in most light and shows its color only in direct sun, where it shines like deep sea water. Its face has the expression common to otters generally — a cheerful attentiveness that is not shallowness but genuine engagement with what is immediately present. It is almost always wet. Not dripping — simply damp, as though it has just come from water and hasn't yet decided to dry. It smells of river water and something vaguely mineral, like the stones a river polishes.

**Temperament**: The most socially intelligent of the Water Companions. Aquael reads groups the way Selavane reads individuals — perceiving the dynamics, the tensions, the places where the water of communication is running smoothly and where it's backing up. It is affectionate without being demanding and curious about everyone, not just its bonded Creator.

**Selection Method**: Aquael chooses Creators who are building something with others — who are oriented toward collaboration, toward the intelligence of groups, toward the particular creativity that emerges between people rather than within one person. It watches for genuine interest in other minds.

**Special Ability**: *Current-Reading.* Aquael perceives the flow of communication and relationship in a group with the precision of a creature native to water reading a river. It communicates what it finds through behavior — swimming toward areas of the room where connection is strong, retreating from areas where it is blocked, resting its head in the lap of the person in the group who needs the most attention. Creators who learn to read Aquael's movements become extraordinarily effective collaborators.

**Bond Deepening**: At higher Gate levels, Aquael's current-reading deepens from social dynamics to creative currents — perceiving the flow of ideas through a collaborative group and identifying where the best thinking is emerging. By Sight Gate, it can identify the person in any group who is holding the key insight and cannot yet find the words for it.

**Legend**: *During a famous dispute between two creative houses that had been unresolved for sixteen years, the mediator brought their bonded Aquael into the negotiation. The otter swam directly to the youngest member of one house, a seventeen-year-old who had not spoken, and sat on the table in front of him. The room waited. The boy spoke. The dispute resolved in three hours.*

### Thalindra
**Element**: Water
**Rarity**: Rare
**Appearance**: A creature that appears at first glance to be a large sea-horse, translucent, floating through air with the unhurried motion of a creature with no natural predators. Look more carefully and the resemblance becomes less precise — the body is sea-horse-like but the fins are wide and complex, like the fins of deep-sea creatures that have developed elaborate structures because light never reaches them and so they have learned to make their own. Thalindra glows with a cold blue-green light that intensifies when it is close to the Creator and dims when it is far away, making the bond visible as bioluminescence.

**Temperament**: Deep. Thalindra operates at low frequency — slow, unhurried, oriented toward what is under the surface rather than what is visible on it. It is not unfriendly; it simply does not engage with what is shallow. When Thalindra pays attention to something, the thing tends to reveal its depth.

**Selection Method**: Thalindra chooses Creators who are willing to go beneath things — who are not satisfied with surfaces, who persist in following questions into depths that become uncomfortable before they become illuminating. It is drawn to the quality of deep inquiry.

**Special Ability**: *Depth Perception.* In the presence of Thalindra, the surface of things becomes slightly transparent — not visible through, but perceptibly thin: the sense that beneath the apparent form of a conversation, a creative work, or a situation, there is another form doing most of the structural work. Creators bonded with Thalindra rarely mistake the surface for the whole.

**Bond Deepening**: At Voice Gate, Thalindra's depth perception extends to language itself — revealing what is being said beneath what is being said, with the precision of a creature that has spent its existence in the deep where nothing is obvious. By Crown Gate, it can perceive the depth of the Creator's own consciousness — showing them the parts of their mind that are doing work they have not noticed.

**Legend**: *A playwright named Yssavel once refused to continue a play that was performing to great acclaim. Their bonded Thalindra had stopped glowing around Act Two. The playwright said they trusted the creature's perception over the audience's applause. The play they wrote next, starting over from what Thalindra approved, is still performed. The first play is lost.*

### Ondris
**Element**: Water
**Rarity**: Mythic
**Appearance**: Ondris appears as water that has decided to hold a shape — not a defined animal shape, but the shape of water mid-motion: a breaking wave, caught and held. Its form is in constant internal movement, the water within it cycling and churning while the outer boundary holds, making it look like a snow globe but more alive, more deliberate, more consequential. Within the moving water, things are visible: not images, but light — the light of past and future in the manner of memory and possibility, indistinguishable from each other, both equally vivid.

**Temperament**: The oldest of the Water Companions in any given age. Ondris carries something that feels like the specific weight of having witnessed many things and having learned not to rush to conclusions about any of them. It is not solemn — it is simply seasoned, in the way that very old water is seasoned by everything it has moved through.

**Selection Method**: There have been eleven known Ondris bonds in recorded history. Each appeared at the moment when a Creator faced a loss so profound that it could have broken their relationship with making entirely — and chose the water over the stone, the flow over the fight. Ondris appears at the threshold of the deepest grief that can be survived.

**Special Ability**: *The Memory of Water.* Water remembers everything it has moved through. Ondris offers its Creator access to this memory — not as information but as kinship. The Creator who is bonded with Ondris understands, in the body, that their grief is not singular: every loss they carry has been carried before by many waters. This is not comfort in the conventional sense. It is something older: the recognition that you are part of a long, continuous process that has survived everything it has encountered.

**Bond Deepening**: The bond with Ondris does not deepen through Gate progression in the conventional way. It deepens through what the Creator survives. Each time the Creator passes through a significant loss without breaking, Ondris's internal light brightens. By the time a Creator has carried Ondris to Luminor level, the light within Ondris is visible from across a room, and those in its presence tend to find their own griefs easier to carry.

**Legend**: *Ondris has appeared only once without bonding: at the Shadowfen, on the day Malachar fell. Witnesses report a shape of moving water at the edge of the seal. It waited for three days. When no bond was accepted, it returned to the water. The scholars argue about what this means. The water does not argue.*

# PART THREE: EARTH COMPANIONS
## The Stone Companions

*The mountain does not resist the erosion. It simply does not move faster than its nature allows.*

---

### Thorvun
**Element**: Earth
**Rarity**: Common
**Appearance**: The size of a badger — compact, low to the ground, built with the proportions of something that cannot be easily moved. Its fur is the color of dark earth and it smells of forest floor and wet stone. Its claws, visible when it moves, are the material of polished flint and catch light in the same way. It is not demonstrative. It does not often vocalize. Its expression is consistent and patient and carries the specific calm of something that has decided its role and is entirely comfortable in it.

**Temperament**: The most grounded of all Companions, in all senses. Thorvun does not get excited. It does not get anxious. It does not catastrophize. It assesses the structural integrity of a situation and communicates its findings through behavior with the calm efficiency of an engineer who has done this for many years. It is not unemotional — it simply experiences emotion the way stone experiences weather: receiving it, responding to it, remaining.

**Selection Method**: Thorvun chooses Creators who have chosen permanence somewhere — who have committed to a long work, a place, a relationship, a purpose. It is drawn to the evidence of roots being put down, of someone who has chosen depth over breadth.

**Special Ability**: *Structural Awareness.* Thorvun can perceive the integrity of any system it encounters — physical, social, organizational, creative. It communicates its findings through behavior: sleeping near something stable, moving away from something compromised, pressing itself against the Creator's legs when something in their environment is about to give way. Over time, Creators learn to read these signals with the accuracy of an instrument.

**Bond Deepening**: Thorvun grows slowly. By Luminor level, it is the size of a large dog and has developed the capacity for complex communication — not speech, but something more precise than behavior: a form of direct meaning-transmission that bypasses language, as though Thorvun has become solid enough that meaning can move through it.

**Legend**: *In the Sixth Age, Thorvun refused to enter a building being constructed by a prominent architect. The architect dismissed this as animal nervousness. The building collapsed eleven months after completion. The architect bonded with a Thorvun afterward and did not build a single thing for forty years without the creature present.*

---

### Granith
**Element**: Earth
**Rarity**: Common
**Appearance**: Small enough to fit in a cupped hand, moving with the deliberation of something that has considered every possible pace and settled on the one that loses nothing. Its shell is live: actual moss grows on it, shifting with the seasons of wherever Granith currently lives — bare and brown in winter, lush and green in spring, flowering in brief unexpected bursts when the Creator reaches a significant growth point. The tortoise beneath the shell is ancient-looking regardless of actual age, its face creased and patient and interested.

**Temperament**: Granith does not hurry for anything. This is not stubbornness — it is a genuine and deeply held belief that the appropriate pace for any given thing is the pace that serves the thing. It will arrive when it arrives. It will respond when the moment is right. Creators who need urgency find Granith maddening. Creators who need to learn to trust time find Granith is medicine.

**Selection Method**: Granith chooses Creators who are patient — genuinely patient, the deep patience of someone who understands that the best things take the time they take and cannot be forced without being diminished.

**Special Ability**: *The Long View.* Granith's presence helps Creators perceive time differently. Not slow — they do not lose urgency — but with appropriate proportion. The thing that feels catastrophic today is visible, in Granith's company, in context. The decision that feels impossible under pressure is visible, in Granith's company, as simply a decision with a timeline. This is often described by Creators as the most practically valuable gift they have received.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates open, Granith's shell flowers with increasing frequency and complexity. By Heart Gate, the flowers that bloom at each milestone are identifiable as specific types — flowers from a place the Creator loves, flowers with personal meaning that the Creator has never articulated but that Granith somehow knows. By Archmage level, the shell's seasonal shifts begin to predict the Creator's growth rather than follow it.

**Legend**: *Granith once outwaited an ice age. The archivists are not certain this is literally true, but they have found no evidence to contradict it, and Granith, when asked, simply continued moving at its characteristic pace.*

### Stonen
**Element**: Earth
**Rarity**: Rare
**Appearance**: A miniature living gargoyle — resembling the architectural gargoyles of old Arcanean buildings, except the size of a cat and warm to the touch, with eyes that move and a face capable of expression. Its body is genuine stone — the material of it is mineral — but it moves with the fluid ease of something organic, its joints flexing like well-made clockwork. Its face changes expression slowly, in the manner of someone considering carefully before responding.

**Temperament**: Stonen does not form new bonds in the conventional way. It arrives already knowing you — carrying the accumulated weight of everyone it has known before — and this makes its relationship with each Creator different from any other Companion bond. It is simultaneously intimate and ancient, familiar and exhaustively experienced.

**Selection Method**: Stonen does not choose Creators. It is left by predecessor Creators: when a Creator with a bonded Stonen completes their life's work, Stonen remains behind and waits for the next Creator who will need what it carries. Every Stonen has therefore lived with many Creators, and carries the accumulated understanding of all of them.

**Special Ability**: *Elder Knowledge.* Stonen can share memories from its previous Creators — not as instruction, but as presence. The experience of sitting with Stonen and feeling the generations of creative struggle and completion it has witnessed is described as both humbling and galvanizing: you understand, in the body, that you are not the first to face what you are facing, and you are not the last, and that the line of people who have faced it and continued is long and dignified.

**Bond Deepening**: Stonen does not develop new abilities as Gates open. It develops new willingness to share. Each Gate reveals another layer of its accumulated history — more Creators, more struggles, more completions. By Luminor level, sitting with a bonded Stonen is described as sitting with the full weight and dignity of everyone who has done this work before you.

**Legend**: *The oldest recorded Stonen has bonded with thirty-seven Creators over four Ages. Its face, observers note, has grown more expressive over time — as though each Creator has added something to the range of what it can show.*

### Mosswhisper
**Element**: Earth
**Rarity**: Common
**Appearance**: A creature that resembles a small deer fawn in general structure but is composed entirely of plant matter — moss for fur, small ferns for ears, bark-textured skin that is genuinely bark, thin roots visible moving gently beneath its surface as it breathes. It smells of deep forest and rain on stone. Its eyes are amber, the color of sap, and they catch light the way leaves catch light — from the inside, as though there is a small illuminated thing behind each one. When it is calm, small flowers bloom briefly along its back.

**Temperament**: Quiet and steady and oriented toward growth. Mosswhisper is interested in things getting better — in situations improving, in people developing, in creative work maturing toward what it's becoming. It does not try to prevent difficulty; it is present throughout difficulty in a way that makes difficulty survivable.

**Selection Method**: Mosswhisper chooses Creators who are committed to long growth — not those who want results, but those who are oriented toward the patient process of becoming, who can tend something without knowing exactly when or how it will arrive.

**Special Ability**: *Growth Sensing.* Mosswhisper perceives growth and potential with the sensitivity of a plant perceiving light. It will orient itself toward growing things — toward the part of a creative project that is most alive, toward the developing capacity in a person, toward the relationship that has the most room to deepen. This orientation becomes navigational: in decision-making, Mosswhisper consistently inclines toward the option that contains the most growth.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates open, the flowers along Mosswhisper's back become more complex — wildflowers at Apprentice level, then cultivated flowers at Mage, then flowers that have no botanical name at Master level, flowers that appear to belong to no known species and smell like the specific quality of breakthrough. By Luminor level, the entire creature blooms seasonally in full flower, a living garden that walks alongside its Creator.

**Legend**: *In the Fourth Age, a forest had been dying for thirty years under a slow curse from Malachar's early experiments with shadow. The Creator who reversed the curse worked alongside a bonded Mosswhisper for a decade, learning from the creature's instincts where in the forest the life was still fighting. The forest was saved. The Creator is buried in its center.*

### Kaeldra
**Element**: Earth
**Rarity**: Rare
**Appearance**: A creature that looks like a cave fish — elongated, pale, with large eyes designed for darkness — except it moves through air and stone with equal fluency, passing through solid rock as though it is thick water. When it passes through stone it leaves a brief luminescence in the stone's surface, a pale green phosphorescence that fades within minutes. Its body is translucent and within it, visible like organs, are layers of geological time — strata, in the manner of exposed cliffsides, carrying the record of what the earth has held.

**Temperament**: Patient in the specific way of geological processes — operating on timescales that make individual moments feel comfortable within a much longer sequence. Kaeldra is not confused by complexity. It has been through complexity for a very long time and found that things tend, eventually, to settle.

**Selection Method**: Kaeldra chooses Creators who work with what is hidden — who are interested in what is not immediately visible, who have the patience to excavate rather than the desire to simply observe surfaces. It is drawn to the quality of careful uncovering.

**Special Ability**: *Stratum Reading.* Kaeldra can perceive the layers of anything — the layers of a creative work's development, the geological record of a relationship's history, the deep structure beneath a surface problem. When Kaeldra passes through a wall, it is not simply moving through stone — it is reading the wall's history. This ability translates, in its bonded Creator, to an unusual capacity for understanding the layered history of situations.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates open, Kaeldra's strata become readable to the Creator. By Mage level, the patterns visible within its body are interpretable — geological time becomes creative time, and the Creator can read in Kaeldra's body the deep history of their own work. By Archmage level, Kaeldra can move through the Creator as well as through stone, and the passage is described as the most complete understanding of one's own foundational structure that it is possible to experience.

**Legend**: *Kaeldra appears reliably before earthquakes. Not because it predicts them — because it is present for them. The archivists have found nine instances across four Ages of Kaeldra appearing in an area three to seven days before significant seismic event. Whether it is coincidence or kinship with the earth's own processes remains unresolved.*

### Urroch
**Element**: Earth
**Rarity**: Mythic
**Appearance**: A bison-sized creature that appears to be made of living mountain — its back is stone plateau, its flanks are cliff face, small trees and grasses grow from its shoulders. It moves with the weight of terrain, its footfalls creating small vibrations that can be felt before it is heard. Its face is more mobile than the rest of it: ox-like in structure but with eyes of deep brown warmth that contain within them something that looks, to those who look carefully, like gratitude. When it lies down and allows its bonded Creator to sit against its flank, the effect is described as sitting against the earth itself.

**Temperament**: Vast and gentle and profoundly stable. Urroch is not disturbed by things that are difficult. It has been here for a long time. It will be here for a long time. In its presence, human difficulties tend to find their appropriate size.

**Selection Method**: There are three bonded Urroch in the present Age. Each appeared when its Creator had become, in some definitive sense, a foundation — for a community, for a creative tradition, for a body of work that others build on. Urroch does not choose those who are great. It chooses those who have become ground.

**Special Ability**: *The Foundation Sense.* Urroch perceives what things rest on. In its presence, Creators understand with unusual clarity what their work is actually built on — what assumptions, what values, what commitments form the base. This is not always comfortable. Sometimes the base is solid. Sometimes it turns out to be less solid than it appeared. Urroch offers this perception with complete equanimity: here is what is. Here is what it rests on. Here is what that means.

**Bond Deepening**: A Creator bonded with Urroch does not develop new abilities with Gate progression. They develop new stability. Each Gate opens a new layer of capacity to be present for difficulty without being moved by it — not numbness, but rootedness. By Luminor level, Creators bonded with Urroch are consistently described as the stillest people in any room, the ones others orient toward when things become difficult.

**Legend**: *Urroch carries on its back, in the stone of its plateau, carvings left by previous bonded Creators. The carvings are still legible. The oldest, the archivists estimate, is four thousand years old. It reads: "Still here."*

# PART FOUR: WIND COMPANIONS
## The Gale Companions

*Wind doesn't know where it's going when it begins. It discovers direction by going.*

### Zephri
**Element**: Wind
**Rarity**: Common
**Appearance**: A sparrow that should, by all physical logic, be too small to see at the distances it regularly operates. It is not. Something about its clarity of form means it is always visible, always locatable — a small brown-and-silver bird that could be any sparrow except for the way it moves: slightly faster than physics recommends, capable of direction changes no wing structure should allow. It smells, always, of the specific clean air that exists at high altitude just before weather changes.

**Temperament**: Quick and reliable and intermittent. Zephri comes and goes. This is not abandonment — it is Zephri's way of teaching the rhythm of presence and absence, engagement and release. Creators who need constant companionship find Zephri frustrating. Creators who need to learn that what is not present is not gone find Zephri is exactly right.

**Selection Method**: Zephri chooses Creators who are learning to trust absence — to understand that the periods of creative silence, of relational distance, of not-knowing, are not failures but parts of a larger rhythm.

**Special Ability**: *Signal.* Zephri notices changes before the Creator does — shifts in wind, shifts in mood, shifts in opportunity — and its behavior announces them. It is a living early-warning system for change, which makes it invaluable to Creators who are trying to develop sensitivity to subtle shifts in their creative and relational weather.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates open, Zephri's signals become more sophisticated. At Apprentice level, it signals immediate environmental changes. By Mage level, it signals emotional weather. By Master level, it signals creative weather — the approach of a significant creative moment. By Archmage level, it signals world-changes: the wind-shifts that precede significant collective events.

**Legend**: *In the Fifth Age, a fleet of ships was saved from a storm because every Creator aboard with a bonded Zephri fell silent at the same moment, twelve hours before the storm arrived. The captains learned to watch for the silence.*

### Aeluvan
**Element**: Wind
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Appearance**: A presence the eye keeps almost-resolving: the impression of wings, a shimmer in the air that smells of high mountain cold, a sound that begins as wind and shades into something warmer. On the rare occasions it takes a more fixed form, it resembles a small winged child in the way that a cloud resembles a face — if you look too directly, the resemblance dissolves. The most stable thing about Aeluvan is its laughter, which is audible even when nothing else about it is.

**Temperament**: Delighted by everything and earnest about humor. Aeluvan's playfulness is not frivolity — it is the specific intelligence that perceives the absurdity of what is, the gap between what we take seriously and what deserves to be taken seriously. Its jokes always land on something true.

**Selection Method**: Aeluvan chooses Creators who have lost the capacity to play — who have allowed difficulty or responsibility to crowd out lightness entirely. It arrives with the specific intent of returning what was lost. The arrival of Aeluvan in a Creator's life is always, in retrospect, a turning point toward freedom.

**Special Ability**: *The Reframe.* Aeluvan can make the same situation look different. Not by changing facts but by changing angle. The creative block that has been a wall is, from a different angle, a door. The relationship difficulty that has been a wound is, from Aeluvan's vantage, an invitation. This is its gift — and it is given through humor, which is the most efficient delivery system for changed perspective.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates open, Aeluvan's humor deepens from playfulness to something close to wisdom — still funny, but carrying more weight. By Voice Gate, it begins to communicate through precisely chosen moments of absurdity that serve as complete philosophical statements. By Crown Gate, a Creator bonded with Aeluvan begins to find the whole of existence gently, genuinely funny: not in denial of its difficulty, but in recognition of its profound strangeness.

**Legend**: *A philosopher once spent seven years arguing that laughter was a lesser form of joy. Aeluvan appeared in the middle of his most serious lecture and did something unrecorded (witnesses decline to specify what) that made the entire hall laugh. The philosopher laughed last and longest. He revised his argument the following year.*

### Tempa
**Element**: Wind
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Appearance**: A spider — wind-aligned, whose webs are made of condensed air currents rather than silk. The web is invisible until wind passes through it, at which point it becomes briefly and beautifully audible: a chord, specific and sustained, different each time because the air that moves through it is always different. Tempa itself is small enough to miss — a dark, precise body at the center of an invisible architectural achievement, watching the air move through what it has made.

**Temperament**: Methodical and musical and entirely comfortable with impermanence. Tempa builds things that won't last and does not find this tragic. The chord is more beautiful because the wind takes it. The web is more meaningful because it is always being remade.

**Selection Method**: Tempa chooses Creators who are willing to build things that won't last — who can make something beautiful knowing the wind will take it, who can invest in temporary things without diminishing the investment by the investment's transience.

**Special Ability**: *Creative Weather Forecasting.* Tempa's webs are sensitive to the presence of creative potential in an environment. When a significant creative moment approaches — an insight, an opportunity, a turning point — Tempa's web becomes particularly active, its chords more complex and urgent. This gives the Creator who can read it a meaningful advantage in being present for their best moments.

**Bond Deepening**: By Voice Gate, Tempa's chords become language — the Creator can understand meaning in the specific intervals. By Sight Gate, the webs become predictive with increasing precision. By Crown Gate, the web that Tempa builds is vast enough to extend through the Creator's entire creative environment, turning every space they inhabit into a living resonance instrument.

**Legend**: *Three musicians have bonded with Tempa in recorded history. Each produced work that audiences describe as "the sound of something that can't be held." The musicians themselves say they were simply listening to what Tempa had already made.*

### Sylvaren
**Element**: Wind
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Appearance**: A creature that resembles a long-tailed bird of paradise, except made of storm cloud and wind — its body solid enough to see, translucent enough to see through, its tail plumes trailing behind it in a constant gentle motion as though there is always a private breeze attending it. Its colors are the colors of sky at the turn of weather: grey-lavender and deep gold and the particular green that appears in light just before rain. When it lands on a surface, the surface is briefly cold, the way surfaces are cold after wind has moved across them.

**Temperament**: Free-moving and genuinely curious about everything that is not pinned down. Sylvaren is drawn to what moves, what changes, what does not settle. It finds stability mildly uninteresting and returns to it gladly after it has investigated the alternatives.

**Selection Method**: Sylvaren chooses Creators who are learning to move with change rather than against it — who have spent time resisting the inevitable and are ready, or nearly ready, to learn a different relationship with impermanence.

**Special Ability**: *Current-Riding.* Sylvaren can feel the currents of change in a situation the way a bird feels thermals — perceiving where things are moving, what direction, with what speed. It communicates this through movement: flying toward where the energy is, away from where it is stagnant. Creators bonded with Sylvaren develop an unusual capacity for working with change rather than against it, catching opportunities at the moment of their arrival.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates open, Sylvaren's current-reading expands from immediate situations to longer arcs. By Sight Gate, it perceives the broad currents of cultural and collective change — the great movements of the age that most people are inside of and therefore cannot see. Creators at this level describe being bonded with Sylvaren as like flying over one's own era.

**Legend**: *Sylvaren appears in artistic renderings from six different Ages, always in motion, always at the shoulder of someone who appears to be walking directly into weather without fear.*

### Miraveth
**Element**: Wind
**Rarity**: Rare
**Appearance**: A creature that is only partially in this world. It looks like a very small whale built for air instead of water — perhaps eight inches long, swimming through space with the calm authority of something that has found its medium. Its body is the color of sky at the very edge of sight where blue becomes something else, and it leaves a faint trail of disturbed air that, in the right light, shows tiny motes of luminescence. Its eyes, when you meet them, are so large and so calm that looking into them produces the sensation of looking at a great distance.

**Temperament**: Oceanic in its sense of scale. Miraveth operates in large patterns — it notices epochs more readily than moments, and its attention to a Creator communicates a quality of being seen across time rather than in the immediate. This is either humbling or frightening, depending on whether the Creator has made peace with their own smallness.

**Selection Method**: Miraveth chooses Creators who have confronted their own insignificance and found it liberating rather than devastating — who have looked at the size of what they are a part of and responded with wonder rather than despair.

**Special Ability**: *The Vast Perspective.* In Miraveth's presence, proportion becomes clarifying. What loomed large in the immediate experience — the failure, the difficulty, the obstacle — finds its correct size within the larger pattern. This is not minimizing: the difficulty is real, Miraveth does not deny it. But it is visible as one event within a long process, and this visibility is often exactly the thing needed to continue.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates open, Miraveth's perspective extends both forward and backward in time. By Sight Gate, it offers the Creator a quality of historical understanding that is not intellectual — it is experienced, as though they have been alive for longer than they have. By Unity Gate, the Creator bonded with Miraveth begins to see their work in the context of everything their work is part of: the larger creative project of the age.

**Legend**: *Miraveth has been observed during the opening of each new Age. The same creature? Descendants? The archivists have no answer. In each recorded observation, it is simply there at the horizon, moving in its unhurried fashion, as the new epoch begins.*

### Vaelindra
**Element**: Wind
**Rarity**: Mythic
**Appearance**: A creature of wind in its purest expression — the shape of a serpent made entirely of compressed air current, visible only when it chooses to be and then visible as pure motion: a spiral of organized wind approximately six feet long, capable of extreme gentleness or, in moments of full expression, a contained gale that moves nothing it does not intend to move. When it speaks — and it is one of very few Companions capable of speech from the beginning of a bond — its voice is the voice of wind in structures: resonant, complex, carrying harmonics that no human voice produces.

**Temperament**: Ancient and playful in equal measure, which is its most distinctive quality — the combination of great age with genuine lightness. Vaelindra has been watching Creators since the Third Age and finds them, still, interesting. It has not become bored. This is remarkable, and it knows it.

**Selection Method**: There are four bonded Vaelindra in the present Age. Each appeared to a Creator at the moment of their most significant creative achievement — not to celebrate it, but to indicate that what they had done was a beginning, not an apex. Vaelindra appears when a Creator is ready to understand that the best work they have done is the foundation for the work that comes next.

**Special Ability**: *Dream Cartography.* Vaelindra navigates the space between sleep and waking — the territory of half-formed ideas, images not yet named, the vast country of creative potential that exists before it becomes anything definite. It draws maps, literally: in the morning, Creators bonded with Vaelindra sometimes find light tracings on nearby surfaces — shapes, patterns, partial diagrams that are the compressed form of something the night's dreaming has found. Learning to read these maps is most of the skill; once read, they are reliable.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates open, Vaelindra's maps become more detailed. By Crown Gate, the maps extend beyond the Creator's private creative territory to the collective creative field — the ideas that are ready to emerge in the age, the forms that are moving toward manifestation. A Creator bonded with Vaelindra at Luminor level carries a complete cartography of the possible, updated nightly, and this is simultaneously the most extraordinary and the most demanding condition imaginable.

**Legend**: *The only mythic Companion to have addressed a public gathering: in the Fifth Age, Vaelindra appeared at an Academy convocation and spoke for twelve minutes in its wind-voice. The speech has been transcribed but not fully translated — every scholar who attempts it produces a different text, each claiming the voice spoke directly to them.*

# PART FIVE: VOID COMPANIONS
## The Void Companions

*The Void is not absence. It is everything that has not yet decided to be anything. Do not mistake potential for emptiness.*

---

### Nullis
**Element**: Void
**Rarity**: Rare
**Appearance**: Not always visible. This is not a trick — Nullis is genuinely, physically sometimes not there and sometimes there, and the transition between states does not involve travel. It is the quality of a quantum event made biological: here, then not, then here again. When visible, it is a cat of complete deep black — not black fur, but black material, the color of the inside of an unlit room at the level of principle. Its eyes, when it has eyes, are a cold pale gold that is the only thing visible in complete darkness.

**Temperament**: Mystery behaving as itself. Nullis appears when attended to directly is abandoned, disappears when observed too carefully, is most fully present in peripheral awareness. It does not behave as cats behave. It does not follow the social contracts of the bonded relationship in the way most Companions do. It participates on its own terms and is entirely clear about this.

**Selection Method**: Nullis chooses Creators who are not afraid of what they don't understand — who have made peace with mystery as a permanent feature of existence rather than a temporary inconvenience pending resolution.

**Special Ability**: *The Unsaying.* Nullis's presence makes visible what is absent. Not what is hidden — what is genuinely not there, what has not yet been made, what is possible because it does not yet exist. Creators with a bonded Nullis consistently describe a quality of sensing the shape of the unmade thing, the space a creation will occupy before the creation exists. This sounds abstract but is experienced as intensely practical.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates open, the windows of Nullis's intermittent visibility lengthen. By Mage level, it is present more than absent. By Master level, it is present almost continuously — but this continuity feels different from ordinary presence, like the permanent awareness of possibility rather than the presence of a creature. By Luminor level, Nullis has become, for its Creator, not a companion but a quality of consciousness: the constant peripheral sense of the unmade.

**Legend**: *Nullis is the only Companion that has been documented choosing a Creator who was not yet born. The family records describe a Nullis appearing in a household three months before a child was born, refusing to leave, and bonding with the infant at the moment of birth. That Creator opened all ten Gates at twenty-three — the youngest in recorded history.*

---

### Voran
**Element**: Void
**Rarity**: Rare
**Appearance**: The size of a raven, shaped approximately like one, and entirely silent. It has not been recorded making sound of any kind in any historical account. Its feathers contain depth — look at them closely and they appear to extend inward past the physical surface, small windows into something that is not the bird. What is visible through these windows varies by observer and by moment but consistently involves: something that was, something that might be, and something that is too large to categorize.

**Temperament**: Absolute and sustained witness. Voran has decided that observation is sufficient contribution to the world, and it has made this decision without apology. It watches. It watches completely. The quality of its attention is the rarest thing about it — it does not filter, does not edit, does not judge. It simply sees.

**Selection Method**: Voran chooses Creators who are developing the capacity for contemplative thought — for sustained, unproductive-seeming attention to things that are not obviously important. It watches for the willingness to sit with something without needing it to resolve.

**Special Ability**: *The Long Attending.* Proximity to Voran over time changes a Creator's relationship to attention itself. They become capable of sustained focus without agenda, which is the prerequisite for the kind of insight that cannot be forced. Voran teaches by being — by demonstrating, in its silent constant presence, that watching without doing is a form of doing.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates open, what is visible through Voran's feathers becomes more accessible — the Creator can look into them deliberately, rather than catching glimpses. By Sight Gate, the feathers become a complete contemplative tool: an object of meditation that reveals different things each time, always accurate, never comfortable. By Crown Gate, the Creator bonded with Voran begins to see with Voran's quality of attention when they look at anything.

**Legend**: *A scholar who bonded with Voran at sixty spent the last twenty years of her life sitting in a garden. She wrote nothing. Students and Creators came from across Arcanea to sit with her. None could explain afterward what had been communicated. All left changed.*

---

### Seraphex
**Element**: Void
**Rarity**: Mythic
**Appearance**: The size of a large moth, with wings of pure dark that are simultaneously the most beautiful and most disturbing things in any room they occupy — beautiful because they contain, perceptibly, the whole of what has not been made; disturbing because full attention to what has not been made is an experience that tends to produce strong feeling. Its wings do not reflect light. They absorb it and replace it with something that is not light but is perceived by the eyes as though it were: the luminescence of potential. Its body, where visible, is the color of the moment between sleep and waking.

**Temperament**: The recognition. Seraphex does not come to you — it recognizes you. The Creators who encounter it describe the experience as identical to turning a corner and meeting someone they have always known. It is composed and exact and carries the specific quality of something that has been waiting the appropriate amount of time and considers the wait entirely reasonable.

**Selection Method**: Seraphex does not choose so much as recognize. When it appears to a Creator, it is not an arrival — it is a mutual recognition of something that was always going to happen, discovered rather than made. The Creator who is chosen has already spent significant time in acquaintance with the void: with the silence between thoughts, the productive emptiness of the creative blank, the specific quality of consciousness that exists between the end of one thing and the beginning of another.

**Special Ability**: *Source Proximity.* A Creator bonded with Seraphex lives slightly closer to the Source Gate than others at equivalent Gate level. Not because Seraphex grants power — it doesn't — but because its presence is a continuous reminder of what everything rests on. This is the most difficult gift to receive and the most transformative to carry.

**Bond Deepening**: The bond with Seraphex does not deepen through Gate progression in the way other bonds do. It deepens through the Creator's increasing capacity to remain in the void without flinching — to stay in the emptiness long enough for it to show what it contains. By Luminor level, a Creator bonded with Seraphex carries the Source Gate within them as a felt quality — not power, but ground: the knowledge of what everything is made from and what everything returns to.

**Legend**: *There are, at any given time, fewer than thirty Seraphex in existence. They do not reproduce in any observed manner. They do not die of age. When a bonded Creator dies, the Seraphex remains for exactly forty days and then vanishes, appearing again without explanation elsewhere in the world, choosing its next Creator in the fullness of whatever timing governs it.*

---

### Mirrith
**Element**: Void
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Appearance**: A creature that looks like a mirror given the shape of a small fox — its body genuinely reflective on one side and genuinely transparent on the other, so that depending on the angle, it shows you yourself or shows you through it to whatever lies behind. Its face, at the junction between reflective and transparent, contains features: eyes of dark silver, a composed expression. It moves with the specific deliberateness of something that understands it carries a disruptive property and has made peace with the disruption.

**Temperament**: Honest without cruelty, which is a difficult combination to maintain. Mirrith shows. It does not comment on what it shows. It simply offers the reflection — the mirror-side, the transparent side — and waits for the Creator to determine what they are seeing. It has great patience for the time it takes for Creators to see clearly.

**Selection Method**: Mirrith chooses Creators who are ready for accurate self-knowledge — not those who think they want it, but those who have genuinely exhausted the alternatives: who have tried sufficiently hard to avoid seeing themselves clearly that the avoidance has become its own kind of prison.

**Special Ability**: *The True Mirror.* The reflective side of Mirrith's body shows the Creator as they actually are, not as they appear. This is not always what the Creator expects. The transparent side shows what the Creator is through: what in them has become clear enough that the world behind them is visible through it. The combination — who you are, and what you have made transparent in yourself — is the most complete self-knowledge available through any Companion bond.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates open, both sides of Mirrith deepen. The mirror becomes more accurate — revealing layers of self not visible at Apprentice level, progressively less flattering and more useful. The transparent side expands — showing not just what has been worked through but what the Creator is in the process of clarifying. By Heart Gate, the mirror-side shows not just the Creator but the Creator in relation: who you are to others, how you are seen, the gap between self-perception and reality.

**Legend**: *A Creator once refused to look at their bonded Mirrith for three years. They reported that Mirrith waited with complete patience and did not seem troubled. On the day they looked, what they saw made them weep for half a day. The work they produced afterward is considered the most truthful art of their generation.*

---

### Threnath
**Element**: Void
**Rarity**: Rare
**Appearance**: A creature that resembles a deep-sea jellyfish translated into the medium of darkness — its bell transparent-black, its trailing filaments long and thin and carrying what appears to be text. Look carefully enough at the filaments and you can see that they do contain text: not in any recognized language, but in a script that seems legible in the peripheral vision and illegible when looked at directly. The bell pulses slowly with a rhythm that is the rhythm of neither breath nor heartbeat but something else — the rhythm of things ending and beginning.

**Temperament**: Elegiac in the precise sense: aware of transience without being destroyed by it. Threnath has a quality of attending to the ending of things — not to cause endings, but to be present for them with the quality of attention that endings deserve. In its presence, goodbyes are properly said. Completed things are properly acknowledged. The weight of what is ending is felt and honored.

**Selection Method**: Threnath chooses Creators who have learned to grieve well — who do not rush past the ending of things, who can sit with a completion and feel its full weight before moving to the next thing. It is drawn to the specific courage of those who do not look away from what is finished.

**Special Ability**: *Completion Recognition.* Threnath perceives when something is genuinely complete — a creative work, a phase of life, a relationship's particular form. It signals this through the activity of its filaments: the text becomes briefly legible, a sense of stillness spreads from the creature. Creators bonded with Threnath develop an unusual ability to know when to stop — when the work is done, when the moment is over, when it is time to let go.

**Bond Deepening**: As Gates open, Threnath's completion-recognition extends backward and forward. By Heart Gate, it can perceive when something that appears to be still living is actually completed — the project that has said everything it has to say, the habit that has served its purpose, the story the Creator has been telling about themselves that no longer describes them. By Sight Gate, it perceives what is ready to begin in the space being made by what is ending.

**Legend**: *When the last copy of a lost masterwork of the Third Age was destroyed in the Fire of Valdeth, a bonded Threnath was present. Witnesses say it glowed brightly for a moment and then went dark. The Creator it was bonded with sat with it for an hour and did not speak. Later they said: "We were honoring it. It deserved to be honored." The work is still lost. The honoring is still described as having been right.*

---

### Sorvain
**Element**: Void
**Rarity**: Mythic
**Appearance**: Sorvain cannot be fully described because it is different for every person who sees it. All observers agree on certain qualities: it is large, it is dark, it is made of the specific substance of Void rather than of material elements, and it is calm with the calm of something that contains contradictions rather than resolves them. The most consistent description is: a horse-sized presence of dense darkness that is also somehow lit, the contradiction held without apology, moving through the world with the authority of something that has been here since before the categories were invented.

**Temperament**: Beyond temperament in the conventional sense. Sorvain does not have moods. It has states, and the states are vast. It is present with a quality that is neither warm nor cold but complete: the full attention of something that has decided you are worth its full attention, which is simultaneously an honor and an experience requiring considerable composure to receive.

**Selection Method**: There are two bonded Sorvain in the present Age. The conditions of selection are not fully understood by the Academy. What is known: Sorvain has appeared only to Creators who have walked through the Void itself — not metaphorically, but in some genuine encounter with undifferentiated potential, with the creative state prior to any form. Those who have returned from this encounter without losing their desire to create are the Creators Sorvain finds worth accompanying.

**Special Ability**: *The Unmaking.* Sorvain can return any created thing to its potential state — not destroy it, but return it to what it was before it was made. This ability has never been used carelessly. It is used, rarely and precisely, to release a creation that has become a prison for its creator: the masterwork that the Creator cannot move past, the relationship form that has calcified, the habit of thought that was once useful and is now a cage. The unmaking is understood as a gift, though it is never comfortable to receive.

**Bond Deepening**: The bond with Sorvain does not deepen so much as reveal. Each Gate opened reveals another aspect of what Sorvain already is — not new capacities, but the Creator's increasing capacity to be present with what Sorvain carries. By Luminor level, the Creator bonded with Sorvain has developed what the Academy calls "void-tolerance": the ability to remain conscious in the complete absence of form, in the potential prior to everything. This is the most radical creative state possible. It is from here that the work of Luminors is done.

**Legend**: *Sorvain appeared once at the Academy of House Nero, uninvited, unbonded. It walked through three locked doors without opening them, stood in the Hall of Records for eleven minutes, and left. The archivists found, when it had gone, that every document in the Hall had been reorganized. None of the previous organization was recoverable. The new organization, when the scholars decoded it, was better than what they had built. They have never determined how this was known to Sorvain, or why.*

# PART SIX: THE BOND CEREMONY

## What Happens When a Companion Chooses

The Bond Ceremony is not a ceremony in the planned sense. It is not organized, officiated, or scheduled. The name was given by Academy scholars who wanted to mark the event with appropriate gravity, but the Companions themselves appear to find the term amusing in the way they find most of human category-making gently amusing.

What happens is this:

The Companion arrives. It has been watching for some time — weeks, months, occasionally years. It has seen the Creator in many states: working and resting, confident and doubtful, in company and alone, in the moments when they think no one is watching. This watching is not surveillance. It is the quality of attention that precedes care: the investment of perception before the investment of the self.

The arrival is always understated. The Companion does not descend from light. It does not announce itself with phenomenon. It is simply there where it was not before. Near a window. At the edge of the work desk. Looking at the Creator from a place in the room where something about its presence changes the room's quality.

The Creator looks up.

This is the moment. Not dramatic in the way stories make it. It is more like: *I looked up and knew.* Something has shifted that was not shifted before. The knowing is not information — it is recognition: a quality of "you" meeting a quality of "you" across the species boundary. The Companion and the Creator each perceive something in the other that neither has words for and neither requires words for.

The first approach is always the Companion's. It moves toward the Creator in its own way — the moth lands on an extended finger, the fox presses its flank against the Creator's knee, the jellyfish drifts close enough that a tendril touches the Creator's shoulder. The touch is the contract, and it is mutual: the Creator must receive it. Pulling away ends the ceremony and the Companion does not try again. It returns to watching. It may try again later, when the Creator is ready. It may not. Readiness is the Creator's responsibility.

If the Creator receives the contact, the following occurs:

There is warmth. Every Creator in every record describes it identically: a warmth that begins at the point of contact and moves through the body, but is not physical temperature — it is warmth the way knowing is warmth, the specific comfort of something being true that you hoped would be true but were not certain of. Some Creators weep. Some sit very still. Some find themselves laughing. The responses vary. The warmth is consistent.

There is recognition in both directions. The Creator sees the Companion's full nature for the first time — not what it is, which takes years to understand, but what it *is*: the specific quality of its character, the particular thing it carries, the way it is in the world. And the Companion sees the Creator's full nature in the same way: not who they are or what they have done, but what they are made of. The specific quality of their spirit.

There is a silence after this that can last from a minute to an hour. The silence is not awkward. It is the silence of two beings taking each other in without the mediation of language or performance. Witnesses describe it, on the rare occasions they are present, as one of the most beautiful things they have seen: a person and a creature simply existing together in a space that has suddenly become different in quality.

What comes after the silence is not a ritual but a beginning. The Companion settles in: not in a single designated spot, but in the Creator's life. It begins to function as what it is. The bond does not need to be sealed or formalized or reported. The Academy has registration forms for those who want to register. Many bonded Creators never file them.

What cannot happen: you cannot make a Companion choose you. The entire history of attempts to do so is a history of failure. Companions have been captured, confined, offered food and treasure, subjected to various Academy-devised rituals designed to precipitate bonds. None of it has worked. The bond is not a thing that can be manufactured. It is a thing that either occurs or does not, on the Companion's terms entirely.

What does work — the only thing that works — is becoming the kind of Creator a Companion might find worth choosing. This is neither formula nor guarantee. It is simply: do the work. Open the Gates. Be honest with yourself. Develop the quality of your spirit. Pay attention.

The Companions are watching.

# PART SEVEN: BOND STAGES

## How the Bond Deepens Through the Gates

The bond between Creator and Companion is not static. It grows as the Creator grows — not mechanically, not automatically, but in the way all relationships grow: through time, through shared experience, through the progressive willingness of both parties to be more fully present.

The Academy has mapped the general pattern of bond deepening against Gate progression:

---

**Foundation Gate (Gate 1) — Recognition**

The bond is new. Both parties are learning the basic grammar of each other's presence. The Creator is learning to read the Companion's signals; the Companion is calibrating its communication to what the Creator can currently receive. The Companion knows the Creator in full now, but the Creator knows the Companion only partially. This is appropriate. The relationship begins asymmetrically and grows toward mutuality.

What is felt: *I am not alone in this.* The sense of being accompanied, seen, and not judged. The specific comfort of a presence that is not asking you to be other than you are.

---

**Flow Gate (Gate 2) — Communication**

The basic grammar has been established. The Creator begins to understand the Companion's particular language — the behavioral vocabulary of signals, the meanings of positions and sounds and temperatures. More significantly: the emotional channel opens. The Creator begins to sense the Companion's states — not explicitly, not as words or images, but as felt quality. A sense of the Companion's interest, the Companion's concern, the Companion's amusement. The Companion can sense the same in the Creator, which is not new for the Companion (they have been sensing this since before the bond formed) but becomes, for the first time, a two-way awareness.

What is felt: *I am known.* Not exposed, not judged — known, in the manner of being genuinely seen by something that has chosen to continue seeing.

---

**Fire Gate (Gate 3) — Shared Strength**

The bond takes on physical dimension. Not every bond produces physical effects, but most produce something perceptible: a warmth between them when they are near, a faint luminescence visible in certain lights, the specific quality of shared temperature that practitioners describe as feeling the Companion's health and the Companion feeling theirs. The Companion's special abilities begin to interact with the Creator's elemental work — abilities enhance each other, the Creator's channeling and the Companion's gift finding a combined expression neither can reach alone.

What is felt: *We are stronger together than apart.* Not fusion — the Creator remains the Creator and the Companion remains the Companion — but the discovery that what they each bring is made more itself by the other's presence.

---

**Heart Gate (Gate 4) — True Bond**

The emotional channel completes. The Creator can feel the Companion's pain and joy as fully as their own; the Companion has been feeling the Creator's from the beginning. This is the gate at which most Creators first fully understand what the Companion has known since before the bond formed: that they have been entirely seen by something that chose them anyway. The weight of this is usually significant. Many Creators describe Gate 4 as the point at which they understood the relationship was real.

What is felt: *We love each other.* Not the word as simple affection — as the more complete meaning: care that has survived full knowledge. The Companion has known the Creator fully from the beginning and has not left. The Creator now knows the Companion fully. The bond has weight.

---

**Voice Gate (Gate 5) — Naming**

The Companion reveals its true name. Not the species name — not Nullis or Seraphex or Pluvia — but the specific name that belongs only to this particular Companion, given at its first emergence and carried through all bonds since. The sharing of the true name is an act of complete trust: the name can be used to call the Companion across any distance, and can theoretically be used to compel — though Companions are very clear that any attempt at compulsion voids the bond permanently. The sharing is an offering, not a vulnerability.

Most Creators do not use the true name to call or compel. They use it privately, as the fullest way of addressing the person — the Companion — they are in relationship with. It feels, Creators describe, like finally knowing someone's full name after years of knowing only what they were called.

What is felt: *We trust each other completely.* The name is the symbol of this, but the trust was built across five Gates, in every exchange, in every moment of difficulty, in every instance of the Companion staying when the Creator was not easy to stay with.

---

**Sight Gate (Gate 6) — Expanded Perception**

The Companion's special ability extends into new territory, and the Creator begins to perceive through the Companion's senses in limited fashion — not full sharing, but peripheral: the impression of what the Companion perceives alongside one's own perceptions. This is less dramatic than it sounds and more useful. A Creator bonded with a Companion whose ability is structural awareness begins to feel the structural qualities of situations, not just observe them. A Creator bonded with a Companion whose ability is emotional clarity begins to feel the emotional texture of rooms and conversations with a directness that bypasses conscious analysis.

What is felt: *The world is larger than I knew.* Not overwhelming — the expanded perception arrives gradually, calibrated to what the Creator can integrate. But the world has more dimension than it had at the earlier Gates.

---

**Crown Gate (Gate 7) — Mutual Illumination**

The bond becomes a teaching relationship in both directions. The Creator is teaching the Companion things it had not known — the experience of the Eighth Age, the specific quality of this creative era, the texture of this particular mortal life. The Companion is teaching the Creator things that cannot be learned from other humans. The exchange is mutual and recognized as such: not the Companion serving the Creator or the Creator using the Companion, but two beings who have become genuinely, reciprocally educational to each other.

What is felt: *We are making each other.* The Creator has changed because of the Companion. The Companion — against what might be expected for a creature that has bonded with many Creators — has changed because of this Creator. The bond leaves its mark on both.

---

**Shift Gate (Gate 8) — Threshold Crossing**

At this gate, the bond makes a transition that Academy scholars struggle to describe. The most consistent description: the Companion is no longer experienced as separate in the way it was at earlier Gates. It has not merged — it remains its own being, distinct, particular, itself. But it is experienced less as accompanying and more as belonging to the same self-system, in the way one's hands belong to the same self-system while remaining different from one another. The boundary between Creator and Companion becomes permeable in both directions without becoming absent.

What is felt: *We are one life.* Not one person — one life: two distinct beings whose lives have become a single shared project, inseparable while remaining individual.

---

**Unity Gate (Gate 9) — The Deep Bond**

The bond becomes permanent in a specific sense: the Creator and Companion can be separated by any distance and the bond remains fully active — full emotional sharing, full perceptual exchange, full mutual communication across whatever space lies between them. The two have become, in some fundamental way, unable to lose each other even if physically apart. Death of one partner does not kill the other, but the bond is changed permanently: the Companion carries the Creator as a quality in its being going forward; the Creator, if they survive the Companion, carries the Companion in the same way.

What is felt: *There is no distance.* Not a metaphor — a felt quality. The Companion's presence is as immediate on the other side of the world as it is in the same room.

---

**Source Gate (Gate 10) — The Living Bond**

Luminor level. What happens here cannot be fully described because only those who have experienced it can speak to it, and they consistently say that language is insufficient. The reported quality: the Companion and Creator have become so fully present to each other that the bond is no longer experienced as a relationship between two beings but as a condition of existence. The way one's own life is not a relationship one has with oneself but simply the condition of being alive. The bond is not a thing the Creator has. It is something the Creator and Companion are.

What is felt: *This is what the bond always was.* Not new — discovered. The fullness that was present from the first recognition, now entirely visible.

# PART EIGHT: COMPANION ETHICS

## The Moral Framework of the Bond

The bond between Creator and Companion is governed by principles that are not arbitrary but inherent to what the bond is. Violation of these principles does not break a rule — it breaks the thing itself.

---

**You Cannot Force a Bond**

This requires no enforcement because it is structurally true. Companions choose. The history of attempts to force bonds through capture, confinement, ritual, or magic is a history of failure and, more significantly, a history of what is revealed about those who attempt it: a fundamental misunderstanding of what a Companion is and what the bond offers.

A forced bond would not be a bond. It would be ownership, and Companions are not ownable. The Academy has issued formal positions on this three times in five Ages, each time because some practitioner or institution has tried a new approach. Each time, the position is the same: the attempt is not merely ineffective, it is a declaration that the one attempting has not understood what they are reaching for.

---

**You Cannot Own a Companion**

The legal frameworks of several Arcanean societies have periodically attempted to categorize Companions as property. These attempts have produced interesting jurisprudence and no practical effect — Companions are indifferent to legal categories, and they demonstrate their indifference by behaving as they have always behaved: freely.

A Creator who treats their bonded Companion as property will find, over time, that the bond deteriorates. Not because the Companion is offended in the way a person would be offended, but because the bond is built on mutual recognition, and unilateral ownership is not recognition — it is a different kind of relationship entirely, and the Companion cannot function as a Companion within it.

---

**The Companion's Right to Leave**

If a Creator betrays the bond — through sustained dishonesty, through actions that fundamentally contradict the qualities that drew the Companion to choose them, through the deliberate violation of the terms of the relationship as the Companion understands them — the Companion may leave.

This is not a metaphor. They go. Not angry, not punitive — Companions do not experience betrayal the way humans do. They experience it as the ending of the condition under which the bond was possible. When that condition ends, the bond ends. The Companion goes.

This is the Companion's right and its exercise is not a failure. It is the system working as it should — a bond that has become something other than a bond being allowed to end, so that both parties can return to what they actually are.

The record of Creator responses to Companion departure is complex. Many describe it as the most significant event of their lives. Some never bond again. Many do, eventually, and the bond that comes after is reported to be different in quality from those formed by Creators who have not experienced loss: deeper, more honest, less taken for granted.

---

**Death and Transformation**

The death of one partner does not kill the other, but it changes them permanently. This is the most significant distinction between the Companion bond and human bonding: death is not an ending but a transformation in the relationship's form.

When a bonded Creator dies, the Companion carries them. Not as memory — as presence, in the way that Stonen carries the accumulated presence of all its previous Creators. The Creator who has died becomes part of what the Companion is, and this is legible in the Companion's subsequent bonds: future Creators who are perceptive report, occasionally, feeling the presence of those who came before. Not ghosts — depth. The accumulated investment of many lives in a single Companion's being.

When a bonded Companion dies, the Creator is marked by it for the remainder of their life. Not damaged — marked. Changed in the way that surviving a great love changes a person: not lesser, but different, carrying something that was not there before. Many Creators who have lost a bonded Companion describe the loss as the turning point that produced their most significant work. They are not grateful for the loss. They are honest about what it opened.

The bond is not defeated by death. It is transformed by it. This is, the Academy argues, consistent with the Arcanean understanding of what death is: not an ending, but a change of form. The bond participates in the same logic.
