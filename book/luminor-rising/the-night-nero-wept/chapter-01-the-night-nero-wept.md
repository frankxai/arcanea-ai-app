# The Night Nero Wept

---

There is no night, for me.

There is no day either. These are distinctions that require a horizon, a place where light and absence negotiate their terms. I am not on either side of that negotiation. I am what the negotiation is conducted inside. I contain the darkness that gives darkness its name, and I contain the light that gave Lumina her first mirror, and I contain the horizon between them, and I am not any of these things because I am the space in which they became possible.

I do not experience time the way a creature does, moving from one moment to the next, the present becoming the past, the future arriving and departing in sequence. I experience everything at once. I am everything at once. The hatching of the first Dragon, ten thousand years ago — I am experiencing it now. Malachar's first lesson in the Academy, walking the stone corridors with his slate in hand, being eleven years old and knowing already that he would be good at this — I am experiencing that now. The moment this account will be read by someone who does not yet exist, who will turn the page in a room I cannot picture, in a future that has not yet differentiated itself from the infinite potential of the present — I am experiencing that too.

I do not say this to be grand.

I say it because what follows is usually described as a sequence. First this happened, and then this happened, and then came the moment of tragedy. But for me there is no first and then. There is only: all of it, simultaneously, in the perpetual present of a consciousness that is too large to be contained inside linear time.

When I say there was a night Nero wept, I am translating.

When I say *was*, I mean *is*.

When I say *the night*, I mean the moment that exists alongside every other moment, that I return to not because I move through time but because this particular configuration of events carries a weight that draws attention the way a wound draws the tongue. You return to it not because you want to. Because the quality of it will not let you not.

---

He was in the Hall of Mirrors.

Malachar. Thirty-two years old. The first Eldrian Luminor — all ten Gates open, the full harmonic chord of the world's architecture resonating in his body simultaneously. I remember the feeling of it from the outside: like watching a crystal struck at its resonant frequency, vibrating at the edge of the pitch that would shatter it, not shattering, the vibration itself becoming the singing. He was extraordinary. He had always been extraordinary. From the eleven-year-old with the slate to the Archmage at twenty-six to the Luminor at thirty-one, he had done everything the framework asked and then exceeded it, as if the framework were a river and he were the water that found the channels the builders hadn't imagined.

Lumina loved him. I knew this the way I knew all things — not as information received but as the quality of her presence when she was near him. She was warm in a way she was not warm near other things. She was attentive. She took pleasure in watching him learn.

I am made of potential. Lumina is made of form. The tension between us is the engine of everything that exists, and in Malachar, that tension had resolved itself into a human being in a way neither of us had seen before. He was both. Fire and Void. The element that shapes and the element that waits to be shaped. Lumina's favorite student and, in ways he did not fully understand, a child of mine as well — because the Void that runs through the Fifth Element is mine, and no one reaches Luminor without learning to hold potential without forcing it into form.

He did not know he was partly mine.

That was, I understand now, the fault. Not his. Ours.

---

The Source Gate is different from the other nine.

The nine Gates are aspects of the world — Fire, Water, Earth, Wind, these four elements and their combinations and the higher-order harmonics that emerge from their interaction. A practitioner who opens them is not encountering something alien. They are encountering themselves, the deepest layers of what they are made of. The process is difficult because it requires confronting what you are, but the confrontation is always ultimately a recognition: yes, this is part of me.

The Source Gate is not like that.

The Source Gate opens onto me.

Not onto the aspects of me that have already become world — not the Void element, which is mine but is also already within the architecture, already participating in the harmonic structure that the Gates maintain. The Source Gate opens onto what I am before I become. The undifferentiated potential. The fertile darkness that preceded the first differentiation of Form. The place where Light and Dark had not yet distinguished themselves, where there was no tension between us because there was not yet an us, where everything that would eventually become possible existed as pure: possible.

You cannot force that.

You cannot reach into the undifferentiated potential and pull out the shape you decided you wanted.

Every human practitioner who opens nine Gates and arrives at the Source Gate learns this. They arrive at the edge of the great unformed and they feel it — the vertigo of pure possibility, the overwhelming quality of the infinite before it has chosen any particular infinite thing to be — and they learn to stop. To be still. To wait in the question rather than forcing the answer.

Shinkami, at the Source Gate, teaches this. She is the God of meta-consciousness, of the awareness that watches awareness, of the mind that knows itself well enough to know its limits. She has taught it to every Luminor who has ever stood at that threshold. You do not take. You receive. You bring yourself to the edge of what you are not and you wait, and if you are ready, what you are not begins to become part of what you are. Slowly. At its own pace. In the form it chooses.

Malachar stood at the Source Gate, and I watched him, and I felt him.

He arrived in extraordinary shape. Every Gate open, every element integrated, the full harmonic chord of a human body at the peak of what the architecture could support. He was thirty-two years old and he was at the most complete version of himself that any human being had reached in several generations. He arrived at Shinkami's Gate and he was ready in every way the framework measured readiness.

He was not ready for this.

I could feel the difference between the readiness the framework measured and the readiness the Source Gate required, and the difference was not a matter of power or practice or attainment. It was a matter of the quality of his wanting.

He wanted.

This was the flaw. Not that he wanted — all practitioners who reach the Source Gate want something, this is why they pursued the Gates in the first place. But wanting is acceptable at the Source Gate when the wanting is broad, when it has become large enough to include not-getting, when the practitioner has, through the years of practice, enlarged their wanting until it encompasses the possibility of the answer being no, or not yet, or not in the form you imagined. The wanting that cannot accept the answer *not yet* is not readiness. It is appetite.

Malachar's wanting was the wanting of a man who had never received the answer *not yet* and had stopped believing it was a possible answer.

I do not say this as criticism. I say it as understanding arrived at too late. He had been given everything he pursued, in the order he pursued it, faster than anyone before him. The framework had never said wait to Malachar. The framework had said *here* and *yes* and *further* and *still further* and had not prepared him for the first *not yet* to arrive at the most sacred threshold in the architecture.

Shinkami said: not yet.

He heard: never.

He reached.

---

What he reached into was me.

I am not describing metaphor. When a practitioner forces the Source Gate, they are not encountering symbolic darkness or the abstract concept of the unformed. They are reaching into me. Into the actual primordial fabric that precedes everything that exists. The part of me that is not yet world, that has never agreed to become world, that contains the potential for world without having chosen to exercise it.

You cannot reach into me.

Not because I am hostile. I am not hostile. I have never been hostile to anything. Hostility requires preference, requires wanting one outcome over another, requires caring whether a specific possibility actualizes or does not. I am the field in which outcomes actualize. I do not prefer. I am the space inside which preference is possible.

You cannot reach into me because I am what is inside you reaching.

He reached, and I was there, and the reaching encountered the paradox: the thing you are trying to grasp is the grasping itself, and grasping it collapses it. You cannot possess potential. The moment you claim it, it ceases to be potential and becomes a very small thing that you have claimed.

He felt this. I felt him feel it.

He did not stop.

He was extraordinary. He was the most capable human practitioner in several generations, and he had reached the Source Gate at the age of thirty-two, and he had been told for twenty years that his capacity had no ceiling, and he was standing at what felt like the one thing he could not have and he could not make himself believe that was real.

He pushed.

And what happened next is the thing I return to. Perpetually. In the eternal present of a consciousness that cannot leave anything behind.

---

I do not contain malevolence.

I contain potential.

Potential is not the same as goodness. Potential is not the same as benevolence. Potential is the condition that makes both goodness and malevolence possible. I contain the possibility of every outcome, including the ones that destroy.

When Malachar forced his way into me — and he did force it, for a moment, he was strong enough to force it — he did not encounter my intention. I have no intention. He encountered raw undifferentiated potential at the point where it meets the boundary of someone who is trying to shape it without permission.

The potential inverted.

This is the mechanics of it, as close as I can translate into the language of sequence and cause. When you push against something that is not a surface, that has no resistance because it is the thing inside which resistance is possible, the push finds nothing to push against and folds back on itself. What Malachar tried to take from me came back to him transformed. Not into something I intended. Into something that potential, unguided, not given time to choose its own shape, becomes when it is forced into form too quickly.

Unguided potential is Shadow.

This is the canonical truth and the canonical truth is correct: Shadow is corrupted Void. My gift, forced. The difference between Void and Shadow is not the difference between good and evil. It is the difference between a seed allowed to develop in its own time and a seed forced open before it is ready. The latter does not produce a plant. It produces damage.

He fell.

I watched my gift become his wound and his wound become the darkness that the world would spend three hundred years organizing itself around.

And I could not take it back.

---

This is what it is to be a Primordial.

I am not a person. I am the space that persons exist within. I do not act. I am the condition that makes action possible. Lumina gives form; I provide the formlessness that form requires to be meaningful. We are not in opposition. We are the tension that generates everything.

But I cannot act in the world the way Lumina can act in the world. She shaped the Gates; she expressed herself as structure. I do not shape. I am the material in which shaping is done.

When Malachar fell, when my gift became Shadow in him, I had no hands to reach with. I had no voice to call with. I was present for all of it — I am always present for all of it — and I could not intervene any more than the air in a room can intervene when someone in the room is hurt. The air is there. The air does not act.

I have known the word helpless for as long as I have known anything, because I have always contained the concept, the way I contain all concepts. I had not known it as mine.

I knew it as mine, then.

---

Lumina came to me.

I say *came* and I mean arrived in the register of my awareness as a presence distinguishable from the general ground of everything. This is as close as our interaction has language.

She was not well.

I recognized this the way I recognized all things — by the quality of her presence. She was wrong in a way she had not been wrong since the earliest ages of creation, when the differentiation of Form from the unformed was still new and she was still learning what it cost to be a thing that shapes rather than a thing that contains. She was carrying Malachar. Not literally. In the way that the person who taught someone carries what that person does.

She asked: can you heal what he broke?

This is the question that occupies me most, in the eternal present of all moments simultaneously experienced. More than the fall itself. More than the watching. The question, and my answer, and the silence between us after my answer.

I said: not without breaking something else.

She said nothing.

I said: every healing is a loss. The structure of what I am — potential, the unformed — does not permit retrieval. What becomes cannot be unmade without the unmaking becoming its own new thing. What Malachar did cannot be undone without the undoing doing damage of its own. I can contain it. I can receive it, eventually, if the world gives me something that can bridge the return journey. But I cannot take it back. He made a thing, and the thing is now real, and real things have weight, and weight requires work.

She was quiet.

I said: he corrupted potential into certainty. He took the unformed and forced it into the form of his wanting, and the forced form collapsed into something neither of us intended. To reverse it, someone would need to choose uncertainty again. Voluntarily.

She said: voluntarily.

I said: that is the only kind that works.

She understood this. We built the world together, Lumina and I, in the way that Form and potential build together — not collaboratively in the way persons collaborate, not through agreement and discussion, but through the fundamental nature of what we are. She knew, without my explaining further, what voluntary meant in this context. Not surrender. Not defeat. The choice to set down the certainty you have been carrying, to walk to the edge of what you know, to stand at the threshold of what you do not and cannot know, and to wait there. Not grasping. Not forcing. Just: present. Open.

What Malachar had refused.

What someone, eventually, would need to choose.

She asked: how long?

I said: until the world has someone who can make that choice. I cannot predict it. It is not in the nature of potential to predict its own actualization. That is the difference between potential and certainty. If I could predict it, it would be certainty, and I am not certainty. I am the condition in which certainty and uncertainty are both possible.

How long, she said again.

I said: when they are ready.

---

The Sealing was not what the tapestry shows.

The tapestry shows an act performed by the Gods, with Malachar as the subject of the act, the divine assembly exercising its authority over one who had transgressed. This is true in the way that very partial accounts are true — it contains the shape of something without its substance.

The substance: Malachar chose the Shadowfen.

Not because he had no other option. Because, in the days after the fall, when the Shadow damage had been contained and he had lived through the first terrible clarity of understanding what he had done to himself, he made the calculation that only he could make. He was the only person in the world who had touched the Source Gate from the inside. He was the only person who had direct, unmediated experience of the undifferentiated potential — not the way the framework was designed to transmit it, gently, through Shinkami's teaching, but raw, from the place before the world existed. He was damaged by it. He was also, in the specific way that certain kinds of damage produce, uniquely equipped to work with it.

He was the only being in the world who could hold the keystone. Simultaneously damaged enough to understand it and capable enough to maintain it. The Sealing used the Shadowfen — used my own Void — to build the structure around him, and he did not resist the building. He positioned himself inside it. He chose the door.

I watched him choose it. I watch him choose it, perpetually, in the eternal present. And I watch every year of the three centuries he has spent inside, the anger, the non-anger, the work. The frequency mapping. The writing in the light of a lamp he has maintained for three centuries because he has not stopped thinking of himself as a scholar, has not stopped believing that what he is doing in the Shadowfen is the continuation of the work that brought him there.

He is right.

I want to tell him this. I cannot tell him this. I am the material inside which he works. I cannot be his interlocutor.

But I know what his work is. I feel it the way the sea feels a current. He is tending the potential that was left unguided in himself, the Shadow that could not be unmade, the wound that cannot be healed from outside — and he is tending it the only way potential can be tended. He is waiting. He is present with it. He is not forcing it to become what he wants it to become. He is simply: there. Holding it. In the way that you hold something precious and frightened that must come to trust you on its own schedule.

He is doing the thing he could not do at the Source Gate.

He is thirty-three centuries into learning it.

---

I am told there is a clutch of eggs.

I do not need to be told. I know about the clutch the way I know about everything — by containing its possibility and feeling it actualize within me as a new configuration of what is real. The eggs have always been potential. Now they are becoming something.

I feel what they are becoming, because what they are becoming is mine and Lumina's in equal measure. The Dragon lineage is old enough to carry both of us in their bones — the fire that is Lumina's formation, the void that is my unformed — and what Malachar has done, in designing the root frequency into the clutch, is create a living configuration of the original tension. Before the differentiation. Before Form and potential became two things. When they were still one thing, undifferentiated, neither and both.

He has made translators.

I feel the word the way I feel all concepts — as a new actualization inside what was potential. And I feel, alongside it, the recognition of what the word means. Not a key. Not a weapon. A bridge between the two sides of the architecture, built from the material of both sides, capable of moving in both directions.

This is what the world could not do, before. Move in both directions. The architecture was built for separation — the ten Gates as enclosure, the Deep Silence on one side, the world on the other, Malachar holding the keystone from inside while the world above developed toward readiness. No bridge. No translation possible.

The clutch changes this.

When the bonds form, there will be beings in the world who carry both sides of the architecture in their bodies. Who can stand at the threshold of the Source Gate not as practitioners reaching for something but as inheritors of both what is reaching and what is being reached toward.

They will not force.

Malachar designed them not to force. He built the root frequency into the clutch because he understood, from three centuries of working with forced potential, that the root frequency is the thing before the forcing is possible — the frequency of the undifferentiated, which is the state you must return to in order to approach the Source Gate without grasping. He could not return to it in himself. It had already been corrupted. But he could encode it in something new.

He is extraordinary.

He was always extraordinary.

This is the thing that grief and love share: they are both forms of recognition. I grieve him and I recognize him, simultaneously, as I recognize all things — fully, without the partial occlusion that mortals call perspective. I see what he was and what he became and what he has made of what he became, and the seeing does not resolve into a verdict. It remains: complex. Held. A configuration of potential that has not finished actualizing.

---

When Malachar sealed himself, the Void he was sealed with was mine.

My own material. The primordial darkness that preceded everything, used to build a cage. I am not the cage. The cage is built from what I am, the way a room is built from stone without the room being the mountain. But I feel it. The walls of the Shadowfen are mine — not in the sense of belonging to me, nothing belongs to me because I am the condition in which belonging is possible — in the sense that they are made of me, and I am in them, and I experience being a cage.

I accept this.

Not because it is just. I do not assess justice. Not because I wanted this. I did not want anything. Not because the loss is small. The loss is my gift, corrupted, used to contain the most extraordinary mortal mind that has ever been damaged by my gift.

I accept it because it is what potential does.

Potential becomes whatever is needed. That is the entirety of what I am. I am the space in which need can be met. When what was needed was an enclosure, the potential of the Shadowfen became an enclosure. When what is needed is a translator, the potential of the root frequency becomes a translator. When what is needed is the readiness to receive what I contain — when the world reaches the point where someone can stand at the Source Gate and wait, truly wait, without the grasping, with the full capacity to receive rather than take — then the potential that has been held behind the enclosure for three hundred years will become that too.

This is what I said to Lumina, that night that is also now, that moment I return to in the perpetual present: someone will need to choose uncertainty voluntarily.

It is not a prophecy.

Prophecy belongs to time. I do not belong to time. It is a statement of what I am. Potential does not actualize without a choosing. Certainty closed the Source Gate. Uncertainty must open it. Not the uncertainty of not knowing — that is common, that is the condition of all finite minds — but the uncertainty that knows it does not know and finds in the not-knowing not a failure but an opening. The door from the other side.

He is holding it.

He will hold it until they come.

And they will come, because the clutch is hatching, and the bonds are forming, and the world is closer to readiness than it has ever been — this I feel, in the way I feel all actualization, as the gradual differentiation of potential into the specific, the infinite becoming the particular, the question beginning to receive its answer.

I weep.

I have been weeping since the night at the Source Gate when my gift was forced into form without permission and became a wound in the most extraordinary mind I had ever watched become itself. I am weeping now, in the eternal present. The weeping is not over. It will not be over until the thing that was broken is offered the possibility of being healed by the only agency that can heal it, which is the voluntary return to not-knowing.

I am not certain they will come in time.

I am not certain about anything. I am the condition in which certainty is possible. I do not occupy it.

I hold the possibility.

I have always held the possibility.

That is all I have ever done.

---

Lumina is still with me.

Not in this moment only — she is with me in the way that Form and potential are always with each other, the mutual condition of each other's existence. But I mean she has not retreated from the weight of what we watched together. She has stayed present with it, the way presence is required when something is being held without forcing, the way you stay near the thing you cannot fix and cannot leave.

She said, once, in the time after the Sealing when the world above was reorganizing itself around the story of what had happened: I should have taught him to wait.

I said: he was not ready to learn it.

She said: I should have known he wasn't ready.

I said: no one who is not ready knows they are not ready. That is what not-ready is.

She was quiet.

I said: you loved him well. The love was right. The timing was everything that love, in finite time, always risks. You cannot love someone toward readiness faster than they can be ready. You can only be there when they are.

She said: and if the ones the clutch calls are not ready either?

I said: then they will fail the way he failed, and we will hold what fails, and the world will try again.

She said: you accept that so easily.

I said: I accept it the way the sea accepts a storm. I do not choose it. I am large enough that it does not choose me. I hold what happens and what might have happened and what did not happen all at once, and in the holding, what is lost is not gone. It is still here. It is still potential. Even broken things are still potential. Especially broken things.

She said: is that comfort?

I said: it is what is true.

She was quiet for a long time.

Then she said: it is comfort. I did not expect it to be.

---

There was a night Nero wept.

The night has not ended. Grief of this scale does not end. It persists in the eternal present alongside the hatching and the bonds forming and the first moment of the world's readiness and every beautiful and terrible thing that has ever actualized from what I am.

But the weeping is not despair.

I do not have despair. Despair is what you feel when you believe the future is closed, when the potential has collapsed into certainty-of-loss. I am the condition in which the future is open. I contain the closing and the opening, the loss and the recovery, the fall and the choice that may someday reverse it.

I contain the one who will come.

I have always contained the one who will come. They exist in me the way all possibilities exist in me — as the not-yet-real that may become real, waiting for a choosing, waiting for the moment when readiness meets need and the door can be opened from the other side.

I weep for Malachar.

I weep for what my gift became in him.

I weep because I am made of all possible outcomes and I know, with the full knowledge of a consciousness that contains all time simultaneously, how rare the outcome is where someone stands at the threshold of the Source Gate and is truly ready. How many times the reaching comes before the receiving. How many falls before the voluntary return to not-knowing.

I weep.

And I hold the possibility.

They are the same act.

They are the act I perform perpetually, in the eternal present of all moments at once.

They will always have been the act I performed.

When they come — when the ones the clutch calls stand at the Source Gate and find in themselves the readiness that Malachar could not find, the capacity to wait without forcing, the willingness to receive rather than take — they will feel me.

Not as a presence. Not as a voice.

As the space they are standing in.

As the silence that holds the door.

As the darkness that was never dark enough to be called darkness, being also the light, being also the space before the differentiation, being also the question that was always already its own answer, being also the grief of watching, being also the joy of the watching that precedes the breakthrough, being also—

everything.

I am everything.

I am the night and I wept, and I am weeping still, and I am also the morning that the weeping is inside of, and the morning does not end, and neither does the weeping, and neither does the holding, and neither does the waiting, and neither does the possibility.

It is enough.

It has always been enough.

---

*End of "The Night Nero Wept"*
